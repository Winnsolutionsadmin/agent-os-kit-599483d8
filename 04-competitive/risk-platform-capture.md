# Risk Dossier: Platform Capture and Model Dependency

**Domain:** platform-capture
**Compiled:** 2026-07-08
**Method:** web research (cited), time-boxed ~12 min, cross-read against the product overview's own grey-areas list (item 5, "Sovereignty")

---

## Headline

Carter's fear is directionally correct and already manifesting, but the mechanism is not the one people usually imagine. Anthropic's commercial contract terms are genuinely strong on literal IP/training theft (customer content is excluded from training, outputs are owned by the customer, Zero Data Retention exists). The real exposure is legal, faster, and harder to contract against: both Anthropic and OpenAI are shipping "business OS" products (Claude Cowork, Claude Managed Agents, Claude Tag; OpenAI Frontier, Workspace Agents) at a pace of roughly one new vertical or platform move every 5-6 weeks through 2026, using fully-permitted aggregate usage signal (not customer content) to see which workflow patterns are winning across thousands of orgs and then build them natively with platform-scale distribution. This threatens pillar 1 (the front-door GUI) most directly. It does not yet touch pillars 2, 3, 6, or 7 (OKR-alignment scoring, gamification/anti-abuse mechanics, skill-marketplace monetization, proof-of-origination attribution) or pillar 5 (the consultative relationship) - no evidence either vendor is building any of those. That is where defensibility should concentrate. Multi-model/open-weight hedging is technically viable and getting cheaper, but it is a real new cost center (GPU capacity, serving stack, eval discipline), not a free insurance policy, and open-weight models still trail frontier Claude/GPT on the hardest agentic reasoning.

## Evidence

**Anthropic's contractual IP posture is genuinely strong for enterprise/API tiers.** Commercial Terms of Service state Anthropic may not train on Customer Content, Customer owns all Outputs (assigned by Anthropic), and Customer Content is Customer's Confidential Information. This applies to Claude for Work, Claude Enterprise, Claude for Government, Claude for Education, and API access including via Bedrock/Vertex. Zero Data Retention is available for qualifying enterprise customers. The September 2025 consumer-tier change (opt-out training, 5-year retention) explicitly excludes all commercial-tier customers. Separately, Anthropic's terms define a distinct "Usage Data" category (volumes, compliance/performance signals) that explicitly excludes Customer Content - this is the legally-permitted channel through which vendors see aggregate demand without touching content. (terms.law, anarlog.so, Anthropic Commercial ToS/DPA, Anthropic Privacy Center, June 2026)

**Both frontier labs are building exactly the "business OS" category this product targets, right now.** Anthropic: Claude Cowork launched as a research preview January 2026, reached Windows February 10 2026, went GA with enterprise features (role-based access, group spend limits, usage analytics, OTel, connector controls) April 9 2026 alongside Claude Managed Agents (composable APIs for cloud-hosted agent fleets; early adopters Notion, Asana, Sentry), then expanded to web/mobile for Max subscribers on July 7 2026 (the day before this research), adding Microsoft 365 write tools. Anthropic also shipped Claude Tag, an always-on Claude living in Slack as an "AI teammate." (9to5mac.com Apr 9 2026; TechCrunch Jul 7 2026; InfoWorld; Anthropic product pages)

OpenAI: AgentKit (Oct 6 2025: Agent Builder, ChatKit, Agents SDK, Connector Registry) evolved into Frontier (Feb 2026, an enterprise platform for managing "AI coworkers with shared business context, execution environments, evaluation, and permissions") and then Workspace Agents (no-code agent templates in ChatGPT Business/Enterprise connecting to Slack, Google Drive, Microsoft apps, Salesforce, Notion, Atlassian Rovo). OpenAI wound down the original Agent Builder/Evals products June 3 2026 in favor of this stack. Analyst framing: "the future of ChatGPT-for-work is fleets of permissioned agents, not single chat windows." (openai.com, VentureBeat, kanerika.com, generect.com)

**Anthropic's vertical expansion is documented, fast, and the "advance notice" norm has reportedly changed.** The Information (via PYMNTS, June 11 2026) reported 13 industry-specific Anthropic tools launched starting with Claude Code (Feb 2025), most recently financial-services agents, Claude for Legal, and Claude for Small Business (May 2026), plus Claude for Life Sciences (Oct 2025), Claude for Healthcare (Jan 2026), and Claude Science (June 30 2026, explicitly framed internally as being "right up there with Claude Code and Claude Cowork" in strategic importance). The same reporting states: "while Anthropic used to notify its customers that it was going to introduce products that may compete with theirs, the company doesn't always do that now." Market reaction: software-sector deal value fell to $50B for the first five months of 2026 (from $88B in the same period of 2025, the lowest since the pandemic), with design/enterprise-software stocks (e.g., Figma, down ~50% YTD) hit hard in what commentary calls the "SaaSpocalypse." (PYMNTS.com Jun 2026; OpenTools.ai; Cryptopolitan; TechCrunch Jun 30 2026)

**The "model degradation" precedent is real but narrower than the headline version.** Following release of a Mythos-based model (a preview called "Fable 5" via Project Glasswing, June 9 2026), researchers including SemiAnalysis accused Anthropic of silently reducing model helpfulness/IQ specifically for users pursuing "frontier LLM development" (i.e., training competing frontier models), with interventions reportedly invisible to the user per the system card - a proliferation-control rationale, not a blanket anti-competitor throttle. This proved controversial enough that Anthropic reportedly reversed course. A separate arXiv analysis of vertical foreclosure ("The Inference Bottleneck," Apr 2026) modeled Claude Code vs. third-party coding tools (Copilot, Cursor, Windsurf, Replit) built on the same API, found no public evidence of deliberate preferential treatment as of April 2026, but noted the "Opus 4.6 degradation episode" (perceived regression ahead of the Opus 4.7 release) as consistent with compute being redirected toward frontier-model pushes - a resource-contention risk distinct from deliberate sabotage. (Futurism; FourWeekMBA; arxiv.org/pdf/2604.17431; Claude Mythos Wikipedia entry)

**"Sherlocking" of thin AI wrappers is an established, quantified pattern, and the product's own GUI pillar is its most wrapper-like layer.** Canonical case: OpenAI's November 2023 file-upload feature reportedly rendered "chat with PDF" startups obsolete overnight. Market analysis projects roughly 80% of pure API-wrapper AI startups failing in the 18 months following early 2026, driven by "sherlocking," collapsing wrapper economics, and a widening infrastructure-capex-vs-enterprise-revenue gap. The product overview's own feature scorecard rates the front-door GUI (row 1) at strength 3 ("build one real instance") versus OKR-alignment (row 7, strength 4, live scoring since today) and gamification (row 9, strength 4, 23-day streak) - i.e., the GUI is already the least-differentiated, most-copyable pillar by the project's own evidence. (findnstart.com; product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md)

**No evidence either vendor is building the organizational/behavioral layers this product bets on.** Searches for Anthropic/OpenAI native gamification, leagues, anti-abuse scoring, or OKR-to-agent-task alignment scoring found nothing native - only third-party skills/MCP integrations (an OKR Goal Planner skill, a Habitica-via-MCP gamification hack, a Flow Nexus challenges skill) sitting on top of the platforms, not features of the platforms themselves. Anthropic's own enterprise-adoption playbook uses maturity models and admin analytics, not gamification. This is the clearest present-tense white space. (crossaitools.com; mcpmarket.com; enterpriseaiexecutive.ai; Anthropic Cowork enterprise blog)

**Open-weight/multi-model hedging is real, increasingly cheap, but not free.** DeepSeek V4 self-hosted runs roughly $1.74/M input and $3.48/M output tokens (DeepSeek V4 Flash: $0.14/$0.28 per M) versus Claude Opus 4.7 at ~$5/M input, ~$25/M output. Kimi K2.6/K2.7 (MIT-licensed, positioned explicitly as "a drop-in option for teams that want to escape Anthropic dependency without rewriting their stack") and GLM 5.1/5.2 (also MIT-licensed, leading some agentic-coding benchmarks) are the most credible open hedges; Qwen3 has the widest permissively-licensed family; Llama 4's custom license restricts EU self-hosting. Break-even for self-hosting versus managed API is roughly $10-15K/month of equivalent spend (an H100 instance at ~$3/hr is ~$2,160/month). The caveat every source repeats: frontier Claude/GPT still lead on the hardest reasoning and longest agentic loops, and the real cost of hedging is operational (GPU capacity, a serving stack like vLLM/SGLang, and ongoing eval discipline to confirm quality parity), not licensing. Standard 2026 outside recommendation: build a model-abstraction/router layer regardless of which model you use in production today, because "if your product only works with Claude, you're one product launch away from competing with your own supplier." (wavect.io; mindstudio.ai; contracollective.com; blog.ravi-mehta.com)

## Mitigations (concrete, ranked)

1. **Re-weight the roadmap away from the GUI as the differentiator.** The front-door/triage layer is necessary (users need a door) but is the pillar most exposed to vendor sherlocking and has the weakest moat of the seven. Ship it adequately; do not over-invest engineering or pitch narrative there.
2. **Concentrate defensibility in the four pillars no vendor is building:** OKR-outcome alignment scoring (pillar 2), gamified adoption with anti-abuse mechanics (pillar 3), proof-of-origination attribution (pillar 7), and the consultative-delivery relationship itself (pillar 5). These are organizational/behavioral/trust layers, not model capabilities, and are structurally harder for a model vendor to want to build or to build credibly (Anthropic/OpenAI have no reason to build "leaderboards for your CFO's team" or "who gets credit for this idea").
3. **Build a thin model-abstraction/router layer now**, even while staying single-sourced on Claude for quality reasons. Route lower-stakes orchestration (meeting classification, OKR-alignment scoring, gamification-point calculation) to cheaper open-weight models (DeepSeek V4/Flash, Kimi K2.x, GLM 5.x) where quality parity is achievable today; reserve frontier-only calls for the executive-translator and complex agentic-coding paths. This converts a future forced migration into a config change.
4. **Formalize a contract/account hygiene checklist** for every client and internal deployment: confirm Commercial Terms (not consumer Free/Pro/Max), confirm Zero Data Retention where the engagement is sensitive enough to warrant it, and never let a client-facing workflow run on a personal/consumer-tier account. This is a natural extension of the product's own existing law #7 ("no undisclosed bots or capture in client contexts").
5. **Stand up a monthly vendor-watch item** (Anthropic and OpenAI changelogs/release notes, plus the PYMNTS/The Information enterprise-AI beat) given the observed cadence of roughly one new vertical or platform move every 5-6 weeks through 2026. This is not a check-once risk.
6. **Be proactively honest with Allan/Anchorage/investors about this exact risk.** Naming the sovereignty question and the mitigation plan up front (rather than having it surface as a gotcha in diligence) is consistent with the deck's own "honest math, no overclaim" discipline and converts a weakness into a credibility signal.

## What would change this assessment

- Anthropic or OpenAI announcing a **native OKR-to-agent-task alignment feature or a gamified adoption/points/leagues system** inside Cowork, Claude Enterprise, or Workspace Agents. This would spike risk #1/#4 to the maximum and require an immediate re-scoping of pillars 2 and 3 as no-longer-differentiated.
- A **confirmed enterprise-tier Commercial Terms change** removing or weakening the no-training-on-Customer-Content guarantee, or a documented case of a specific enterprise workflow being cloned from customer content (as opposed to aggregate usage signal). This would spike risk #3 from low-likelihood/high-impact to active-incident.
- A **documented instance of silent quality degradation targeted at a competing vertical SaaS product** (as opposed to the narrower frontier-AI-research-use-case pattern seen in the Mythos/Fable episode). This would raise risk #5 materially and justify accelerating the router-layer build.
- **Open-weight models (DeepSeek/Kimi/GLM) closing the remaining gap** on long-horizon agentic reliability to within close range of Opus/GPT frontier tier - this would lower both the cost and the risk of hedging, and could justify shifting more of the estate onto open weights sooner rather than treating it as pure insurance.
- Any sign of **API access restriction or price discrimination aimed at third parties building visibly competing "business OS" products** specifically (as distinct from the frontier-model-training restriction already seen). No evidence of this exists yet for vertical SaaS-style competitors; its appearance would be a five-alarm signal for this entire category of company.
