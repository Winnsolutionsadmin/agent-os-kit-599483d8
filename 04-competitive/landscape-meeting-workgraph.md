
# Competitive Landscape: Meeting Intelligence + OKR/Work-Graph (vs. our capture + OKR pillars)

**Compiled:** 2026-07-08. Category owner: meeting-workgraph swarm agent.
**Scope:** Gong, Otter, Fireflies, Read AI, Granola, Limitless, Fathom (meeting capture) x WorkBoard, Quantive, Perdoo, Betterworks, Asana/Monday AI (OKR/work-graph).
**Method:** live web research (WebSearch), 2025-2026 sources prioritized. Every claim below is sourced with URL + date where the source stated one. Speculation is explicitly flagged.
**Reference:** evaluated against `research/synthesis/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` (7 pillars: calm GUI front door, OKR-decomposed agent subtasks, gamified adoption, meeting-capture intelligence, consultative delivery moat, skill marketplace, proof-of-origination).

---

## Headline

The center of gravity in **both** categories moved from "capture/record" to "agent executes the follow-up" during 2026, faster than a casual read of these products in 2025 would suggest. Gong (revenue) and Otter (general enterprise) both now explicitly frame meetings as agent *triggers*, not just transcripts. WorkBoard absorbed Quantive (the #1 and #2 pure-play OKR vendors are now one company) and is shipping "AI Chief of Staff" agents that occupy almost exactly the persona we planned to enter through. Asana and Monday.com both repositioned in 2026 around agentic "operating system" language that is uncomfortably close to our own descriptor.

None of them, however, combine meeting capture + OKR/commit-level alignment scoring + gamified adoption + consulting-delivery moat + skill marketplace + attribution in one system. That full-stack combination remains genuine whitespace. The real threat is not one player cloning all seven pillars; it's (a) Gong/Otter closing the capture-to-action gap faster than we close our own, and (b) WorkBoard/Asana/Monday occupying the "OS" and "chief of staff" language and buyer persona we planned to use as our wedge, before we have a name or multi-tenant product.

## Per-player analysis

### Gong
Gong now calls itself the "Revenue AI Operating System," built on a "Revenue Graph" with specialized agents (AI Data Extractor, AI Deep Researcher, AI Tracker, AI Briefer, AI Tasker) plus user-buildable Custom Agents. The June 24, 2026 "Mission Big Dipper" launch introduced the "Revenue Harness," an agentic execution layer explicitly built to solve "generic tools that break in production because they lack revenue context, governance, and human-in-the-loop control", i.e., they are solving the exact same governed-autonomy problem our command-center's 90%/20-reps graduation rule targets, but shipped, not shelved. Agents run with scoped data access, audit trails, and configurable human oversight. ARR passed $500M with 55% YoY growth as of May 2026, and Gong is now a Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader for "Revenue Action Orchestration." Their AI Data Extractor auto-fills CRM fields "with no rep input required", meeting to executed-record-update with no human gate at all for low-risk data entry.
Why it doesn't fully threaten us: everything is sales/revenue-scoped. No OKR decomposition, no gamified adoption layer, no cross-department "whole org" ambition, no consulting-delivery-as-moat model, no skill marketplace, no attribution/origination layer. It is the best-executed proof that "meeting -> governed autonomous action" is now normal buyer expectation, which raises the bar our own exec-agent-gui has to clear, but it is not building our product.
Sources: [Gong Agents announcement](https://www.gong.io/blog/announcing-gong-agents-for-revenue-teams), [Gong AI innovations press release](https://www.gong.io/press/gong-unveils-new-ai-innovations-to-help-revenue-teams-drive-growth-at-scale), [Mission Big Dipper / Revenue Harness, PR Newswire, Jun 24 2026](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/gong-launches-mission-big-dipper-unveils-industry-first-revenue-harness-302808677.html), [Gong Revenue AI OS blog](https://www.gong.io/blog/new-product-announcements-gong-revenue-ai-operating-system), [Gong ARR $500M, May 2026](https://www.gong.io/press/gong-growth-accelerates-past-55-yoy-arr-tops-500m).

### Otter.ai
The April 28, 2026 "Conversational Knowledge Engine" launch is the single most direct signal in this category: Otter explicitly says conversations should be "the trigger for automated action across the enterprise," not just a transcript. It ships as both an MCP client (pulling Gmail/Drive/Notion/Salesforce into Otter AI Chat) and an MCP server (letting Claude or ChatGPT query meeting history for user-initiated tasks). A concrete example given: Otter auto-populates an Asana project with action items from a Zoom discussion. Otter for Desktop now captures any application's audio without a bot joining the call, addressing a "no visible presence" concern that maps to our own no-undisclosed-bots law. Scale: 35M+ users, 1B+ meetings, 30M+ action items generated, past $100M ARR.
Why it's the highest-threat pure meeting-capture player: broad (any department, not sales-only), cheap/accessible relative to Gong, and openly pursuing "knowledge graph + agentic workflow" positioning that overlaps our pillar 4 almost exactly. It still has no OKR/alignment scoring, no gamification, no consulting motion, and (as of this search) no attribution/origination concept.
Sources: [Otter Conversational Knowledge Engine announcement](https://otter.ai/blog/otter-ai-evolves-from-ai-notetaker-to-create-100b-enterprise-conversational-knowledge-engine-market), [Fast Company, "Otter wants AI agents to mine your meetings"](https://www.fastcompany.com/91532774/otter-wants-its-ai-to-unlock-information-from-all-your-business-meetings).

### Fireflies.ai
"AI Skills" let users build agents from meeting data (200+ templates: BANT, competitor mentions, candidate scorecards, weekly reports), runnable on-demand, post-meeting, or on a recurring schedule. "Fireflies Agents" push meeting data into 50+ apps (Salesforce, Slack, Asana). 2026 additions: "Live Assist" (real-time in-meeting coaching), an MCP server for Claude/ChatGPT/Devin, and a standalone desktop app. Scale: 20M+ users, 500k+ organizations, 75% of Fortune 500. One reviewer caveat is load-bearing: "workflow automations run on configured rules and an AI-credit system rather than open-ended autonomy", meaning Fireflies' execution is closer to templated automation than genuinely agentic judgment calls.
Threat is moderate: broad distribution and real automation, but the rules-based (not judgment-based) execution model, and total absence of OKR/gamification/consulting/attribution, keeps it well short of our concept.
Sources: [Fireflies AI Skills guide](https://guide.fireflies.ai/articles/6161594443-learn-about-ai-skills-create-automated-workflows-from-your-meetings), [Fireflies.ai homepage](https://fireflies.ai/).

### Read AI
Read AI's March 2026 "Ada" launch is notable: Ada is explicitly branded a "Digital Twin," handling scheduling, knowledge-base Q&A, and follow-ups by email (users literally email ada@read.ai). Read also ships a dedicated "Agentic Workflow Suite" of proactive, semi-autonomous agents (Monday Briefing, Recommendations, calendar optimization) plus "Sales AGI" for Salesforce/HubSpot deal automation, and an MCP Open Beta. This is a fuller agent stack than Fireflies, closer to Otter's ambition, still with no OKR-alignment or gamification layer and no consulting/services motion (Read is pure self-serve SaaS, free tier + $19.75/user Pro).
Sources: [Read AI March 2026 update](https://www.read.ai/post/read-it-whats-new-at-read-ai----march-2026), [Read AI Agentic Workflow Suite](https://www.read.ai/agents).

### Granola
Granola raised a $125M Series C (Index Ventures led, joined by Kleiner Perkins/Lightspeed/Spark/NFDG) at a $1.5B valuation on March 25, 2026, up from $250M in its prior round, a signal of serious capital behind a pivot from "notetaker" to "enterprise AI context layer." It shipped an MCP server (Feb 2026), personal + enterprise APIs, and team "Spaces," and connects into Claude, ChatGPT, Lovable, Figma Make, Replit, Manus, v0, Bolt.new. Per the company, genuinely agentic features that "use information in your notes to automate manual tasks" are in development and "expected to launch within a year" of the March 2026 report, i.e., Granola is explicitly behind Otter/Gong/Read on the execution axis today, but has runway (and now capital) to close the gap fast. Notably there was public friction (an a16z partner among those upset) when Granola locked down local on-device data that third-party AI-agent workflows had been quietly built on top of, which the co-founder had to walk back.
Threat today is moderate-low on execution, but the valuation and capital raise mean this could reprice upward within two quarters. Flag for re-check.
Sources: [TechCrunch, Granola $125M Series C, Mar 25 2026](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/25/granola-raises-125m-hits-1-5b-valuation-as-it-expands-from-meeting-notetaker-to-enterprise-ai-app/).

### Limitless (pendant)
Limitless was acquired by Meta on December 5, 2025; new pendants are no longer sold, and existing customers get free support/Unlimited-plan access through 2026 only. Meta is reportedly building a successor wearable running "Muse Spark" with an unreleased consumer agent codenamed "Hatch," targeting 10M wearable sales in H2 2026. This is a consumer hardware/personal-memory play now inside Meta, not an org-software competitor. (Note: a same-named but unrelated company, "Limitless Labs" / formerly LimitlessCNC, raised a $20M Series A in June 2026 for manufacturing CAM agents, unrelated to the pendant, flagged so it isn't conflated.)
Threat: low. Different buyer, different budget line, different product category. Worth a watch-flag only if Meta's "Hatch" agent gets bundled into a Workplace/enterprise Meta product later, speculative, not sourced for 2026.
Sources: [Engadget, Meta AI pendant reporting](https://www.engadget.com/2184224/meta-developing-ai-pendant-more-smart-glass-models/), [Limitless Labs $20M Series A](https://www.intelligence360.news/limitless-labs-raises-20m-series-a-to-expand-its-physical-ai-foundation-model-and-platform-for-precision-manufacturing/).

### Fathom
Fathom writes structured data directly into CRM fields (not just a notes block) on Salesforce/HubSpot, syncs to Slack/Notion/Asana, and ships an MCP/public API so users can "build custom agents and pipelines on top of Fathom's transcripts." "AI Scorecards" evaluate rep talk patterns (competing directly with a slice of Gong). 2026 also brought bot-free desktop capture (local audio capture, no bot joining the call) and a Premium price increase ($15 to $19/month). Fathom remains sales/customer-meeting scoped and is the most budget/free-tier-friendly of the group, which makes it a likely tool a client already has running (relevant for our consulting-delivery motion: expect to find Fathom already installed at target accounts).
Sources: [Fathom overview](https://www.fathom.ai/overview), [Fathom homepage](https://www.fathom.ai/).

### WorkBoard (+ Quantive)
The single most consequential structural move in the OKR category: WorkBoard acquired Quantive, announced May 28, 2025, explicitly framed as combining "the top two players in enterprise OKRs and strategy execution." Quantive customers are transitioning onto the WorkBoard platform. Since then, "WorkBoardAI" has shipped Generation-2 agents: an "AI Chief of Staff" (drives team OKRs/outcomes, runs managers' operating cadence) and an "AI Leadership Coach" (preps 1-on-1s and difficult conversations) for every leader in the org, a Strategy Analyst Agent, and (announced June 2026, available July 2026) a Portfolio Analyst Agent for chat-first Strategic Portfolio Management. The platform runs on a shared "Enterprise Knowledge Graph" the agents draw on, and integrates with Workday, Jira, Azure DevOps, Slack, Teams, plus a Microsoft 365 Copilot agent. Enterprise customer list: Boeing, Viasat, BAE Systems, 3M, Renault, Capital One, State Street, Cisco, Workday, Red Hat, UnitedHealth Group. Backers include SoftBank, a16z, Microsoft's M12, Workday Ventures.
This is our highest-threat OKR-category player for two specific reasons that are not about generic size: (1) WorkBoard's "AI Chief of Staff" agent literally occupies the buyer persona and language we planned to use as our GTM wedge ("sell the C-suite chief-of-staff seat first"); a prospect who already has a WorkBoard Chief-of-Staff agent has a harder time seeing why they need ours. (2) it is enterprise-proven, Microsoft/Workday-integrated infrastructure, meaning if it decided to bolt on meeting capture and gamified adoption it would be extending a beachhead, not starting from zero. What it still lacks versus us: no meeting-capture pipeline of its own, no gamification/points/leagues adoption layer, no consulting-delivery model (WorkBoard sells enterprise software + CS, not hands-on consulting engagements), no skill marketplace, no attribution layer, and its UI is a strategy-office dashboard, not a calm conversational front door for every employee.
Sources: [WorkBoard acquires Quantive, May 28 2025](https://www.workboard.com/news/workboard-acquires-quantive), [WorkBoard Portfolio Analyst Agent / SPM launch, Businesswire, Jun 29 2026](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260629227363/en/WorkBoard-Extends-its-Strategy-Execution-Platform-with-new-AI-Native-Strategic-Portfolio-Management-Solution-and-Portfolio-Analyst-Agents), [WorkBoardAI product page](https://www.workboard.com/product/).

### Perdoo
Perdoo's AI is assistant-level: drafting Strategic Pillars/KPIs/OKRs, an in-app coach ("Vince") that hands off to a human, an "AI Chat" that can read account data, and MCP connectivity to ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity. No autonomous execution agents were found. Competitor comparison pages (weight accordingly, they're not neutral) describe Perdoo as "closing gaps" and behind WorkBoard specifically on agents. Lowest threat in the OKR set.
Sources: [Perdoo release notes](https://support.perdoo.com/en/articles/10153623-release-notes-coming-soon), [Perdoo vs WorkBoard comparison, Mooncamp](https://mooncamp.com/blog/perdoo-vs-workboard).

### Betterworks
Repositioned in 2026 around "AI-native" performance + "Talent Intelligence," and acquired Rypple for "AI-powered manager enablement" (2026, exact date not found in this search). AI features (Goal Assist, Feedback Assist, Conversation Assist, Feedback Summary) are explicitly co-pilot/assist-framed rather than autonomous-agent-framed; a comparison piece notes "AI agents" as a term appears more in WorkBoard's marketing than Betterworks'. Its own 2026 research (State of Performance Enablement Report, n=2,387) found 92% of executives are comfortable using AI at work vs. 51% of employees, a data point worth stealing for our own adoption-gap argument, since it independently validates the "reward, don't just deploy" thesis behind our gamification pillar.
Sources: [Betterworks homepage](https://www.betterworks.com/), [Betterworks review, PeopleManagingPeople](https://peoplemanagingpeople.com/tools/betterworks-review/).

### Asana (AI Studio / AI Teammates)
Asana's language is the closest textual overlap with our own positioning found in this whole sweep: reporting describes Asana's strategy as building "the operating system for human-agent teams" (Fortune, May 29, 2026), following its $75M acquisition of no-code agent-builder StackAI (TechCrunch, May 28, 2026). "AI Teammates" ship as ~30 prebuilt agents (Campaign Brief Writer, Workflow Optimizer, Compliance Specialist, etc.) that "analyze project context, collaborate with teams, and help complete tasks directly within shared projects," distinct from chat-only bots. AI Studio is a no-code agentic-workflow builder (Basic/Plus/Pro tiers). AI products now reportedly drive over 17% of new ARR, with $100k+ AI Studio accounts nearly doubling in a quarter. Fortune's framing ("Asana was battered by the AI age... betting its future on humans and agents working together") is itself a tell: Asana is repositioning defensively against exactly the kind of agent-native org software we're building, from a task-management incumbent's position of AI-cycle weakness.
No OKR-scoring engine, no gamification-for-adoption mechanic, no meeting-capture pipeline, no consulting-delivery model, no attribution layer found. But the brand/capital/distribution and the direct "OS for human-agent teams" language make this a name-collision risk worth tracking even before it's a capability collision.
Sources: [Asana AI Teammates](https://asana.com/product/ai/ai-teammates), [TechCrunch, Asana acquires StackAI, May 28 2026](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/28/asana-acquires-no-code-agent-builder-stack-ai/), [Fortune, May 29 2026](https://fortune.com/2026/05/29/asana-was-battered-by-the-ai-age-now-its-betting-its-future-on-humans-and-agents-working-together/).

### Monday.com (AI Work Platform)
Monday's May 2026 repositioning from "Work Management Platform" to "AI Work Platform" is described by the company as "the largest strategic shift in the company's history." Four capability pillars: monday sidekick, monday vibe, monday agents, monday workflows, plus one-click Claude/ChatGPT/Copilot connectors. "Call My Agent" lets any workflow automation trigger an agent mid-flow; "monday magic" generates a working solution from a prompt; governance includes per-agent action logs, scoped data access, and simulation-mode validation before agents act live, again, directly analogous to the graduated-autonomy problem our command-center's 90%/20-reps rule targets. Credit-based consumption pricing (new accounts since May 6, 2026) meters agent usage. Monday.com's own marketing calls it "the AI Workspace for People & Agents."
Same read as Asana: strong brand/capital, aggressive and fast agent rollout, "OS"-adjacent language, real governance thinking, but zero OKR-scoring engine, zero gamification-for-adoption, zero meeting-capture pipeline of its own, zero consulting/services delivery model, zero attribution layer.
Sources: [monday.com homepage](https://monday.com/), [monday.com 2026-2027 roadmap summary](https://www.fruitionservices.io/post/mondaycom-product-and-ai-roadmap-2026-2027/).

## What nobody does / whitespace

1. **Gamified adoption is absent industry-wide.** Every player above measures or reports AI/agent adoption (Gong touts "monthly active users of AI agents up 75%"; Betterworks' own survey shows a 92%-vs-51% exec/employee adoption gap) but none of them **reward** individual employees with points, tiers, streaks, or leagues for using the agents. Adoption is tracked as a vanity/success metric for the vendor, never gamified back to the end user. This remains our clearest, least-contested differentiator.
2. **"Reward, not replace" framing is absent.** All twelve players frame agents as doing work a human used to do (CRM entry, follow-up drafting, scorecard prep), efficiency/replacement logic. None frame agent output as something that earns the employee comp, time-back, or recognition. This is a messaging and mechanic gap our pillar 3 fills directly.
3. **Nobody scores the unit of work itself against strategy.** WorkBoard's alignment lives at the OKR/initiative/dashboard layer (top-down, admin-authored). Nobody found here scores an individual commit, ticket, or artifact against the OKR tree the way the-sage's `score_alignment()` does (99.3% on 277 commits). This is a real, demonstrated technical asset none of the OKR incumbents have shown evidence of.
4. **Nobody spans the whole org from one conversational surface.** Gong is revenue-only. WorkBoard is the strategy office's tool. Asana/Monday are project/task-centric (broadening, but still task-graph DNA, not "ask a question, get a decision card" DNA). Our calm two-pane front door aimed at every employee from CEO to line worker, with Approve/Edit/Skip triage as the universal interaction, has no direct analog in this set.
5. **Consulting-as-moat is absent.** All twelve are pure (or overwhelmingly) self-serve SaaS with enterprise CS at most. None sell hands-on, out-of-the-box-consultation-per-business as explicit product. Our Anchorage-pattern engagement scaffold is structurally different from how any of these land.
6. **No pay-per-skill-run marketplace.** Asana has templates; Monday has an apps marketplace (install-based, not metered); nobody found here monetizes individual skill/agent runs at the marketplace level.
7. **No attribution/proof-of-origination layer anywhere.** Zero evidence across all twelve that any of them track who originated an idea or authorized an autonomous action as a trust or compensation primitive. This remains a genuinely open field, not just relative to us but industry-wide.
8. **Convergence of meeting capture and OKR/work-graph is still largely unbuilt.** A direct search for platforms combining meeting intelligence with OKR alignment tracking came back empty of named products; the market commentary only gestures at "strategic planning and execution" value from meeting data in the abstract. If any single player closes pillars 2 and 4 together first (most plausible candidate: Otter, given its "any conversation triggers agentic action" ambition and MCP breadth; or WorkBoard, given its Enterprise Knowledge Graph, if it added ingestion), that combination becomes the single biggest structural threat to our two named pillars. Neither has done it yet as of this research pass.

**Aggregate read for the two named pillars:**
- **OKR pillar (pillar 2):** WorkBoard+Quantive is the one player that could credibly claim "we already do OKR-to-agent execution" to a prospect today, and it has annexed language ("Chief of Staff agent") close to our own GTM wedge. This is the single most important competitive fact from this sweep for the 7/15 concept doc and the Allan pitch: **assume any sophisticated buyer has seen or will see a WorkBoard-style pitch**, and be ready to differentiate on (a) commit/artifact-level alignment scoring rather than dashboard-level, (b) gamified adoption, (c) the conversational front door, and (d) the consulting-delivery motion.
- **Meeting-capture pillar (pillar 4):** Otter and Gong have both crossed decisively into "meeting triggers agent action," which narrows our capture-side differentiation to execution quality, governance design (the 90%/20-reps rule is still a genuinely distinctive artifact versus what's public from Gong/Monday's governance approaches, which are ruleset/simulation-based rather than proven-agreement-rate-based), and the fact that we fuse capture into OKR scoring and gamified reward rather than leaving it as a standalone intelligence product.
- **No single player threatens the full seven-pillar stack.** The compounding combination (front door + OKR + gamification + capture + consulting + marketplace + attribution) is still unclaimed. The risk is speed and naming, not architecture: several of these companies (Asana, Monday, WorkBoard, Gong) have far more capital, distribution, and enterprise trust today than we do, and could each bolt on one more of our pillars faster than we can ship all seven.
