# Competitive Landscape: Agent-Platform and Agent-OS Startups (as of 2026-07-08)

**Category:** agent-startups
**Method:** live web research (WebSearch), 2026-07-08. Time-boxed to ~12 min of research.
**Scope:** Sierra, Lindy, Dust, Relevance AI, CrewAI, LangChain/LangGraph, Cognition (Devin), Factory, plus the YC/a16z "company OS" / "AI chief of staff" cohort, plus context players (Glean, Meta internal, meeting-capture incumbents) surfaced during research because they bear directly on threat assessment.

---

## Headline

2026 has been the best-funded year in agent-platform history (Sierra $950M, Cognition $1B, Factory $150M, LangChain $125M, Dust $40M, all closed between April and May 2026), which proves the category is real and capital-rich, not that our concept is safe. But every well-funded player is a **horizontal point-tool or a vertical-point-solution**, not the specific bundle in the product concept overview. Nobody found in this research combines: (1) a calm org-wide front door with Approve/Edit/Skip triage into live agent sessions, (2) OKR trees decomposed into weighted, scored agent subtasks, (3) gamified adoption with anti-abuse mechanics sold as a product, (4) owned meeting capture feeding that same OKR/CRM/memory stack, (5) a consulting-plus-equity delivery model, (6) a skill marketplace, and (7) origination attribution. The two closest conceptual collisions are **Dust** (explicitly branded "multiplayer operating system for enterprise AI") and **Bond** (YC AI chief-of-staff, same C-suite entry wedge we picked). The single biggest incumbent risk to Pillar 1 specifically is **Glean** ($7.2B valuation, $300M ARR, already the persistent enterprise front door wired into every SaaS tool). The single biggest validating-but-alarming signal for Pillar 3 is that **Meta already ships an internal gamified AI-adoption program** (Level Up) tied to performance reviews, proving the mechanic works at hyperscaler scale but also showing large incumbents can DIY it without buying anything. Sierra's own product is literally named "Agent OS," which is a direct branding collision worth knowing about even though its scope (external customer-service agents) is different from ours (internal, whole-org).

## Per-player analysis

**Sierra.** Bret Taylor and Clay Bavor's company closed $950M at a $15.8B valuation on 2026-05-04 (Tiger Global and Google's GV leading, Benchmark/Sequoia/Greenoaks participating), taking total raised past $1.5B ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/04/sierra-raises-950m-as-the-race-to-own-enterprise-ai-gets-serious/), [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/04/bret-taylor-sierra-fundraise-openai.html)). Sierra now reports roughly $200M ARR and over 40% of the Fortune 50 as customers, with ~700 employees ([Latka](https://getlatka.com/companies/sierra)). Critically, Sierra's own product is branded **"Agent OS"** and its 2026 releases (Agent Studio 2.0, Ghostwriter conversational agent-builder, Workspaces for cross-team collaboration, Agent Data Platform for memory/context) are explicitly building toward a persistent, multi-surface operating layer ([Sierra blog](https://sierra.ai/blog/agent-os-2-0)). But Sierra is built and sold for **external, customer-facing** interactions (chat, phone, SMS, email, ChatGPT plugin), priced per resolved case. It has no OKR engine, no gamification, no meeting-capture ownership, and sells through enterprise AE motion, not consulting-plus-equity. The threat is brand collision on "Agent OS" plus platform gravity: "Workspaces" already nudges toward internal, cross-functional use, and a company with $1B+ in the bank can build an OKR or gamification module in a quarter if it decides the internal-facing market is worth entering.

**Dust.** Closed a $40M Series B on 2026-05-18 (Sequoia and Abstract leading, Snowflake Ventures and Datadog participating), taking total funding past $60M ([Yahoo Finance](https://finance.yahoo.com/sectors/technology/articles/dust-raises-40m-ai-multiplayer-133000633.html), [Tech.eu](https://tech.eu/2026/05/18/dust-raises-40m-series-b-to-build-the-multiplayer-operating-system-for-enterprise-ai/)). Dust explicitly frames itself as the **"multiplayer" operating system for enterprise AI**, contrasted against single-player assistants, serving 3,000+ organizations with 300,000+ agents deployed, 41,000 monthly active users, and 240% net revenue retention in 2025 ([SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2026/05/18/multiplayer-ai-startup-dust-swipes-40m-funding-help-enterprises-move-beyond-isolated-ai-assistants/)). This is the closest positioning collision with our Pillar 1 thesis (one calm, shared surface where humans and agents collaborate org-wide). It has no OKR-decomposition engine, no gamification, no owned meeting-capture pipeline, no consulting-plus-equity delivery, and no marketplace or attribution layer disclosed, but its funding, growth rate, and "operating system" framing make it the pure-play startup most likely to organically grow toward our shape.

**Lindy.** Founded by Flo Crivello (ex-Uber PM), Lindy has raised roughly $50M total, with its largest disclosed round a $35M Series B (no new 2026 round surfaced in this search) ([Tracxn](https://tracxn.com/d/companies/lindy/__FJe0QVe6UcRHtdiPJpmyRG3livSd4eIGsIxMxz-kNPI)). It is a no-code builder for individual "Lindies" (email triage, scheduling, sales, support) with 5,000+ integrations and a Computer Use mode. This overlaps only lightly with Pillar 1 (a build-it-now conversational builder) and the workflow-template end of Pillar 6. No OKR engine, no gamification, no meeting-capture, no consultative delivery, no attribution. Individual/SMB-productivity framing, not org-wide OS framing.

**Relevance AI.** Sydney/SF company building an "AI workforce" platform; raised $24M Series B in May 2025 (Bessemer leading), total ~$37M, now 124 employees (up from 80 in 2025) with enterprise logos including Canva, Databricks, Confluent, Autodesk, KPMG ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/05/06/relevance-ai-raises-24m-series-b-to-help-anyone-build-teams-of-ai-agents/), [Tracxn](https://tracxn.com/d/companies/relevance-ai/__GcnNqHYzkszu5Cqnv3GUOXxUAPJ0AGlKnzFyOXHCE70)). No 2026 round found. Overlaps only with Pillar 1 (agent-team builder UI). Nothing on OKR, gamification, capture, consulting-equity delivery, or attribution.

**CrewAI.** Open-source multi-agent orchestration framework (role-based "crews"), $18M Series A in October 2024 (Insight Partners), no new round found for 2026 ([Insight Partners](https://www.insightpartners.com/ideas/crewai-scaleup-ai-story/)). Distribution is enormous for a dev tool: 47.8k GitHub stars, 27M+ PyPI downloads, ~2B executions in the trailing 12 months, and 60% of Fortune 500 reportedly using it somewhere ([getpanto.ai](https://www.getpanto.ai/blog/crewai-platform-statistics)). This is developer infrastructure, not an end-user product; the real risk is that CrewAI (or LangGraph) becomes the substrate a fast-moving competitor uses to clone our front door and OKR-subtask engine in weeks rather than build it themselves.

**LangChain / LangGraph.** Raised $125M Series B at a $1.25B valuation on 2025-10-20 (IVP leading; ServiceNow, Workday, Cisco, Datadog, Databricks all invested strategically) ([LangChain blog](https://www.langchain.com/blog/series-b), [SiliconANGLE](https://siliconangle.com/2025/10/20/ai-agent-tooling-provider-langchain-raises-125m-1-25b-valuation/)). As of mid-2026 it claims 100M+ monthly downloads, 139k+ GitHub stars, and 6,000+ active customers on LangSmith, and at its 2026 Interrupt conference shipped a no-code Agent Builder and an "Insights Agent," both nudging toward the front-door and observability layers ([Sacra](https://sacra.com/c/langchain/)). Same category as CrewAI: developer/infra platform with a huge enterprise customer base it could cross-sell an OKR or gamification layer into, but currently has none of our 7 pillars natively.

**Cognition (Devin).** Raised over $1B at a $26B valuation on 2026-05-27 (Lux Capital, General Catalyst, 8VC co-leading; Founders Fund participating), total funding past $2.5B ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/27/ai-coding-startup-cognition-raises-1b-at-25b-pre-money-valuation/)). ARR run-rate hit $492M in May 2026, up from $37M a year earlier, and 89% of Cognition's own code is now committed by Devin itself ([TechFundingNews](https://techfundingnews.com/the-ai-startup-replacing-software-engineers-just-raised-1b-at-26b-valuation-and-it-is-already-writing-89-of-cognitions-own-code/)). This is a software-engineering agent (plus the acquired Windsurf IDE), not an org-wide OS. Its relevance to us is narrow but real: it is already delivering exactly the "engineering department" rung of our GTM ladder (weighted, agent-run subtasks, just scoped only to code) to blue-chip accounts (Citi, Goldman Sachs, US Army/Navy), which means engineering leaders we plan to sell into may already have an agent-run subtask system before we show up.

**Factory (Droids).** Raised $150M Series C at a $1.5B valuation on 2026-04-16 (Khosla Ventures leading, Keith Rabois joining the board) ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/16/factory-hits-1-5b-valuation-to-build-ai-coding-for-enterprises/)). Revenue reportedly doubled month-over-month for six consecutive months, with customers including NVIDIA, Adobe, EY, Morgan Stanley ([Factory](https://factory.ai/news/series-c)). In June 2026 Factory explicitly rebranded its ambition as "Factory 2.0," describing the next phase as "an interconnected, agent-native, end-to-end system" rather than a point tool ([Factory](https://factory.ai/news/software-factory)). That is OS-scale language aimed squarely at the same engineering-department wedge in our GTM ladder, making Factory the closer near-term threat to that specific rung than Cognition.

**Bond ("Donna").** YC X25 (Spring 2025) company, founders Chloe Samaha and Rene Sultan, backed by YC, Fellows Fund, Goodwater Capital, E14, and CoreNest ([YC](https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/bond), [Crunchbase](https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/bond-37a1)). Bond is explicitly an "AI Chief of Staff": it plugs into Slack, Jira, Notion, GitHub, Salesforce, surfaces blockers/risks/wins, and delivers a "Presidential Brief" morning summary plus an "Ask Donna Anything" chat interface, stating its mission as "collapse management layers and help companies ship 1000x faster" ([Fondo](https://fondo.com/blog/bond-launches), [HireTop](https://hiretop.com/blog4/ai-chief-of-staff-for-ceos-bond-donna/)). This is the single closest wedge collision in the whole set: same buyer (CEO / chief-of-staff seat), same "one window" thesis, similar decision-surfacing framing. The critical gap: Bond is a **read-only visibility and reporting layer**. It does not decompose OKRs into weighted agent-run subtasks, does not dispatch or triage live agent execution with Approve/Edit/Skip, has no gamification, no owned meeting-capture, no consulting delivery, and no marketplace or attribution. It validates the wedge is real and fundable at seed stage; it has not yet built the execution layer that is the substance of our concept.

**Orchid.** YC-listed executive assistant living in iMessage: drafts replies, books meetings, "you approve, it handles the rest" ([YC](https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/industry/ai-assistant)). This directly patterns-matches our "familiar UX, text-an-agent like iMessage" law and our own imessage.html demo concept, but is scoped to a single person's personal admin, not org-wide OKR-aligned agent work.

**Clarm, Flott, OrgOrg.** All YC-listed, all validate that "OS for X" is the current default framing YC founders reach for in 2026: Clarm pitches itself as the GTM half of "Cursor to build, Clarm to sell," Flott is an explicit "company OS" for logistics, and OrgOrg is a Workspace-extension productivity suite from the ex-Zenter/Google Slides founder ([YC industry page](https://www.ycombinator.com/companies/industry/ai-assistant)). None overlap meaningfully with our specific bundle, but Flott in particular is a useful proof point: vertical "company OS" products are already being built one industry at a time, which is exactly the fragmentation risk our horizontal, whole-org positioning is betting against.

**Glean (added context).** Not in the original list but too material to omit from a Pillar-1 threat assessment. Glean raised $150M Series F at a $7.2B valuation on 2025-06-10 (Wellington Management leading) and by May 2026 reported $300M ARR, up from $208M at the end of 2025 ([Glean](https://www.glean.com/blog/glean-series-f-announcement), [Crunchbase News](https://news.crunchbase.com/venture/ai-powered-work-assistant-glean-valuation-jumps/), [ValueAdd VC](https://valueaddvc.com/blog/glean-valuation-revenue-2026-300m-arr-enterprise-ai-search)). Glean already indexes every SaaS tool a company uses into a permissions-aware knowledge graph and sells a persistent assistant used roughly six times per day per active user, plus an agent-building platform now driving 100M+ agent actions annually ([Futurum](https://futurumgroup.com/insights/glean-doubles-arr-to-200m-can-its-knowledge-graph-beat-copilot/)). Glean has no OKR-decomposition engine, no gamification, no meeting-capture ownership, and sells self-serve/enterprise-AE, not consulting-plus-equity. But of every player surveyed, Glean has the most enterprise trust, data access, and distribution already in place to bolt an OKR/gamification layer onto its existing front door faster than most challengers could build the front door from scratch.

**Meta "Level Up" (internal program, not a vendor).** Meta began tying employee performance reviews to AI usage across all roles starting with the 2026 review cycle, communicated internally by Head of People Janelle Gale, and built a gamified internal tool called "Level Up" that awards badges for AI milestones ahead of formal scoring ([Winbuzzer](https://winbuzzer.com/2026/02/04/meta-ties-employee-performance-reviews-ai-usage-2026-xcxwbn/), [HR Grapevine](https://www.hrgrapevine.com/us/content/article/2025-11-17-meta-to-formally-review-employees-ai-performance-from-2026)). This is not a competitor (it is not for sale), but it is the single strongest piece of evidence in this research that our Pillar 3 thesis (gamify adoption, reward don't replace) is directionally correct and already proven at hyperscaler scale. It cuts both ways: a prospective client's first reaction to our gamification pitch may be "why don't we just build our own Level Up," and Gartner-cited skepticism (only 1 in 5 AI investments shows measurable ROI) plus a Workday finding that 37% of "time saved" by AI is lost to rework are exactly the failure modes our anti-abuse/token-burn-vs-output design is meant to prevent, meaning we should use this data point in the pitch as a cautionary tale we are architecturally built to avoid.

**Meeting-capture cohort (Fathom, Fireflies, Otter, Granola, Read.ai, Recall.ai).** A 2026 survey tracked 21+ meeting-intelligence tools now competing on capture mode (bot vs bot-free), CRM depth, and vertical specialization ([Avoma](https://www.avoma.com/blog/fathom-vs-fireflies)). This confirms Pillar 4 alone is a commodity, crowded layer; Fireflies already pushes CRM auto-population and Fathom gates CRM sync behind a paid tier, meaning the defensible move is what we do downstream of capture (feed OKR scoring, gamification points, attribution) rather than owning capture as the moat.

**Skill marketplace cohort (Agensi, MCP-Hive, x402, Circle, OKX).** The agent-skill-marketplace category grew from one registry in December 2025 to eight by Q2 2026, and agent-to-agent payment rails (x402 on Ethereum/Base, Circle's May 2026 marketplace, OKX's agent-hiring marketplace) are being built out fast, with 165M+ transactions already on x402 ([Agensi](https://www.agensi.io/learn/skills-marketplace-ai-agents), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/crypto-exchange-okx-wants-ai-agents-to-hire-and-pay-each-other/)). All of these are developer/agent-tool marketplaces or agent-to-agent commerce rails; none are an **org-internal** marketplace where a single company's own proprietary skills become a metered, monetizable asset the way Pillar 6 envisions. This gap is still open, though the infrastructure (payment rails, standards like SKILL.md) to build it fast now exists off-the-shelf.

## What nobody does / whitespace

- **OKR-to-weighted-agent-subtask decomposition with alignment scoring (Pillar 2): confirmed absent everywhere searched.** Traditional OKR software (Workboard, Weekdone, Tability, Profit.co) tracks human-reported progress; none of the agent platforms (Sierra, Dust, Lindy, Relevance, CrewAI, LangChain) or the chief-of-staff startups (Bond) score actual agent-executed work against a weighted objective tree. This is the most defensible, least-contested pillar in the whole set as of this research.
- **Gamified adoption sold as a product, with anti-abuse mechanics, for AI-agent usage specifically (Pillar 3): confirmed absent as a startup product.** The only real-world implementation found is Meta's internal-only Level Up program. Generic enterprise gamification vendors (Centrical, $45M funded; Docebo) gamify training and performance broadly, not AI-agent token-burn-vs-output with anti-gaming caps. Nobody is selling this as SaaS today.
- **Consulting-plus-equity delivery bundled with a proprietary product (Pillar 5): confirmed absent among the pure-play platforms.** Sierra, Dust, Relevance, CrewAI, LangChain all sell self-serve or enterprise-AE motion for cash only. The closest analogs are the big consultancies (Accenture, Deloitte, BCG) doing AI transformation work, but they integrate other vendors' products rather than owning their own; conversely, none of the product companies take equity. The combination is currently unclaimed.
- **Single front door that both triages incoming decisions (Approve/Edit/Skip) AND dispatches/resumes live agent sessions, across the WHOLE org rather than one function (Pillar 1): partially claimed in pieces, never combined.** Bond does read-only surfacing without execution dispatch. Dust does collaborative multiplayer execution without OKR-weighted triage. Glean does search/assist at massive scale without OKR or gamification framing. Orchid does approve-based execution for one person's personal admin, not org-wide work.
- **Proof-of-origination attribution (Pillar 7): confirmed completely absent across every player surveyed,** including the agent-payment/marketplace cohort (x402, OKX, Circle), which is building agent-to-agent payment and reputation rails but not human-idea-origination tracking inside an org. This remains the single most wide-open long-horizon pillar in the market.
- **Org-internal skill marketplace with per-run monetization of a company's own proprietary workflows (Pillar 6): the infrastructure now exists (SKILL.md standard, 8 marketplaces, agent payment rails) but nobody applies it inside a single org's boundary** the way "pay cents every time a skill runs" was framed on the founders' call; every marketplace found is either developer-facing (Agensi, MCP-Hive) or a public agent-to-agent commerce rail (x402, OKX, Circle), not a private, per-company economy.

**Overall reading:** the market is capital-rich and moving fast (five 2026 mega-rounds in six weeks), but structurally fragmented. Every well-funded player has picked one surface (customer-facing agents, developer infra, coding agents, enterprise search) and gone deep, not wide. The specific 7-pillar bundle in the product concept overview, especially the OKR-decomposition engine (Pillar 2), the sellable gamification product (Pillar 3), the consulting-plus-equity delivery model (Pillar 5), and attribution (Pillar 7), remains uncontested by any named or discovered player as of 2026-07-08. The two fastest-moving risks are (a) a well-capitalized horizontal platform (Dust or Glean) deciding to bolt on an OKR/gamification layer using its existing distribution, and (b) a YC-speed chief-of-staff startup (Bond) adding an execution/triage layer on top of the visibility layer it already has.
