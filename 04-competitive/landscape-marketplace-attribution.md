# Competitive Landscape: Skill/Agent Marketplaces + Provenance/Attribution (Pillars 6 and 7)

**Compiled:** 2026-07-08. Category: marketplace-attribution. Method: live web research (WebSearch), time-boxed ~12 min. All claims sourced with URL, dated where the source dates itself. Speculation is flagged explicitly.
**Scope:** Tests two of our seven pillars against the live market: Pillar 6 (skill marketplace, pay-per-skill-run) and Pillar 7 (proof-of-origination attribution). Threat ratings are against OUR specific concept (gamified, OKR-wired, chief-of-staff-first agent OS), not generic company size.

---

## Headline

Skill/agent marketplaces are real, funded, and consolidating fast (8 marketplaces in Q2 2026, expected to shrink to 3-4 within a year), but almost none of them meter true "pay-per-run" cents at the product layer yet, most still pay creators through opaque engagement pools (OpenAI) or flat rev-share (Vercel skills.sh, Agensi), and only Salesforce and ClickUp have shipped real per-action consumption pricing at enterprise scale. Proof-of-origination for workplace ideas/work-product, our Pillar 7, is confirmed whitespace: nobody found in this sweep is building "who originated this idea, who authorized this agent run, tied to a gamified comp/points system" as a named horizontal product. The closest analogs are all vertical and partial: Cursor's Agent Trace (code-only, open spec, fast multi-vendor adoption), Sureel AI (music/media, just acquired by Warner), C2PA (media authenticity), World ID (proof-of-personhood, pivoting hard into enterprise via Zoom/DocuSign/Okta), and conventional idea-management software (ROI dashboards, not cryptographic attribution, not gamified). The bigger near-term threat to our concept is not a attribution startup beating us to Pillar 7; it is the big platforms (Anthropic, Salesforce, Microsoft, ClickUp) shipping Pillar 6's billing/attribution layer as a feature of their own marketplace before we build ours, which would commoditize the "marketplace as network effect" thesis from underneath.

## Per-player analysis

### OpenAI GPT Store
Revenue sharing is engagement-based and opaque: payouts reportedly average roughly $0.03 per conversation, most creators need 25+ conversations/week to qualify at all, and independent community reports put median quarterly earnings under $100 for qualifying creators. Practitioner consensus by mid-2026 is that serious builders have moved to direct B2B consulting ($5-20k setup + maintenance) rather than relying on store payouts. This is a consumer-scale marketplace with a massive user base but no real per-run cents economics and nothing resembling org-level attribution or OKR-tied scoring.
Source: [OpenAI Developer Community](https://community.openai.com/t/what-is-the-status-with-gpt-store-revenue-share/839172), [thegptshop.online blog](https://www.thegptshop.online/blog/openai-gpt-store-revenue-sharing), [digitalapplied.com 2026 guide](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/gpt-store-custom-gpts-business-guide-2026).

### Anthropic Claude Skills / MCP ecosystem
This is the platform layer we would most likely build Pillar 6 on top of, which makes it simultaneously an enabler and the single biggest platform risk. As of Q2 2026 the ecosystem grew from one registry (Dec 2025) to eight marketplaces, with Anthropic's own official directory (claude-plugins-official, 55+ curated plugins) sitting alongside large community registries (one indexes 21,700+ skills / 12,500+ MCP servers; SkillsMP indexes 800,000+ scraped from GitHub with minimal curation). Critically, Anthropic Claude Skills and OpenAI Codex plugins are both currently free distribution channels with no native billing layer. There is an open, unresolved GitHub feature request (#30727, filed March 2026) explicitly asking Anthropic to build "an official Anthropic-hosted marketplace with verified publishers... security review, code signing... a searchable catalog with ratings" - i.e., almost exactly our Pillar 6 plus a chunk of Pillar 7's provenance layer. If Anthropic ships that natively (and the pressure to do so is visible and public), our marketplace pillar's differentiation shrinks to "gamification + OKR-tie-in on top of Anthropic's rails," which is still valuable but materially smaller than "the marketplace" itself.
Source: [Totalum blog, agent skills marketplaces 2026](https://www.totalum.app/blog/agent-skills-marketplaces-2026), [GitHub anthropics/claude-code Issue #30727](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/issues/30727), [Claude Code security docs](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/security).

### Salesforce AgentExchange + Agentforce (Flex Credits)
AgentExchange itself is a free-to-browse catalog; the real monetization sits in Agentforce's consumption model: standard actions cost 20 credits ($0.10), voice actions 30 credits ($0.15), credits sell at $500/100k, and there's an alternative $2-per-24-hour-conversation model. Salesforce reported roughly $800M in Agentforce ARR by end of fiscal 2026 and 29,000 deals closed in Q4 alone. This is the most mature real "pay-per-action" economics found in this sweep, at enterprise scale, and Salesforce is explicitly name-checked in our own product materials as a company that could copy our approach with zero technical barrier. It has no gamification, no OKR-alignment scoring, and no attribution/provenance layer; it competes on CRM-anchored distribution, not on our C-suite/chief-of-staff wedge.
Source: [jitendrazaa.com Agentforce credits guide 2026](https://www.jitendrazaa.com/blog/salesforce/salesforce-agentforce-credits-cost-model-complete-guide-2026/), [AgentsIndex AgentExchange pricing](https://agentsindex.ai/pricing/salesforce-agentexchange), [SaaStrAI on Agentforce pricing models](https://www.saastr.com/salesforce-now-has-3-pricing-models-for-agentforce-and-maybe-right-now-thats-the-way-to-do-it/).

### Microsoft 365 Agent Store + Agent 365, Google Gemini Enterprise / Agent Gallery
Both consolidated their agent distribution into unified marketplaces in H1 2026 (Microsoft merged Azure Marketplace + AppSource into one Microsoft Marketplace with 11,000+ models and 4,000+ AI apps/agents; Google folded Agentspace into the rebranded Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform with an Agent Gallery pulling in Cloud Marketplace partner agents from Adobe, Salesforce, ServiceNow, Workday). Analyst framing worth flagging directly against our GTM: "the 2026 marketplace winners are the ones attached to existing enterprise procurement plumbing." Neither has gamification, OKR-wiring, or attribution features; both compete on procurement-layer distribution, which is a real structural advantage our consulting-led wedge does not have, and it is a plausible channel risk for the "engineering department -> whole org" GTM rung if a CIO simply buys agents through an existing Microsoft or Google enterprise agreement instead of engaging us.
Source: [Microsoft Learn, Agent Store in M365 Copilot](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365/copilot/copilot-agent-store), [Google Cloud Blog, new Gemini Enterprise](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/ai-machine-learning/the-new-gemini-enterprise-one-platform-for-agent-development), [Bain & Company, Google Cloud Next 2026](https://www.bain.com/insights/google_cloud_next_2026_the_agentic_enterprise_control_plane_comes_into_view/).

### ClickUp Super Agents / monday.com agents
Directly relevant because our own internal estate is "ClickUp-deep" (per the concept overview), which creates a real build-vs-buy tension. ClickUp Super Agents already ship real per-use metering: 100-300 credits per Super Agent use, with overage at $0.001/credit, i.e. literal "pay cents per skill run" shipping today, at a company our own materials call out by name as a low-barrier copier. monday.com bundles "monday agents" (pre-built + no-code builder) rather than a discrete marketplace. Neither has OKR-alignment scoring, gamified leagues, or origination attribution; both are workflow-automation-first, not conversational-front-door-first. The platform-dependency risk is worth flagging to Track 6 (data layer): if our GTM builds further into ClickUp as backbone (open question in the concept overview), we are building our marketplace pillar's likely first real competitor's own infrastructure underneath us.
Source: [ClickUp Super Agents](https://clickup.com/brain/agents), [workmanagementhub.com ClickUp Super Agents 2026 guide](https://workmanagementhub.com/clickup-super-agents-2026/), [monday.com vs ClickUp 2026 comparison](https://monday.com/blog/project-management/monday-com-vs-clickup/).

### Nevermined (agent payments/attribution protocol)
The closest single infrastructure match to the combination our Pillars 6+7 need: cryptographically signed, append-only usage logs (tamper-proof metering matching billed amounts line-item by line-item), a DID-based Agent Identity System giving each agent a persistent, portable reputation, and smart-contract-based revenue splitting across multiple contributing agents. It supports x402, Google's A2A, and AP2 protocols and reports 1.38M transactions since May 2025 with sub-cent micropayments from $0.001. This is B2B infrastructure for agent-to-agent commerce, not a workplace-idea or org-facing product, so it is not a direct competitor to our GUI/OKR/gamification stack; it is more plausibly a build-on-top option (or a well-capitalized adjacent player who could move up-stack into vertical apps faster than we can build the plumbing ourselves). Worth a deliberate build-vs-adopt decision rather than reinventing DID/metering primitives from scratch.
Source: [Nevermined, AI Agent Payment Systems 2026](https://nevermined.ai/blog/ai-agent-payment-systems), [Nevermined, MCP Monetization for AI Agents](https://nevermined.ai/blog/mcp-monetization-ai-agents).

### Cursor Agent Trace (open specification)
The most concrete, fastest-moving attribution effort found in this entire sweep, and the one closest to satisfying our must-have #5 ("attribution flags ambiguity") and Pillar 7's "what ran autonomously" question, but scoped to code only. Agent Trace is a vendor-neutral, CC-BY-4.0 JSON spec that tags code ranges as human/AI/mixed/unknown at file or line granularity, tied to conversation records; it explicitly does not touch legal ownership, training-data provenance, or quality. Adoption is real and fast for a spec only announced in February 2026: Cline, OpenCode, Cognition (Devin), git-ai, Google Jules, Amp, Vercel, and Cloudflare have all signaled support, and Thoughtworks featured it on its Technology Radar as a recommended assessment. If Agent Trace (or a successor building on the same pattern, note the academic "CoTrace" goal-level critique already circling it) extends past code into general work-product, it becomes the free, open, multi-vendor-backed standard our proprietary Pillar 7 would have to interoperate with rather than compete against head-on.
Source: [InfoQ, Agent Trace: Cursor Proposes Open Spec](https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/02/agent-trace-cursor/), [GitHub cursor/agent-trace](https://github.com/cursor/agent-trace), [Thoughtworks Technology Radar, Agent Trace](https://www.thoughtworks.com/en-us/radar/platforms/agent-trace).

### World ID / Humanity Protocol (Tools for Humanity)
World completed a major protocol overhaul in April 2026 (account-based architecture, key rotation, multi-key support) explicitly framed around enterprise and AI-agent use cases, and has pivoted its business model to charge enterprises for humanity verification while keeping it free for end users, with named partners Zoom (deepfake-detection beta for meeting participants), DocuSign (document-signing trust), Okta, Shopify, VanEck, and Tinder; an AgentKit lets agents act on behalf of a cryptographically verified human. Humanity Protocol pivoted away from proof-of-personhood language toward a "Proof-of-Trust" verifiable-credentials network (still palm-biometric-based) for identity/age/employment claims. Neither addresses "who originated this specific idea/work-product inside an org," but both are building exactly the human-verification substrate our must-have #7 ("no undisclosed bots or capture... capture is sanctioned and curated") and our Pillar 7 "who authorized" question would eventually need, and World is already inside Zoom meetings, i.e. adjacent to our own meeting-capture pillar. Regulatory friction is real (World is banned or restricted in Spain, Germany, Brazil, Hong Kong, Portugal, Kenya, South Korea), so this is not a smooth adoption story, but the enterprise partnership list is concrete and growing.
Source: [World, World ID Full-Stack Proof of Human](https://world.org/blog/announcements/world-id-full-stack-proof-of-human), [Biometric Update, World shifts to enterprise proof-of-humanity](https://www.biometricupdate.com/202606/world-shifts-from-crypto-identity-experiment-to-enterprise-proof-of-humanity), [Biometric Update, Zoom World ID beta](https://www.biometricupdate.com/202605/zoom-opens-beta-for-world-id-deepfake-verification-in-enterprise-meetings), [Biometric Update, Humanity Protocol pivot](https://www.biometricupdate.com/202602/humanity-protocol-pivots-from-proof-of-personhood-but-sticks-with-palm-biometrics).

### C2PA / Content Credentials
A mature, 6,000+ member coalition (as of January 2026) with real hardware signing (Leica, Sony, Nikon, Canon, Samsung Galaxy S26; Apple/Pixel 11 announced), platform display support (Meta, X, LinkedIn, TikTok), and a hard regulatory forcing function: EU AI Act Article 50 enforcement begins August 2026 and requires machine-readable AI-content disclosure, which proprietary in-platform labels alone cannot satisfy across jurisdictions. This is the dominant standard for "is this piece of media AI-generated / who made it," not for "who originated this workplace idea," so it is a different problem domain from our Pillar 7. It matters mainly as a possible interoperability requirement if our attribution layer ever touches externally-shared or client-facing content, and as an existence proof that provenance infrastructure can reach real regulatory teeth. An academic critique (arXiv, 2026) argues the spec is still flawed/incomplete and that a signed credential proves a device signed a file, not that the claim reflects reality, a limitation worth remembering if we ever borrow the pattern.
Source: [eyesift.com, C2PA Adoption Status 2026](https://www.eyesift.com/faq/c2pa-content-credentials-2026-cryptographic-provenance-adoption/), [Adobe, Content Authenticity for Enterprise](https://business.adobe.com/blog/content-authenticity-arrives-for-enterprises), [arXiv, Verifying Provenance of Digital Media](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.24890v1).

### Sureel AI (acquired by Warner Music Group, June 2026) and ProRata
Sureel traces GenAI content lineage back to original creators for royalty/compliance purposes and runs a name/image/likeness attribution suite for voice clones and AI avatars; Warner Music acquired it in June 2026 and will keep it operating as a standalone platform. ProRata combines licensed-content attribution with a 50% revenue-share model and closed a $40M Series B. Both prove "attribution startup" is a fundable, exitable thesis, strategic acquirers will pay for it, but both are locked to media/music/publishing verticals, not workplace ideas or internal org work-product, so they validate the category without being direct competitors.
Source: [TechCrunch, Warner Music acquires Sureel AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/10/warner-music-acquires-ai-attribution-startup-sureel-ai/), [Music Ally, Warner Music buying Sureel AI](https://musically.com/2026/06/10/warner-music-group-is-buying-ai-attribution-startup-sureel-ai/).

### DraftMarks (academic, CHI 2026)
A research system (not a company) that visualizes human-vs-AI contribution inline in a document via skeuomorphic marks (eraser crumbs, glue residue), evaluated as more legible than plain AI-usage disclosure. It is evidence of academic direction of travel toward legible, in-context attribution, not a market threat.
Source: [arXiv, DraftMarks](https://arxiv.org/html/2509.23505v1), [CHI 2026 proceedings, DraftMarks](https://dl.acm.org/doi/full/10.1145/3772318.3791109).

### Idea management platforms (IdeaScale, HYPE, ITONICS, Ideawake, Sideways 6, Brightidea, Wazoku)
This is the closest existing commercial category to "proof-of-origination for workplace ideas," and the clean finding from this sweep is that none of the market leaders do cryptographic or immutable attribution. Their 2026 positioning is AI-assisted idea clustering/evaluation plus financial ROI attribution (linking an implemented idea to measured revenue/cost-saving impact for board reporting), which is a real feature but different from "provably, immutably, this person originated this," and it is not tied to agent-run subtasks or OKR alignment scoring. The one blockchain-native example found, IdeaChain, is a niche open-innovation concept, not a market leader. This is a incumbent-owns-the-workflow risk more than a technology risk: these vendors already sit inside large enterprises as the "submit an idea" system of record, so if any of them decided to bolt on immutable attribution and agentic execution, they would start from real distribution, not zero.
Source: [ideawake.com, Definitive Guide to Innovation Management Platforms 2026](https://ideawake.com/the-definitive-guide-to-innovation-management-platforms-in-2026/), [inspireip.com, Top 15 Idea and Innovation Management Software 2026](https://inspireip.com/idea-and-innovation-management-software/), [Medium, IdeaChain](https://medium.com/innovation-machine/open-innovation-an-idea-about-ideas-ideachain-a1bcf22061c3).

### AI-powered employee recognition platforms (Workday + Achievers, Awardco, Advantage Club)
Workday and Achievers launched a joint AI-powered recognition/rewards product in April 2026, folded directly into the Workday experience, positioned around retention in an AI-driven labor market. This overlaps our Pillar 3 (gamification/rewards economy) more than Pillars 6/7: it analyzes peer recognition patterns to surface top contributors, which is social/reputational credit, not agent-run attribution or OKR-tied scoring, and it rides on Workday's existing HR-system-of-record distribution, a genuine adjacent threat to the "reward-not-replace" adoption thesis if a CHRO simply buys this instead of engaging us.
Source: [Workday newsroom, Workday and Achievers launch](https://newsroom.workday.com/2026-04-16-Workday-and-Achievers-Launch-AI-Powered-Recognition-and-Rewards-Solution-to-Boost-Employee-Engagement-and-Retention), [HR Brew, Workday bets on recognition](https://www.hr-brew.com/stories/2026/04/30/workday-achievers-recognition-partnership).

### Broader context: gender/credit gap and "AI appropriator" (signal, not a competitor)
Worth carrying into the pitch narrative rather than the threat table: a Lean In survey found only 18% of women who use AI at work report being praised for it versus 27% of men, and Littler Mendelson's 2025 HR guidance flags that few employers have addressed AI-assisted-work attribution in policy at all. This is market evidence that the underlying pain Pillar 7 targets (nobody knows who gets credit once AI is in the loop) is real, felt, and currently unaddressed by policy or product, i.e. supporting evidence for the pitch, not a competitor.
Source: [Women's Business Daily, AI Is Taking Credit for Your Work](https://www.womensbusinessdaily.com/career/ai-is-taking-credit-for-your-work-heres-how-to-make-sure-you-get-it/), [ComplexDiscovery, The AI Appropriator](https://complexdiscovery.com/the-ai-appropriator-a-new-species-of-credit-thief-is-reshaping-the-corporate-workplace/).

## What nobody does / whitespace

1. **Nobody combines pay-per-skill-run + cryptographic proof-of-origination + gamified OKR-alignment scoring in one product.** Every element exists somewhere (metering at Salesforce/ClickUp/Nevermined; attribution at Agent Trace/Nevermined/World ID; gamification at recognition platforms), but no single player found in this sweep stacks all three the way our seven pillars do. This is the core whitespace claim and it held up under direct search for the exact framing ("proof of origination," "idea provenance," "pay per skill run") returning no dedicated competitor.
2. **Nobody does horizontal, org-wide "who originated this idea/work-product" attribution.** Every attribution play found is vertical-locked: code (Cursor Agent Trace), music/NIL (Sureel), content licensing (ProRata), media authenticity (C2PA), academic writing (DraftMarks). None claims "any idea, any work-product, any employee, any department" the way our must-have #16 ("domain-agnostic... must apply towards any person in your organization") does.
3. **Nobody ties attribution to compensation/rewards.** Agent Trace records who wrote what; it does not feed a bonus pool. Idea-management ROI attribution feeds a dashboard; it does not feed a personal points/streak/league system. Our reward-not-replace thesis, wiring attribution directly into comp and league standing, is unclaimed territory.
4. **True usage-metered marketplaces for internal/org-facing skills barely exist yet.** Real "pay cents per run" economics exist today only for consumer/customer-facing agents (Intercom Fin at $0.99/resolution, Zendesk at $1.50-2.00/resolution) or generic PM-tool credits (ClickUp). Nobody has shipped a metered marketplace specifically for internal organizational skills/workflows tied to a alignment/output score, which is exactly the "pay cents every time a skill runs" quote from our own founders' call.
5. **Nobody is using proof-of-personhood infrastructure (World ID class) for internal workplace-idea attribution.** World's enterprise partnerships (Zoom, DocuSign, Okta) are all about verifying a real human is present/signing, not about which human should get credit for originating a specific idea inside a company. This is a genuine adjacent-technology gap our Pillar 7 could exploit, or could partner into rather than build from scratch, since the human-verification primitive already exists and is enterprise-credentialed.
6. **The marketplace consolidation window is closing, not opening.** Analyst commentary explicitly expects the current 8 agent-skill marketplaces to shrink to 3-4 dominant platforms within a year via acquisition/shutdown. If Pillar 6 is going to differentiate on being "the" marketplace (rather than gamification/attribution layered on someone else's), the window to stake that claim is short, and the most likely acquirers of a standalone skill marketplace are exactly the big platforms already listed here (Anthropic, OpenAI, Vercel), not us.
