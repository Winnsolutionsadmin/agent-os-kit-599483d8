S01
The category

Every company is about to run on agents. The real question is who takes us through it without breaking what works.

That part isn't in question. The question each CEO actually faces is simpler and more personal, and it is the one this business answers.

The product is deliberately unnamed today. The category it occupies has a name: the trusted companion that brings a company through the AI transition and gives it a head start. Consulting plus software, one accountable operator, one calm surface.

S03
The premise

Let's say the uncomfortable part ourselves: the frontier labs can build this.

That's not a risk to this plan. It's the premise of the market. Capability doesn't install itself. A company needs someone who knows its people, its meetings, its objectives, and its risk lines, and who stays accountable while the org changes underneath. That's a companion role, not a platform feature.

  • The premise is now visibly true: Anthropic launched a $1.5B enterprise AI services joint venture with Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs MEASURED own announcement + CNBC, 2026-05-04. The labs themselves are entering the transition motion.
  • Hygiene, in one line, because that's what it is: the system can swap the model vendor it runs on, and every account and key is audited. Counsel kept it 4/4. A hedge, not a fear posture.
The labs build the capability. Someone still has to walk each company through the transition. We're that companion, and we hand them a head start.

S08
The adoption engine

Every employee has heard the quiet threat inside "AI transformation." This system inverts it.

Adoption earns points, tiers, standing, and time back. The verbatim contract on S04 is the design brief. Three design rules keep it honest.

1
Decoupled from compensation.Counsel kept this 4/4. Recognition and time first; money mechanics only later, deliberately, with HR at the table.
2
Anti-abuse on from day one.Caps, outlier detection, role-adjusted scoring. the game-design collaborator, originating member and the game designer here, has watched his own published metric get gamed by professionals. The mechanics assume adversaries.
3
Output and value, never hours or activity.Nothing in the mechanics rewards looking busy.
  • Status, honestly tagged: the single-operator version runs today MEASURED (S07). The multi-person mechanics are design-stage; the mechanics definition lands 7/31. Nothing here claims a shipped multi-player system.
  • The backlash precedent is real and sourced: Meta's internal AI-token leaderboard (85,000+ employees) was shut down after press exposure, with the CTO stating token usage is not a measure of impact MEASURED Fortune, 2026-04-09. Surveillance with points fails. Reward with consent is the opposite design, and it's ours.
S09
Product law: how trust is earned
PANEL VERDICT, 4/4: "DON'T TOUCH IT"

Gates and a graduation bar do for agent work what covenants do for capital.

No agent ever acts on money, anything irreversible, or anything external without a human gate. No fee or tier purchases an exception, ever.
The system fails open to surfacing. Decisions, money, deadlines, and irreversible items can never be silently suppressed.
Autonomy is earned per decision class, never assumed. An agent graduates only after matching the human's actual choice 90% of the time across 20 or more repetitions, with executive sign-off.

Read it as what it is: risk-reduction structure. Gates, a written kill criterion, and a graduation bar cap the downside before anyone discusses the upside.

Every vendor says "human in the loop." Almost none can show you the graduation ledger. We can, and that's an auditable difference, not a slogan.

S12
The open ground, and the honest threat read

These two facts are true at once, and they belong on the same page.

The open ground

No funded company today occupies the specific stack this deck describes: objective-scored agent execution, plus adoption game mechanics, plus attribution, delivered as a companion engagement. UNVERIFIED as an absence-of-evidence claim; a monthly competitive review sustains it or retires it. We put no time window on this. Any number of months would be false precision, so we don't say one.

The threat read

Two of the seven pillars are commoditizing fast right now: meeting capture and agent chat surfaces, per the competitive dossiers in this corpus. Anyone building on those two pillars alone is building on sand, and we'd say so about our own plan if it did.

The reconciliation is the strategy itself. The defensible position was never any single pillar. It's the un-commoditized combination: scored objectives plus consented game mechanics plus earned autonomy, installed and operated by a companion who's accountable for the whole. Attribution supports this claim as a roadmap-stage pillar; it doesn't anchor it.
Whitespace claims usually die under one good question. This one is stated with its own counterargument attached, which is why it can survive the room.

S14
Business model

Consulting plus software, in that order, on purpose.

  • Consulting revenue leads. It funds the build and generates evidence at the same time (counsel-kept, 4/4). The precedent is signed: a $25K/mo retainer on the existing book MEASURED. The product itself has no revenue yet; we say that plainly.
  • Equity plus cash is the default on transformative engagements. Cash-only requires explicit partner sign-off. We want to be aligned with the outcome, not just paid for the attempt.
  • The skill marketplace is the long-term network effect. Tokenization is parked; it would detract right now.

The advantage in diligence-grade terms, not slogans: relationships compress sales cycles; delivery speed compounds client data; and the running reference implementation is a packaged, demonstrable asset no competitor can photograph.

On the people behind it: the operator is the owner; the vision and the operating estate are his. the game-design collaborator (game design, adoption mechanics) and the engineering collaborator (engineering, his the prior engineering framework framework) are originating members who earn equity through contribution. Entity formation and IP assignment are in motion this week.

A business model where the operator eats the same risk as the client is rare in consulting and rarer in software. That alignment is the product working on its own P&L.
