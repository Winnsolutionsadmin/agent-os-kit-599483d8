# Contribution-earns-equity: mechanism options for originating members

> Input for the 7/11 shared 3-way doc. Per decision 0003: Jarred is THE founder, the vision is his; Nick Metzler and Carter Razink are originating members who EARN equity through contribution, founder control preserved. Per counsel verdict 5: nothing locked under deadline pressure; counsel-level treatment on the framework itself. All numbers below are STRUCTURAL PLACEHOLDERS [unverified], not proposals; real percentages get set with counsel and Jarred only.

## Option A: milestone-vested grants (deliverable-triggered)

Each originating member receives a defined equity grant that vests in tranches tied to NAMED deliverables (e.g. Carter: Recurro doc, methodology memo, engineering-pillar build milestones; Nick: mechanics spec, anti-abuse design, pilot gamification live). No deliverable, no vest. Un-vested portions lapse.

- Why it fits: purest expression of "contribution earns equity"; each live commitment (7/10 Recurro doc, 7/15 methodology, 7/31 mechanics) becomes a vesting trigger; nonperformance self-corrects without a firing conversation.
- Red flags: deliverable definitions become negotiation surface; scope disputes ("does v1 of the mechanics doc count?") need an arbiter, which is Jarred, which stresses relationships; ongoing-effort work (maintenance, iteration) fits badly into one-shot milestones.

## Option B: advisor-style time vesting with a cliff

Standard advisor/early-contributor pattern: fixed grant, 2-4 year monthly vest, 6-12 month cliff, single-trigger acceleration off. Contribution enforced by the cliff plus a board (Jarred) right to end the relationship, stopping the vest.

- Why it fits: boring, counsel-familiar, cheap to paper (standard FAST-style agreements exist); zero per-deliverable negotiation; handles ongoing contribution naturally.
- Red flags: weakest link between equity and actual contribution; a coasting member still vests until Jarred acts; "advisor" framing may read as demotion to people who were on the founding call, which is a relationship risk exactly at the 7/11 conversation.

## Option C: hybrid, small time-vested base + milestone kickers

A modest time-vested base grant (recognizes originating-member status, cliff included) plus milestone-triggered kickers for the named high-value deliverables. Founder control preserved via share class or vote proxy regardless of option.

- Why it fits: honors the origination story AND prices the near-term deliverables; the 7/11 conversation gets a warm open ("you have a base grant") and a sharp incentive ("the big tranches ride on the 7/15 and 7/31 artifacts").
- Red flags: two mechanisms means two documents' worth of counsel time in a week when counsel is already loaded with formation + IP assignment; complexity invites more negotiation, not less.

## DECIDED (Jarred, 2026-07-08, roadmap-mapping session)

**Option C, hybrid, is the lead mechanism for the 7/11 shared doc.** Small time-vested base + milestone kickers tied to the named deliverables. Fallback if counsel bandwidth cannot carry two mechanisms this week: Option A pure. Real percentages remain unset; counsel + Jarred only.

## Recommendation (accepted above)

Option C, with the base kept small enough that a nonperforming member's total stake stays immaterial. It is the only structure that simultaneously (a) makes 0003's "contribution earns equity" literal, (b) gives the 7/11 doc a collaborative tone rather than a piecework tone, and (c) keeps the live commitment table (7/10, 7/15, 7/31) doing double duty as the vesting schedule. If counsel bandwidth this week cannot carry two mechanisms, fall back to Option A pure, since it is closest to 0003's letter.

## What this doc is NOT
Not a proposal to Nick or Carter. Nothing leaves this repo toward them without Jarred authoring and sending it himself.
