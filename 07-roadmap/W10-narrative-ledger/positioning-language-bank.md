## (a) The category statement

The category we occupy: the trusted companion that brings companies through the AI transition and gives them a head start. Phrasings, shortest first.

1. "We're the trusted companion that brings companies through the AI transition."
2. "Companies need a trusted companion to get through this transition. That's us."
3. "The labs build the capability. Someone still has to walk each company through the transition. We're that companion, and we hand them a head start."
4. "This isn't a tool you install. It's a companion that takes your company through the AI transition and leaves you ahead of it."
5. "Every company is going through this transition whether they plan to or not. We're the companion that makes sure they come out ahead."
6. "What we sell is a head start: a trusted companion that carries a company through the AI transition faster than it could go alone."
7. Longer form, for docs: "The frontier labs are building the capability. The transition still happens one company at a time, and each one needs a companion it trusts: something that captures how the company actually runs, scores work against its objectives, and earns autonomy step by step. That companion role is the category. The head start is the product of it."

Descriptor rules: say "the system," "this platform," "what we're building," or "the companion." Never a name. Never "the Agent OS" as if it were a brand.

## (b) The frontier-lab premise, stated confidently

The capability of Anthropic and the other frontier labs is the MARKET PREMISE, not our fear. Say it unprompted. Self-disclosure beats discovery.

1. "We know Anthropic and the other labs can do this. That's the premise of the market, not a threat to it."
2. "Yes, the labs can build this. That's exactly why every company now needs a companion through the transition. Capability arriving is what creates the demand."
3. "The strongest evidence for this market is who just entered it. In May 2026 Anthropic launched a $1.5B enterprise AI services company with Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs, embedding Anthropic engineers inside portfolio companies. OpenAI followed a week later with a $4B deployment company. (MEASURED: Anthropic's own announcement plus CNBC, 2026-05-04; OpenAI's own announcement, 2026-05-11; sources in `07-roadmap/W1-concept-doc-deck/competitor-sources.md`.)"
4. "When the lab itself stands up a $1.5B services company to walk enterprises through this, the premise stops being an argument. It's now visibly true."
5. "The labs entering the companion motion validates the category. What none of those ventures sells is our stack: scored objectives, consented mechanics, autonomy that's earned, not granted."
6. For a skeptic asking "why won't Anthropic just do this?": "They're already doing a version of it, at $1.5B, for Blackstone's portfolio. That's the market forming. The mid-market company that isn't a Goldman portfolio holding still needs a companion, and it needs one that starts from its own meetings, its own objectives, and its own people."

Rule: never present lab capability as an existential risk to us. Platform hedges are engineering hygiene and can be described that way if asked. The organizing frame is always premise, not fear.

## (c) The domino GTM, in the founder's order

Order is fixed: C-levels first, engineers next, all else on demand. Never reorder it.

1. One breath: "Dominos fall in order. C-levels first, as chief of staff and organizer. Engineers next, revamping and overhauling how they build. Everything else follows based on demand."
2. "We start at the top of the company, not the bottom. The C-level gets a chief of staff and an organizer first. Once the executive loop is running, engineering gets the revamp. After that, adoption spreads on pull, not push."
3. "The first domino is the executive's own desk: capture, organize, follow through. The second is engineering: overhaul how the work gets built. The rest of the org comes to us asking, and that's by design."
4. "Nobody below the C-level is asked to adopt anything until the executive is already living in it. Demand does the rest."
5. Why this order, if asked: "Because the executive's loops are the most expensive in the building and the easiest to measure. Prove it there, and every later domino falls on evidence instead of a mandate."

Engineering-domino note: Carter Razink's Recurro framework is an input to the engineering pillar. If it comes up: "Recurro is Carter's own framework for agentic engineering. It feeds our engineering domino." Never present it as the product or a product name.

1. "I'm the founder. The vision is mine." (Jarred speaking.)

3. "Nick Metzler and Carter Razink are originating members. They earn equity through contribution."

## (e) The ask framing

The ask, until the founder supersedes it: a scoped 90-day design-partner pilot plus a smaller strategic check, papered separately. Funding is raised on achievement plus faith from investors Jarred already knows.

1. "We're asking for a scoped 90-day design-partner pilot and a smaller strategic check. That's the whole ask."
2. "This isn't a raise. It's a pilot with named metrics, a written kill criterion, and a smaller check sized to the evidence."
3. "The pilot is the operator seat. The check is the owner seat. Two papers, never blended."
4. "We raise on achievement and faith, from investors we already know. The raise trigger is a signed design-partner pilot. Until that exists, we're not raising."
5. "The evidence-backed raise comes around month six, built on a ledger of measured results a known investor can verify by talking to the design partner."
6. If pressed for a bigger number: "A big diffuse raise before the evidence exists would be the wrong structure for both sides. The pilot generates the evidence. The ownership conversation follows the evidence."
7. Passing gracefully: "If the answer is no, the structure doesn't expire. We'll come back when the ledger shows a signed pilot and day-30 results, so the next look is at measured numbers instead of a proposal."
