## 1. Who the domino-1 buyer is

**Title: the CEO, personally.** The product's first seat is an AI chief of staff / organizer for a C-level executive; the buyer is the executive who will sit in that seat, not a procurement function and not a delegate. Grounding:

- The GTM ladder is C-suite chief-of-staff seat first ("Sell the C-levels first. Then engineering. Then the whole org.", `00-call-notes/2026-07-08-agentic-org-os-rsi-for-orgs-jarred-carter-nick-strategy-call.md`; good/better/best packaging in `01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 2).
- SURVEY: the Stanford Digital Economy Lab study of 51 successful enterprise AI deployments (Pereira, Graylin, Brynjolfsson, March 2026, cited in `03-execution/pilot-design.md`) found CEO personal ownership of the initiative, with weekly check-ins, was the single strongest success predictor; 73% of successful deployments started deliberately small and 63% were framed as experiments. Our buyer must be willing to be that CEO.
- A COO or President who fully owns operations may be an acceptable alternate primary buyer [unverified: founder has only specified "C-levels first"; whether CEO-only or any-C-level is a positioning choice for Jarred].
- Where a human chief of staff already exists, they are either the internal champion or the perceived-displacement blocker; which one is an account-qualification question, not a product question [unverified].

**Org size band: mid-market.** Working band: roughly $50M to $3B revenue, lean executive team [unverified as a founder decision; composed from the evidence below].

- VENDOR/press: Accenture Edge + Google Cloud launched pre-built agentic bundles in July 2026 explicitly targeting the $300M-$3B mid-market revenue band, and the competitive dossier characterizes that as "the same band our GTM is aimed at" (`04-competitive/landscape-consulting-hybrids.md`). That characterization is the dossier author's read, not a founder decision [unverified as founder-confirmed].
- VENDOR/press: the Anthropic + Blackstone/H&F/Goldman JV (May 2026) targets "hundreds of PE-owned mid-sized companies" (`04-competitive/landscape-consulting-hybrids.md`), which locates the category's contested ground in the mid-market and supports positioning below the large-enterprise tier the lab JVs and global SIs serve first.
- MEASURED (internal record): the closest live reference relationship is a ~$4B-valuation, $60B-AUM digital-asset company whose CEO responded receptively to an overhaul framing, already paying a $25K/mo consulting retainer (`02-sweep/swarm-client-relationships.md`; `01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 7). That sets the practical top of the band for a relationship-anchored motion.
- The floor is set by domino-2 economics, not by domino-1 fit: an account with no internal engineering organization has no second domino inside the account (see section 5), so accounts below roughly 50 employees or without a real engineering function are servable but strategically shallow [unverified inference, generalized from the expansion analysis in `03-execution/pilot-design.md`].

**Org shape.**

- Relationship-anchored: the buyer is someone Jarred already knows or can reach through one warm introduction. This mirrors the funding model (achievement + faith from known investors, decision 0003) and the counsel-verified moat reframe: relationships = sales-cycle compression (`01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 12, revision 2).
- Founder-led or owner-led governance where the CEO can personally commit to a 90-day design-partner pilot without a committee (`03-execution/pilot-design.md` 30/60/90 structure; `07-roadmap/W7-pilot-design/tasks.md`).
- Decision-bottlenecked at the top: heavy meeting load, follow-up debt, reporting overhead concentrated on the executive. MEASURED on the reference implementation (fleet-of-one estate): "39 overdue follow-ups, saving 3-6 hours/week" was the addressed pain the CRM stack solved for one operator (`01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 3, Tier A). Generalizing that pain profile to target buyers is [unverified] until design-partner discovery confirms it.
- Trusted-companion demand: the buyer knows frontier labs can build this class of tooling and wants a companion to bring their specific company through the transition with a head start (decision 0003, binding interpretation 3). Buyers who want to wait for a platform vendor to ship it are not the ICP.

## 2. The budget line the engagement draws from

Ranked by fit for domino-1. Final ranking is 8.2's job; this is the ICP-side read.

1. **Executive office / CEO discretionary budget.** The natural comparison the buyer already understands is hiring a human chief of staff: VENDOR-reported $200K-$350K base for the emerging "AI Chief of Staff" executive role at Series B+ companies and large enterprises as of June 2026 (valueaddvc.com, cited in `03-execution/pilot-design.md`). A design-partner engagement priced under one year of that hire is a same-budget-line substitution story.
2. **Consulting / professional-services budget.** The delivery model is a consulting + software hybrid ("an in-the-box solution that requires out-of-box consultation per business", `00-call-notes/2026-07-08-agentic-org-os-rsi-for-orgs-jarred-carter-nick-strategy-call.md`). MEASURED precedent: one signed $25K/mo consulting engagement (`01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 7). A floated "$15K + 50bps" agentic-consulting structure exists as an auto-generated opportunity-brief thesis only ([unverified] as pricing; `01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 8).
3. **Not the IT/software budget at domino-1.** The enterprise-assistant comparables are the wrong shape for a single-executive seat: VENDOR pricing puts Glean at roughly $50-75 per user/month with a ~100-seat floor ($240K-$480K/yr typical) and Moveworks-via-ServiceNow at roughly $100K-$600K+/yr with 8-16 week deployments (both cited in `03-execution/pilot-design.md`). Those are procurement-led, seat-floored purchases. The software budget line enters at domino-2 and domino-3.
4. **Equity component on transformative scope.** Counsel-verified default: equity + cash on transformative engagements; cash-only requires partner sign-off (`01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 12, revision 3). ICP consequence: the buyer must be an owner or control the cap-table decision, which reinforces founder-led/owner-led org shape.

Pilot-cost context: SURVEY (aggregated web synthesis, secondhand, flagged as such in `03-execution/pilot-design.md`): general enterprise AI pilots are commonly quoted at $50K-$200K over 8-16 weeks. The counsel-mandated ask shape is a scoped 90-day design-partner pilot plus a smaller strategic check, not a large raise (`01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 12, revision 1).

## 3. Trigger events that open the door

1. **Personal operational drowning.** The executive is the bottleneck: follow-up debt, meeting load, reporting overhead. MEASURED analogue on the reference implementation (39 overdue follow-ups, 3-6 hours/week recovered, section 1 above); the demo talk-track quantifies the after-state as "14 actions, 3 approvals, 41 seconds of attention" for a full day of agent work (MEASURED as a built demo scenario, not a client result; `01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` sections 3 and 7).
2. **Board or investor pressure to have an AI story.** Urgency thesis from the founding call: "Everybody in the next six, nine, twelve months are going to have an operating system product for their business. The question is who's gonna be the first one." (`01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 1).
3. **Early-mover anxiety with honest numbers behind it.** SURVEY stack: ~1% of orgs fully agentic; McKinsey 62% experimenting vs ~6% high performers; Gartner 17% deployed; MIT ~5% of value captured (`01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 7). The pitch-legal framing is loop-deletion economics, not multipliers (CLAUDE.md hard law).
4. **A failed or stalled prior AI pilot.** SURVEY: MIT's "GenAI Divide" report found ~95% of GenAI pilots fail to reach measurable P&L impact within six months (methodology contested, treat directionally); adjacent 2026 figures put stall/abandonment at 42-63% (Gartner, S&P Global, secondhand; all cited with caveats in `03-execution/pilot-design.md`). A burned buyer is primed for the trusted-companion category (decision 0003) and for externally-delivered pilots, which SURVEY data says succeed roughly 2x as often as internal builds (MIT, same source).
5. **Seeing the demonstration.** The fleet-of-one estate is a packaged, demonstrable proof asset ("this operation is the demonstration", `01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` sections 1 and 12). A live estate readout is the W3-decided demo artifact (`07-roadmap/ROADMAP.md`, resolved questions).
6. **Ownership or capital transition.** New funding round, PE acquisition, or growth inflection that resets the operating agenda [unverified as a trigger for our specific buyer]. Category support: the lab JVs explicitly target PE-owned companies at the moment of ownership transition (VENDOR/press, `04-competitive/landscape-consulting-hybrids.md`).
7. **An existing engagement or warm relationship touch.** The validated wedge is the consultative engagement pattern, already reused across clients (`01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 3, Tier A: /newclient scaffold, 6+ active clients). Domino-1 conversations open where trust already exists (decision 0003).

## 4. Disqualifiers

1. **The CEO will not personally own it.** Delegation to an innovation team or a committee inverts the strongest SURVEY success predictor (Stanford 51-deployment study, via `03-execution/pilot-design.md`). No CEO ownership, no engagement.
2. **Replace-not-reward intent.** A buyer who wants headcount reduction as the headline outcome violates the adoption contract ("we're doing this so you can reward your employees and retain them", `01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` sections 1 and 5, must-have 18). Gamified adoption cannot work against a workforce that correctly reads the tool as a layoff instrument [the second clause is reasoned inference, unverified].
3. **Demands day-1 autonomy.** Product law: never auto-act on money, irreversible, or external actions without a human gate; autonomy is earned via graduation (90% agreement over 20+ reps per decision class) (`01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 5, must-haves 1-4; CLAUDE.md hard law). A buyer who wants a fully autonomous executive agent on day 1 is asking us to break product law.
4. **Will not sign a baseline and a kill criterion.** The pilot design requires a pre-pilot timing baseline, a named business owner, and a written kill criterion before day 1 (`03-execution/pilot-design.md` recommendation; SURVEY support: named metric at kickoff and named C-1+ sponsor are two of the four factors explaining 71% of payback-speed variance, attributed to Bain, secondhand). No baseline means no falsifiable ROI claim, which breaks honest-math law downstream.
5. **Procurement-led, cold-institutional buying.** The motion is relationship-anchored conviction (decision 0003). An RFP process with no warm path is out of scope for domino-1 [unverified as a permanent rule; it is the v1 read of the funding/GTM posture].
6. **Securities-grade disclosure sensitivity in the capture scope before W9 lands.** Meeting capture around executives whose conversations routinely contain material non-public information (public-company earnings, treasury moves) requires a data-governance and counsel posture that does not exist yet (counsel blind spot 2, `01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 12; general risk analysis in `03-execution/pilot-design.md`, generalized here from a pre-correction dossier). Such accounts are deferred or scoped to exclude disclosure-sensitive material until the W9 posture is real, not rejected forever.
7. **Client-experience fragility.** An org in the middle of a customer-facing crisis, where any pilot friction degrades client experience, violates delivery law 19 ("client experience must never degrade", `01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 5) [application to prospect qualification is inference, unverified].
8. **Soft disqualifier: no engineering organization.** Not fatal for domino-1, but it caps the account at the chief-of-staff seat because domino-2 has nothing to land on (section 5). Weigh account-level LTV accordingly [unverified inference].

## 5. Domino-2 inside the same account: the engineer tier

Founder-confirmed sequence (decision 0003): "Engineers next - revamp/overhaul." Call-note grounding: "The engineers will drive the demand of every other aspect of the business" (`00-call-notes/2026-07-08-agentic-org-os-rsi-for-orgs-jarred-carter-nick-strategy-call.md`).

- **Who the domino-2 buyer is:** the CTO or VP Engineering, with the CEO (the domino-1 buyer) as the internal sponsor who has already seen 90 days of gated agent work on their own calendar. The expansion sale is internal referral, not a new cold motion [unverified as founder-specified; consistent with the ladder].
- **What is sold:** an engineering-department revamp/overhaul. Carter Razink's Recurro framework (his recursive-self-improvement-for-engineering-orgs concept) is an input to this engineering pillar, not the product (CLAUDE.md correction 2). Carter's Recurro doc is due 7/10 and will shape the domino-2 offer definition (CLAUDE.md live commitments).
- **Evidence base:** the 16x case study (4-engineer team at 16x baseline vs an 18-engineer control at 2.5x) is [unverified] until Carter's methodology memo lands (due 7/15); until then the only deck-usable framing is output-parity, "4 matched the output of 18" (CLAUDE.md hard law; `01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 12, revision 8).
- **Budget line shift:** domino-2 draws from the engineering productivity/tooling budget rather than the executive office. VENDOR precedent that engineering buyers accept outcome-shaped pricing at scale: Cognition/Devin sells on outcomes (PRs merged), $492M ARR run-rate (`04-competitive/landscape-consulting-hybrids.md`). Pricing shape is 8.2's job.
- **Entry condition:** domino-1 must clear its 30/60/90 success thresholds first (thresholds defined in task 8.3; instrumentation in W7 task 7.3). The founder-defined raise trigger, a signed design-partner pilot, sits at domino-1; second-domino pull is supporting ledger evidence, not the trigger (`07-roadmap/ROADMAP.md`).
- **Account-shape requirement:** an internal engineering organization big enough for a before/after story, plausibly 10+ engineers given the 4-vs-18 shape of the case study [unverified inference; no source sets a floor].
- **Domino-3:** "All else follows based on demand" (decision 0003), the good/better/best whole-org tier (`01-overview/product-concept-overview-2026-07-08.md` section 2). Out of scope for this ICP v1.
