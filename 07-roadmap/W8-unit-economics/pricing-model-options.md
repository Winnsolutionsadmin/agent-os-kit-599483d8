## Option A: Companion Retainer (pilot fee + monthly retainer + software conversion)

The default consultative shape, closest to the MEASURED precedent.

- **Phase 1 (days 1-90):** a fixed design-partner pilot fee. Working band $45-75K for the 90 days [unverified as a founder decision; sits at the concessionary bottom of the SURVEY $50-200K pilot band, defensible as deliberate design-partner pricing]. Covers pilot phase scope: capture + one gated workflow days 1-30, gamification 31-90 (counsel-kept phasing).
- **Phase 2 (post-pilot):** monthly companion retainer. Working band $20-30K/mo, anchored on the MEASURED $25K/mo signed precedent. Story to the buyer: roughly the fully loaded monthly cost of the full-time chief of staff you would otherwise hire ($240-265K+/yr true cost, VENDOR secondhand), with delivery and the software seat included.
- **Phase 3 (domino-2/3 maturity):** software conversion. As playbooks harden into product, part of the retainer converts to per-seat or per-org software subscription (domino-3 whole-org anchors: $20-50/user/mo stacked OKR+gamification band, up to $200-600K/yr ACV enterprise-assistant shape). Consulting hours redeploy to the next account.
- **Equity+cash default:** applies whenever scope is transformative (org-wide overhaul, domino-2 entry). The pilot alone may be cash-only WITH partner sign-off if the account is strategically minor [unverified; founder call].
- **Pros:** matches the proven engagement pattern; predictable revenue; the retainer IS the relationship (trusted-companion positioning, decision 0003). **Cons:** revenue scales with operator capacity until conversion; single-operator estate is a named counsel blind spot (section 12, blind spot 3).

## Option B: Milestone Ladder (pay per threshold cleared)

Outcome-shaped, mapped to the 30/60/90 instrumentation (8.3 defines the thresholds).

- **Structure:** modest signing fee (e.g. $15-25K [unverified working figure]) + fixed payments released as each pilot threshold is cleared: day-30 (capture live + one gated workflow in production), day-60 (gamification live, adoption threshold), day-90 (ROI evidence per 8.3). Post-pilot, milestone pricing continues per domino: engineering-overhaul milestones at domino-2.
- **Category legitimacy:** ~1 in 4 McKinsey fee dollars now outcome-linked (VENDOR/press, via 8.4); Cognition/Devin sells engineering outcomes at scale (VENDOR secondhand, `04-competitive/landscape-consulting-hybrids.md`).
- **Equity+cash default:** milestone completions can vest the equity component symmetrically with cash (mirrors the contribution-earns-equity logic the estate already uses internally, decision 0003).
- **Pros:** strongest achievement-proof artifact per dollar; every paid milestone is a ledger entry that supports the achievement+faith funding model; de-risks the buyer's committee-of-one decision. **Cons:** revenue timing risk sits on us; requires the W7 baseline + kill-criterion contract discipline to be airtight; a missed threshold is public inside the account.

## Option C: Retainer + Equity Kicker (transformative engagements)

The equity+cash default made structural, for accounts where the engagement is a genuine company transformation.

- **Structure:** Option A's retainer spine at a reduced cash rate (e.g. $15-20K/mo [unverified working figure]) + an equity or warrant component sized to the transformation scope. The buyer must control the cap-table decision, which the ICP already requires (founder-led/owner-led org shape, ICP section 2).
- **Sizing discipline:** equity percentage is [unverified] and account-specific; no number is proposed here. Counsel-level securities review is already mandated before 7/17 (section 12, kept list) and any equity instrument goes through that same pass. Dual-role/conflict disclosure in writing before money moves (section 12, revision 11).
- **Pros:** aligns us with the account's outcome for years, embodies trusted-companion positioning, and lowers the cash bar for the buyer at the moment of maximum skepticism. **Cons:** illiquid revenue; valuation and securities complexity per account; concentrates our upside in client outcomes we only partially control.

## Option D: Strategic Check + Concessionary Pilot (the 7/17-18 shape)

Not a repeatable pricing model; the specific counsel-mandated structure for the first design partner.

- **Structure:** the scoped 90-day design-partner pilot at concessionary pricing (bottom of Option A's pilot band, or Option B's ladder with soft milestones) + a smaller strategic check as investment, papered separately from the services agreement. The check is the faith component; the pilot is the achievement component (decision 0003: raises happen on achievement + faith).
- **Discipline:** the two documents never blend; the investment does not buy pilot-scope concessions beyond what any design partner gets, and conflict-of-interest disclosure is written (section 12, revision 11). The signed pilot itself is the raise trigger (ROADMAP resolved question); the pricing here optimizes for a signature and a clean ledger entry, not margin.
- **Pros/cons:** maximal signature probability and evidence value; deliberately sacrifices near-term margin; usable roughly once or twice before it stops being special.
