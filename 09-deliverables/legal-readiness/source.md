## The current standard: the YC post-money SAFE

- A SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) is a right to future equity that converts at a later priced financing. It has no maturity date and no interest, which is what makes it lighter than a convertible note. [web-checked 2026-07-09]
- The **post-money** SAFE has been the standard since 2018, replacing the earlier pre-money SAFE. Its defining property: founder and investor can compute the exact ownership sold, and therefore dilution, at signing. [web-checked 2026-07-09]
- The current forms on YC's documents page are **Postmoney Safe, Valuation Cap Only v1.1** (US) and **Postmoney Safe, MFN Only v1.2**, with international variants for Canada, Cayman, and Singapore. [web-checked 2026-07-09]
- The **valuation-cap-and-discount** variant was removed from YC's site around August 2021 (an archived copy remains on the Wayback Machine); the Safe User Guide still describes it. In that variant the Discount Rate equals 100 minus the discount percent, and at conversion whichever of the cap or the discount favors the investor applies. [web-checked 2026-07-09]
- Source: YC documents page and the "Primer for post-money safe v1.1" (ycombinator.com/documents), retrieved 2026-07-09. VENDOR (YC's own forms and guidance).

## Cap vs discount considerations

- **Valuation cap** sets the maximum valuation at which the SAFE converts. A lower cap gives the investor more ownership per dollar if the priced round is above the cap. On a post-money SAFE the cap directly fixes the investor's ownership floor.
- **Discount** gives the investor a percentage reduction off the priced-round price per share. It rewards early risk without setting a valuation.
- **Cap only** (the current standard form) is the simplest: usually only the cap is negotiated. **Cap and discount** stacks both, with the investor taking whichever is better at conversion. **MFN only** (no cap, no discount) gives the investor the best terms granted to any later SAFE holder.
- For a first, relationship-anchored strategic check, the fewer moving parts the better; the standard cap-only post-money form exists for exactly this. The selection is still counsel plus founder.

Four objections, each with the honest counter. The rule across all four: concede the technology baseline plainly. Capability is the market premise, not our fear; frontier-lab immunity is never claimed. The moat is the organizational psychology of adoption and the trust relationship, not the model.

## The matrix

**1. "We will just build this internally."**
- Concede: a capable internal team can assemble the components; none of the parts are secret.
- Honest counter: building it is not the blocker; getting a whole organization to actually adopt it is. The adoption layer, reward-not-replace, gamification, and human-gated delivery, is the hard part internal builds skip. Externally-partnered tools reach measurable impact more often than internal builds (MIT NANDA 2025; methodology publicly contested, cite directionally only). CORPUS.

**2. "Copilot-class bundles already ship this."**
- Concede: the platform vendors and frontier labs can and will ship agent capability; that is the market premise.
- Honest counter: a horizontal copilot is raw capability without the organizational container: no objective-scoring spine, no gamified adoption, no earned graduated-autonomy governance. Enterprises want a safe, governable companion to cross the transition, not more raw capability. Of organizations experimenting with agents, only about 23 percent have scaled even one system into production and full agentic operation is rarer still; the gap is adoption, not model access. SURVEY (McKinsey Nov 2025).

**3. "There are many agent startups doing this."**
- Concede: many startups ship agent orchestration and workflow tooling.
- Honest counter: they sell software. This is consulting-plus-software that takes cash and equity because it is an in-the-box solution requiring out-of-box consultation per business. The differentiator is the trusted-companion delivery, the relationships that compress the sales cycle, and a running reference implementation that is the demonstration, not a roadmap slide. MEASURED (the reference implementation runs today).

**4. "A big consultancy plus ChatGPT already does this."**
- Concede: large consultancies pair advisory with frontier models competently.
- Honest counter: that engagement delivers a report and leaves. This leaves a running, gamified operating system behind, with provenance on the work and autonomy that is earned rep by rep behind a human gate. The deliverable is an adopted system, not slideware.

## The through-line (say it the same way every time)
- The technology baseline is conceded everywhere. Capabilities do not equal adoption. The moat is organizational psychology, gamification, and the trusted-companion relationship that carries a company across the transition and leaves it ahead.
