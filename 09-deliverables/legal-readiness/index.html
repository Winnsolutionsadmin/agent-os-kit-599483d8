Entity formation and IP assignment checklist

▲ DRAFT FOR LAWYER REVIEW. NOT LEGAL ADVICE. DO NOT EXECUTE.
status DRAFT v0.1 owner Jarred + counsel deadline this week, before 7/17 survival-critical

A clean legal container with owner control preserved and an unbroken chain of title on the IP that makes the company worth forming. A checklist, not an opinion; a licensed attorney selects the actual instruments.

A. Entity

  • Entity type and state chosen with counsel. Delaware C-corporation is the diligence-standard shape; the choice stays counsel plus owner. Vendor paths are framework only. ASSUMPTION
  • Named legal / compliance owner recorded (counsel blind spot 3: one named human accountable, not a diffuse team).
  • Owner confirms the legal form of Winn.solutions (LLC, DBA, or sole proprietorship). Unresolved in the estate and load-bearing: intercompany or background-IP paper cannot be clean against an entity that is not a distinct legal person.

B. Owner control (control, not just percentage)

  • Board composition. Owner controls the board at formation. Originating members do not receive board seats by default.
  • Voting. Owner retains voting control by an instrument counsel selects (dual-class, voting agreement, or irrevocable proxy). Contribution grants can be non-voting or proxied so earned economics never transfer control.
  • Reserved decisions. A written list reserved to the owner: issuing equity, incurring debt, changing purpose, selling or licensing core IP, hiring or removing officers, budgets, any related-party transaction.
  • Protective provisions align with the reserved-decisions list so control survives future financings.

C. IP assignment: background vs foreground schedule

  • Background IP schedule lists the owner's pre-existing estate assets assigned in as the seed platform: the fleet HUD, the OKR engine, the achievement engine, the meeting-capture pipeline, the skill registry, and their reusable patterns. MEASURED
  • Foreground IP is everything created specifically for the company after formation; it belongs to the company.
  • Client-specific material is EXCLUDED: consulting client configurations, client data, and per-client feedback registries stay with Winn.solutions.
  • Mima is EXCLUDED by name from every assignment schedule (a separate venture; not part of this product).
  • Prior-inventions carve-outs filed for every contributor: each collaborator keeps their pre-existing IP; the owner excludes Mima and other out-of-scope projects.

D. Assignment chain (mechanics counsel will paper)

  • Present-tense "hereby assigns" language (not "agrees to assign").
  • Real consideration for the owner's IP assignment: owner stock, not a gift.
  • Section 83(b) election filed within 30 days of any restricted-stock grant tied to the IP assignment.
  • A Confidential Information and Invention Assignment Agreement, with the prior-inventions carve-out attached, for the owner and each contributor.
  • Existing client agreements pulled and checked for an invention-assignment or "improvements" clause that could sweep in the estate tooling (California Labor Code 2870 / 2872).

E. Conflict-of-interest hygiene

  • Where a prospective investor is also a contact in a separate consulting engagement, ordinary written conflict-of-interest disclosure is prepared and delivered before terms are discussed. A routine disclosure item, nothing more.

An outline of the terms a contribution-earns-equity grant would contain, one per originating member, so counsel can draft quickly. Not a grant and not an offer. Nothing goes to the game designer or the engineer except through the owner. No percentages; those are owner plus counsel only. The word "co-owner" is never used; the parties are originating members.

Structure (Option C hybrid, owner-decided)

  • Time-vested base grant (small). A modest restricted grant per originating member that recognizes origination, with a cliff (counsel sets cliff and term). Kept small enough that a non-performing member's total stake stays immaterial.
  • Milestone kickers. Additional tranches, each tied to one named deliverable, vesting only on acceptance of that deliverable.

Unvested treatment

  • Unvested tranches lapse and are forfeited if the tied deliverable is not accepted, or if the member departs, before vesting. No acceleration on non-delivery.
  • The time-vested base stops vesting on departure at the then-current vested amount, subject to the cliff.

Termination path

  • The owner (or the board the owner controls) may end the relationship at any time. On termination, all unvested equity lapses; vested equity is treated per counsel's standard leaver provisions.
  • Termination does not, by itself, transfer or reclaim any prior-inventions carve-out (the prior engineering framework stays the engineer's; any company license to it survives per its own terms).

Owner control preserved

  • Grants are structured so earned economics never carry voting control (non-voting class or an irrevocable proxy, consistent with document 1).
  • Section 83(b) election filed within 30 days of any restricted-stock grant.

Explicitly out of scope for this outline

No valuation, no percentage, no dollar figure. No investment terms (the strategic check is a separate document with no contingency to this one). No employment terms.

SAFE and instrument overview

▲ DRAFT FOR LAWYER REVIEW. NOT LEGAL ADVICE. DO NOT EXECUTE.
status DRAFT v0.1 owner Jarred + counsel deadline before terms are discussed (pre-7/17)

The investment paper's overview: a plain-language map of the standard instrument, not a recommendation and not an offer. Instrument selection is left to the owner and counsel. This document has no contingency to the pilot paper; a strategic check and a design-partner pilot are two separate decisions.

The current standard: the YC post-money SAFE

  • A SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) is a right to future equity that converts at a later priced financing. No maturity date, no interest, which makes it lighter than a convertible note. web-checked 2026-07-09
  • The post-money SAFE has been standard since 2018, replacing the pre-money SAFE. Its defining property: owner and investor compute the exact ownership sold, and therefore dilution, at signing. web-checked 2026-07-09
  • The current forms on YC's documents page are Postmoney Safe, Valuation Cap Only v1.1 (US) and Postmoney Safe, MFN Only v1.2, with international variants for Canada, Cayman, and Singapore. web-checked 2026-07-09
  • The valuation-cap-and-discount variant was removed from YC's site around August 2021 (an archived copy remains on the Wayback Machine); the Safe User Guide still describes it. Discount Rate equals 100 minus the discount percent; at conversion whichever of cap or discount favors the investor applies. web-checked 2026-07-09
  • Source: YC documents page and the "Primer for post-money safe v1.1" (ycombinator.com/documents), retrieved 2026-07-09. VENDOR

Cap vs discount considerations

  • Valuation cap sets the maximum valuation at conversion. On a post-money SAFE the cap fixes the investor's ownership floor.
  • Discount gives a percentage reduction off the priced-round price per share; it rewards early risk without setting a valuation.
  • Cap only (the current standard form) is simplest; usually only the cap is negotiated. Cap and discount stacks both, investor takes whichever is better. MFN only gives the investor the best terms granted to any later SAFE holder.
  • For a first, relationship-anchored check, fewer moving parts is better; the standard cap-only post-money form exists for exactly this. The selection is still counsel plus owner.

Capitalization effect illustration (placeholder numbers, not a proposal)

The mechanics only, with invented numbers to show the shape. Every figure here is illustrative and is NOT a proposed term. ASSUMPTION

  • On a post-money SAFE, ownership on conversion is approximately the investment divided by the post-money valuation cap.
  • Illustrative: a strategic check of $250,000 on a post-money valuation cap of $5,000,000 converts to roughly 5.0 percent of the post-money capitalization. ASSUMPTION
  • That percentage is measured against the post-money cap, so later SAFEs or the priced round dilute the owner and prior holders, not this investor's stated post-money percentage, until the round itself. ASSUMPTION
  • Real cap, check size, and instrument are set by the owner with counsel and appear in the definitive documents, not here.

Accredited-investor and private-placement note (general)

  • A strategic check into a private company is a securities sale, typically placed under a Regulation D exemption, which generally means the investor is accredited and the issuance is documented and, where required, notice-filed. General federal framing only; counsel confirms the exemption and filings.
  • Where the investor is also a contact in a separate consulting engagement, ordinary written conflict-of-interest disclosure precedes the discussion of terms (document 1).

Instrument selection (left to owner + counsel)

SAFE (lightest, standard for a first check), convertible note (adds maturity and interest), or a priced round (heaviest, sets a valuation now). No default is asserted here.

Competitor objection-handling matrix

▲ DRAFT FOR LAWYER REVIEW. NOT LEGAL ADVICE. DO NOT EXECUTE. Internal owner-prep; do not send.
status DRAFT v0.1 owner Jarred deadline before 7/17 (prep) internal only, not investor-facing

Four objections, each with the honest counter. The rule across all four: concede the technology baseline plainly. Capability is the market premise, not our fear; frontier-lab immunity is never claimed. The moat is the organizational psychology of adoption and the trust relationship, not the model.

ObjectionConcedeHonest counter
"We will just build this internally." A capable internal team can assemble the components; none of the parts are secret. Building it is not the blocker; getting a whole organization to adopt it is. The adoption layer (reward-not-replace, gamification, human-gated delivery) is the hard part internal builds skip. Externally-partnered tools reach measurable impact more often than internal builds (MIT NANDA 2025; methodology publicly contested, cite directionally only). CORPUS
"Copilot-class bundles already ship this." Platform vendors and frontier labs can and will ship agent capability; that is the market premise. A horizontal copilot is raw capability without the organizational container: no objective-scoring spine, no gamified adoption, no earned graduated-autonomy governance. Enterprises want a safe, governable companion, not more raw capability. Of organizations experimenting with agents, only about 23 percent have scaled even one system into production and full agentic operation is rarer still; the gap is adoption, not model access. SURVEY (McKinsey Nov 2025)
"There are many agent startups doing this." Many startups ship agent orchestration and workflow tooling. They sell software. This is consulting-plus-software that takes cash and equity because it is an in-the-box solution requiring out-of-box consultation per business. The differentiator is trusted-companion delivery, relationships that compress the sales cycle, and a running reference implementation that is the demonstration. MEASURED
"A big consultancy plus ChatGPT already does this." Large consultancies pair advisory with frontier models competently. That engagement delivers a report and leaves. This leaves a running, gamified operating system behind, with provenance on the work and autonomy earned rep by rep behind a human gate. The deliverable is an adopted system, not slideware.

The through-line (say it the same way every time)

The technology baseline is conceded everywhere. Capabilities do not equal adoption. The moat is organizational psychology, gamification, and the trusted-companion relationship that carries a company across the transition and leaves it ahead.
