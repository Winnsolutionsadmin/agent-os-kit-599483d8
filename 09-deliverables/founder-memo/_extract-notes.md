### E. The 6/19 founding vision (Fieldy-distilled, numbered, all first-person Jarred lines pulled verbatim by the original distillation)
Source file: `00-call-notes/2026-06-19-fieldy-vision-distilled.md` (flagged in its own header as: attendee list is Jarred + unknown-1, "Carter" does NOT appear on tape despite the filename era — 95% confidence it's the right meeting by content match; this predates the 7/8 three-way call by three weeks and is Jarred's solo articulation before Nick/Carter joined).

1. Core thesis: "Communication gaps between employees should be solved, and people should be spending their time being most creative." (meetings are for creativity; agents grab the output and triage it)
2. End-state interface, "one window of consequence": a single zone-screen showing *"what is it that you're working on"* — OKR-style — with the agent layer surfacing decisions. "Not a notification feed. Not 17 terminal windows."
3. Triage as the operating layer: "An agent essentially managing the triage layer." The routine "choose the recommended option ~7/10 times" choices get absorbed; the human sees only high-leverage forks.
4. On existing tools, explicitly rejected: "none of them are right" (of Cursor/Claude/Perplexity computer UIs), including his own Cursor+terminals setup, which only works "when I'm wanting to really hand-hold a particular flow."
5. **The named want (verbatim, strong first-person):** *"This is what I want to cream. I want a personal agent. I haven't built my personal agent yet, and I still need to. A formal personal agent for just me and my wife, and it has access to my home and that kind of thing."* (Claude Code is a stand-in, not the real thing, in his own framing.)
6. Productivity/incentive model: "If I have an employee that has done 10 times the work of another in 2 hours, give them 6 more hours off in their day." — this is the earliest form of the "buy time back" adoption mechanism, pre-dating the 7/8 gamification talk by three weeks.
7. Universality requirement: "It's all about stylizing it in a way that it applies towards any person in your organization... 2-to-5x output for non-devs, even more for devs."
8. CEO framing (co-created in dialogue, Jarred agreed rather than stated solo): "The future job of a CEO is what you just described" — operating the org through the triage/agent layer.
9. What it explicitly is NOT: "Not another notification system. Not a chatbot. Not a Claude Code wrapper."

Use item 5 and item 6 as the strongest "10-year horizon, origin of the whole thing" material for the memo — item 5 in particular is the rawest, most personal line in the entire corpus (wanting a personal agent for himself and his wife, admitting he hasn't built it yet).
