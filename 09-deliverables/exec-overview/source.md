## Why now

**The capability is arriving; the adoption is not.** Anthropic and its peers can build the underlying agent capability. We concede that plainly: it is the market premise, not our fear. What no lab has claimed is the layer on top, the organizational psychology that gets an entire company to actually use the thing. That layer is open.

**The real blocker is fear, not technology.** 94 percent of knowledge workers already use AI at work, yet in a controlled experiment a peer who disclosed using it was rated 10x lazier and 24 points less likely to be chosen for a high-visibility project, at identical output (SURVEY, Atlassian 2026). Employees hide the exact behavior an adoption-dependent product needs them to show. Reward-not-replace is the answer to that, and it is strategy before it is ethics.

**The field is wide open.** 62 percent of organizations are experimenting with agents; roughly 23 percent have scaled even one system into production, and full agentic operation is rarer still (SURVEY, McKinsey Nov 2025). The first org to cross that gap in a vertical takes the lead in it, which is why the model takes equity, not cash alone.

## The seven pillars, one line each

1. **The front door (the face).** One calm surface, a conversation pane beside a workspace pane, where any request becomes an answer, an idea, a task, a workflow, or a build-it-now.
2. **The OKR engine (the brain).** A North Star decomposed into drivers, key results, and weighted agent-run subtasks, so every run scores against a stated objective.
3. **Gamification (the adoption engine).** Points, tiers, streaks, and leagues that reward people for using AI, decoupled from compensation on day one. Reward, do not replace.
4. **Meeting capture (the senses).** Transcripts become CRM entries, action items, decisions, and memory, so the work decided in a meeting gets built while you are still in the room.
5. **Client relationships (the channel, and part of the moat).** Relationships compress the sales cycle, delivery speed compounds client data, and the founder's own running estate is a packaged, demonstrable proof asset.
6. **The skill marketplace (the network effect).** Skills and workflows are the commodity; a 296-skill personal registry with a nightly self-improver is the proof of pattern (MEASURED). A long-term differentiator, not a pilot claim.
7. **Attribution (the trust layer).** Proof-of-origination for ideas and work. This is the least-built pillar today and sits on the roadmap; it is named here as designed, not running.

## How autonomy works: the human gate

The system does the work; the human holds the gate. Every agent output arrives as a triage card with three choices, so nothing that touches money, an outside party, or anything irreversible moves without a person deciding.

**`[ Approve ]`  `[ Edit ]`  `[ Skip ]`**

An advisor earns the right to act on a decision class on its own only after its recommendation has matched the human's actual choice at least 90 percent of the time across at least 20 reps. Autonomy is earned, never assumed, and the human can always take the gate back.
