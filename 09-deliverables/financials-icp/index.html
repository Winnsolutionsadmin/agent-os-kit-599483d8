[ 2 ] Buyer ROI model · explicit formulas

Executive hours alone cannot carry it. Seven dimensions do.

The ROI accrues to the executive’s own operating capacity, not to headcount cost. Every input is an ASSUMPTION with a stated range; the structure is the deliverable, the magnitudes are the negotiation. Nothing is dollarized except at the buyer’s own agreed rate.

The seven dimensions, by formula
DimensionFormulaKey inputs (ASSUMPTION, range)
D1 Workflow cycle time(baseline cycle time per instance − pilot cycle time) × instances per period × value of that timecycle-time reduction 20 to 60% ASSUMPTION; time value at buyer’s rate
D2 Decision latency(baseline request-to-decision elapsed − pilot elapsed) × decisions per period × cost of delay per unit timelatency reduction 30 to 70% ASSUMPTION; cost-of-delay buyer-stated
D3 Rework(baseline rework rate − pilot rework rate) × units per period × cost per reworkrework-rate delta 10 to 40% ASSUMPTION

D6 Adoptionweekly active participation as a share of the cohort; a durability gate, not a dollar line: it multiplies whether D1 to D5 persistadoption 50 to 70% weekly-active ASSUMPTION
D7 Capacity redeployment(executive + team hours recovered) × value of the higher-value work it is redeployed into; NOT headcount removed3 to 6 hrs/wk exec recovered ASSUMPTION (analogue MEASURED n=1)

Why hours alone cannot carry it: hours recovered (part of D7, dollarized at the buyer’s rate) is the most legible line and the easiest to dismiss as soft. A defensible case reads across the set: a workflow that runs faster (D1), decides sooner (D2), with less rework (D3) and fewer escaped defects (D4), where commitments stop slipping (D5), the behavior sticks (D6), and recovered capacity goes to work that matters (D7). The contracted pair is D7 plus D5, because both have measured internal analogues and both are auditable by the buyer’s own records. No baseline, no ROI claim.

The downside branch must survive a failed pilot.

If the pilot fails to convert, the company still stands, for reasons independent of the pilot: the strategic check is sized as runway to the month-6 milestone with contingency (section 9), not as a bet on one conversion; the founder’s existing consulting book already generates services revenue independent of the product (the $25,000/mo precedent among active engagements, MEASURED); full founder cost is already in the model, so there is no hidden capacity cliff; and a failed pilot still produces a baseline, a costed engagement, and lessons that sharpen the next offer. Survival, not growth, is the downside metric.

[ 8 ] The day-91 conversion trigger

What turns the pilot into an annual contract.

An evidence threshold, not a sales close. The pilot converts at day 91 only when the day-90 gate clears on buyer-verifiable evidence.

All of the following must hold at the day-90 gate

  1. Sustained capacity recovery (D7): executive hours recovered holding across 4+ consecutive weeks, in the buyer’s own records.
  2. Missed-commitment debt cleared (D5): overdue count at or near zero and staying there.
  3. Graduation status honest: at least one decision class at or past 90% agreement over 20+ reps with written sign-off, or a documented honest reason. Money, irreversible, and external classes do not graduate within the pilot.
  4. The buyer’s own baseline confirms the ROI on the contracted metric pair (D7 + D5).
  5. Zero ungated money, irreversible, or external actions across the entire pilot. A single ungated action fails the gate regardless of everything else.
  6. CEO personal ownership sustained through weekly check-ins.

Day 91: the buyer converts to the companion retainer (annual, anchored on the $25,000/mo precedent, number founder-set), grants a defined referenceability scope, and opens the domino-2 sponsorship conversation.

[ 10 ] Strategic-check sizing method · method, not a number

A runway-to-milestone calculation, never a valuation of the vision.

The strategic check is the smaller check of the canonical ask. Its size is a founder-and-counsel decision; no band appears in any external material. The method is the deliverable.

strategic check  =  six-month milestone budget

Full founder cost is inside the six-month budget. A converting pilot shrinks the ask, which is the honest direction. The two-transaction discipline holds: the strategic check (investment) and the pilot (services) are separate decisions on separate evidence; a yes to one is never a yes to both.
