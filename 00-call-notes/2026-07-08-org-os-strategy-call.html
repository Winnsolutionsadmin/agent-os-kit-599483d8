Agentic Org OS / RSI for Orgs — the operator, the engineer, the game designer strategy call

Date: 2026-07-08T18:21:02Z | Attendees: the operator, the engineering collaborator, the game-design collaborator

Source: recall | Category: product-strategy / fundraising-prep

Summary

Three-way ideation call between the operator, the engineering collaborator, and the game-design collaborator on building an Agent Operating System / recursively-self-improving productivity platform for businesses. Core thesis: compress time-from-idea-to-execution via a unified, simple UI (Tinder-like swipe cards, Slack-like text interface) that manages employees via AI, ties top-level org OKRs to weighted subtasks, and rewards adoption. Discussed good/better/best packaging (C-level chief of staff → engineering dept → whole-org brain), a skills marketplace (possibly tokenized), sovereignty/open-source angle vs Anthropic capturing customer IP, OAuth rotation economics, and the engineer's case study (team of 4 producing 16x baseline vs control of 18 at 2.5x). the operator plans to pitch Alan (Upexi) for $10-50M seed. Consensus: sell C-levels first, engineering second (they drive demand across the org), consulting + software black-box hybrid model with equity + cash from clients.

Decisions

  • the operator will draft an early deck and concept notes; shared doc for async contribution
  • Pitch Alan (Upexi) end of this week or next week for potential $10M-$50M raise
  • Product will be sold as good/better/best: C-level chief of staff → engineering dept → whole-org agentic OS
  • Take cash + equity from client engagements, not cash alone (first-mover monopolizes vertical)
  • Lead with C-level sale, then engineering team, then cascade to rest of org (engineers drive demand)
  • Working name candidate: 'the prior engineering framework' (the engineer's AI-suggested name for recursive self-improvement for orgs)
  • Async collaboration via Telegram going forward; reconvene after the operator meets Alan
