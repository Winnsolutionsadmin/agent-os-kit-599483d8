Gamification · key statements & agreements

Key statements and agreements from the gamification design sessions. These set the adoption mechanics that make the product land, and the guardrails that keep the mechanics honest.

Key statements

  • Adoption runs on mechanics that reward people for using AI instead of threatening them with replacement.
  • An output-to-token ratio is a fair engagement signal only when it is normalized: leagues, minimum token thresholds, and anti-abuse caps are required, not optional.
  • Skill-based real-money mechanics collapse their own player base; luck and status mechanics retain one. Enterprise adoption should borrow status, streaks, and league dynamics, never wagering.
  • Time-weighted leaderboards are exploitable and must be designed against; every scoring loop needs an abuse review before it ships.

Agreements

  • Social scoring ties to objective-weighted output, with league normalization by role.
  • Fake-outlier capture is a launch requirement for any leaderboard.
  • Rewards celebrate adoption publicly; penalties do not exist in the mechanic set.
