# Capacity plan: the 7/9 to 7/27 collision window

**Wave-2 meeting-pack artifact.** The single-operator founder capacity plan for the window where client delivery, the venture deliverables, and the investor meeting all run at once. It answers the one question a sole founder has to answer honestly: what gets done by whom, and what moves when two things want the same hours.

**Authored:** 2026-07-09. **Governing constraints (fixed, not negotiable):**
- **Client delivery is revenue, and it is protected.** The client experience may never degrade. Client-protected hours are the floor of every day; venture work takes what is left, never the reverse.
- **The demo may never fail live.** The protect-the-demo rule (section 4) exists to guarantee this.
- **One operator.** The founder is the binding constraint. Everything that can move to agents does; everything that cannot is scheduled around the two protected commitments above.

**The window's fixed anchors:**

| Date | Anchor |
|---|---|
| 7/9 to 7/19 | Client Sprint 1 (launch window) |
| 7/12 | Client Sprint 1 **T-0 launch** (protected, single-focus) |

| 7/17 to 7/18 | **Investor meeting** |
| 7/20 to 7/27 | Client Sprint 2 build toward the 7/27 milestone |

## 2. The two genuinely overloaded days, and what moves

Every other day is heavy but single-focus or absorbable. Two days have multiple founder-only demands competing for the same hours. These are the days the plan actively re-shapes.

### 7/11 (Sat): 3-way collaboration doc + interim facts memo, on the eve of T-0

Both deliverables are founder-only, both fall the day before the client T-0 launch (7/12), which is itself fully protected. Blank-page authoring of two documents on 7/11 does not fit.

**What moves:**
1. **Start both on 7/9 to 7/10, not 7/11.** The 3-way doc builds on the existing W2 shared-doc skeleton and the contribution-equity options file; Carter's matrix.build intake (7/9) and Recurro doc (7/10) feed it. By 7/11 it is assembly and sign-off, not a first draft.
2. **The interim facts memo is a tight founder-only one-pager** (founder, contributor, governance, and IP facts, with no line implying an executed agreement that is not signed). Draft it 7/10, sign it 7/11.
3. **Agents pre-assemble** the 3-way scaffold and the facts-memo draft; the founder edits and signs rather than writes from zero.
4. **Protect 7/11 evening for venture.** Client load that day is pre-T-0 checks, not the launch, so the evening block is real if it is defended.

### 7/15 (Wed): deck final lock + methodology memo intake + rehearsal pressure, mid-Sprint-1

The deck locks, the methodology memo arrives, demo rehearsal wants to start, and Sprint 1 is still live. This is the worst convergence in the window, and it sits two days before the meeting.

**What moves:**
1. **Decouple the methodology memo from the deck lock.** The throughput case study appears nowhere in the deck; the memo is a named pilot deliverable, so its 7/15 intake informs internal review but does not gate the deck. The deck ships on schedule whether or not the memo is final.
2. **Front-load deck authoring to 7/13 to 7/14** (buffer days with monitoring-only client load), so 7/15 is a lock-and-polish day, not an authoring day.
3. **Agents own render, stylizing, and lineage QA.** The founder spends 7/15 only on the six flagged judgment spots and the final read.
4. **Rehearsals begin 7/16, not 7/15.** 7/15 stays deck-lock day; the demo work starts the day after the lock, which is exactly what the protect-the-demo rule requires.

## 3. Delegated to agents vs founder-only

**Founder-only (cannot be delegated):**
- All client Sprint 1 delivery, the T-0 launch (7/12), and the close (7/19). Revenue, protected, first call on every hour.
- The investor meeting itself (7/17 to 7/18), and driving the live demo and its rehearsals.
- The six flagged founder-judgment spots on the concept deck.
- Interim facts memo sign-off; entity formation and IP-assignment decisions.
- Any money, irreversible, or external action (product law: a human holds the final gate).

**Runs on agents (parallel, does not draw founder hours):**
- Deck render, stylizing, lineage-chip QA; the competitive one-pager and the other meeting-pack supporting artifacts.
- Reading-room external-layer assembly; positioning language; achievement-ledger and ClickUp sync.
- Off-critical-path research loops (unit economics, data governance).
- The 3-way doc scaffold and the facts-memo draft assembly (founder edits and signs).
- Follow-up email drafting from a pre-written template (founder personalizes and sends).
- Sprint 2 build scaffolding where it is agent-appropriate; demo backup packet assembly (the spike session owns it; the founder verifies before 7/17).

The split is the survival mechanism: the founder's irreplaceable hours go to revenue, the meeting, and the judgment calls; everything mechanical runs on agents so those hours are not spent twice.

---

## 4. Protect-the-demo rule: no new scope after 7/15

**The rule:** after 7/15, the demo is frozen. The only demo work permitted from 7/16 onward is rehearsal and backup-packet verification. Zero new features, zero new data sources, zero new surfaces, zero "wouldn't it be better if."

- The demo freezes 7/16 with **five clean rehearsals** completed before the meeting.
- Any idea that arrives after 7/15 goes to a post-meeting backlog, never into the demo.
- The demo is **read-only by design**; the honest fallback is a timestamped recording plus a printed script, both verified before 7/17.
- The rehearsal target is one reproducible trace shown the same way five times, not five different impressive things shown once.

**Why the rule is absolute:** the worst possible moment for a live failure is in the room, in front of the one known investor the whole window is built around. A frozen, rehearsed, read-only trace beats a fragile last-minute addition every time. The rule protects the two constraints this plan cannot violate: the client experience never degrades, and the demo never fails live. The schedule is built so neither has to.

*End. This plan operationalizes the single-operator-capacity blind spot named in the counsel review and the roadmap's dependency spine; the demo mechanics live with the spike session.*
