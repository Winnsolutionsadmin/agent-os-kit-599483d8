# Competitive positioning: the trusted companion through the AI transition

*External-safe. Every figure is tagged to its source. The product is intentionally unnamed; the working descriptor is an agent operating system for organizations.*

**The frame in one line:** the frontier labs build the capability. Someone still has to walk each company through the transition, company by company, and hand them a head start. That companion role is the position, and it is the one the players below do not occupy.

## The premise: concede the capability, compete on the adoption

The capability is not in question. Anthropic, OpenAI, and the other frontier labs can build agent capability, and that is the premise of this market, not a threat to it. The strongest evidence is who just entered: in May 2026 Anthropic stood up a roughly $1.5B enterprise AI services company with Blackstone, Hellman & Friedman, and Goldman Sachs, and OpenAI followed within a week with a roughly $4B deployment arm (VENDOR, both labs' own announcements). Capability arriving is exactly what creates the demand.

What none of them sells is the adoption. Raw capability lands top-down as infrastructure. A company's people do not adopt infrastructure because it exists; they adopt what captures how they already work, scores that work against the company's own objectives, and rewards them for using it rather than threatening them with replacement. The gap between capability shipped and capability adopted is the whole opportunity, and it is a human and organizational gap, not a model gap.

## The category map

Four categories are moving on this market. Each is real, well-run, and deserves respect. The honest read is not that any of them is weak; it is that each one is optimized for something adjacent to the companion position, for reasons rooted in its own business model.

| Category | Who | Honest one-line respect | What the position they hold does not cover |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Incumbent suites** | Microsoft, Salesforce, ServiceNow, Google, Atlassian, ClickUp | They own distribution and are shipping real agent orchestration at scale today (Salesforce reports roughly 29,000 Agentforce deals and about $800M ARR; Microsoft Agent 365 reached general availability May 1, 2026, VENDOR) | Scoring a team against its own objectives and rewarding adoption is an organizational, HR-adjacent posture that sits outside a seat-and-consumption motion; one major suite retired its objective-tracking product at the end of 2025 with no replacement (VENDOR), leaving that lane thin |
| **Agent platforms and startups** | Sierra, Dust, Glean, Bond, and the builder frameworks | Well-funded, fast, and genuinely category-defining (Sierra at roughly a $15.8B valuation; Dust reporting about 240% net revenue retention; Glean at about $300M ARR, VENDOR) | Each has gone deep on one surface (customer-facing agents, multiplayer collaboration, enterprise search, a read-only chief-of-staff view); none assembles objective-scoring, rewarded adoption, and origination attribution into one product sold as a companion |
| **Consultancies and lab services arms** | EY, Accenture, and the lab-backed services ventures | Real delivery capacity and enterprise trust at a scale no small team can match (EY reports deploying agentic AI to more than 300,000 professionals running roughly 50,000 agents, VENDOR) | They integrate other vendors' products or embed engineers; they do not bring their own objective-scoring and adoption product, and the delivery relationship, not a companion product, is the asset |
| **Meeting-intelligence tools** | Otter, Fireflies, Fathom, Gong, Granola | Excellent capture with enormous reach (Otter reports more than 35M users, VENDOR); this layer is genuinely solved | Capture is where they stop; turning captured work into scored objectives, rewarded adoption, and attributed origination is downstream of them, and that downstream is where the durable value sits |

## The whitespace

Across the whole field, one combination is unoccupied: **work scored against a company's own objectives, adoption rewarded rather than mandated, and origination attributed, all delivered as a trusted companion rather than a tool to install.** Each piece exists somewhere. No incumbent, platform, consultancy, or capture tool assembles all of it in one product sold through a companion relationship.

Stated honestly, and reconciled with the concession above: two of the pieces this position needs, a calm conversational front door and meeting capture, are already commodity. We concede that plainly and build on them; they are plumbing, not the moat. The moat is the combination the market does not assemble, and the structural reason it persists is that the incumbents monetize seats, actions, and consumption, so scoring a team against its objectives and rewarding its people is work their business model does not pay them to do.

**Built versus designed, stated plainly:** of the three differentiating pieces, objective-scoring runs today in the founder's own reference implementation (CORPUS, a reference implementation operated by one person, not an installed customer). The rewarded-adoption mechanics and the origination-attribution layer are designed and on the build path, named here as designed, not claimed as running.

This whitespace is real, and it is not permanent. Well-funded, adjacent players are already circling pieces of it. The position is defended not by being first but by compounding a proprietary feedback loop inside a real company's own data before a better-capitalized copier assembles the same combination.

## Why now

The transition is happening to every company whether it plans for it or not, and it happens one company at a time. Capability is arriving faster than any organization can absorb it, the lab services ventures above are the proof that even the model builders see the adoption gap as the market, and the cost of doing nothing is not zero: it is shadow AI spreading unmanaged and a head start handed to whoever moves first in your vertical. The companion that captures how a company actually runs, scores that work against its objectives, and earns autonomy step by step is the safe way across that gap, and it is the position no one above is built to hold.

**The line:** the labs build the capability. Someone still has to walk each company through the transition. We are that companion, and we hand them a head start.
