Sweep — interface layer

Prepared 2026-07-08 as part of the product-concept consolidation sweep (Track 1.2). Synthesizes six reader passes plus a handful of direct re-reads of primary documents (INTENT.md files, vision-brief.md, the Allan Marshall deck thread, and the claudecode-bar Recall docs) done to resolve contradictions between readers.

Headline

There is no single confirmed app or product called "FleetView," "TeamClaude GUI," "gui-relay," or "mission control." All four names were checked against the available corpora and none is a literal, adopted product name; each is either a colloquial label, an unrelated tool, or a name never actually used by the operator for this concept. What actually exists is a family of related, independently-built efforts converging on the same interaction thesis: a calm, conversational, two-pane "one front door" surface (persistent conversation on one side, modular agent-driven workspace on the other) that absorbs routine agent noise and surfaces only the facts, questions, and decisions a human needs. The most mature, currently-active implementation is claudecode-bar ("the bar"), a native macOS menu-bar HUD the operator built for himself as a solo operator. The most direct candidate for a client/executive-facing product is executive-agent-gui, born 2026-07-03 and explicitly linked at birth to a gamification/enterprise-pilot concept ("the the game designer track") that appears to be the same thread as today's (2026-07-08) the game-design collaborator and the engineering collaborator conversation. The interaction pattern itself, two-pane conversation-plus-workspace with Approve/Edit/Skip triage, is called mima, a personal concept the operator wrote up in February 2026, and it was the template for the Allan Marshall teaching deck's "front door" product mockup built the day before this sweep (2026-07-07).

What exists / was built

claudecode-bar ("the bar" / "Command Deck"). A real, shipping, always-on macOS menu-bar app (Swift AppKit + WebKit) rendering hud.html plus panel views (console.html, dashboard.html, recall.html, browser-live.html, hook-timeline.html). Built for the operator alone, as the operator of a one-human/many-agent fleet. INTENT.md was authored 2026-06-21 from recovered history; the app itself traces to a 2026-06-20 ask for "a clean managed dashboard of hooks/workflows/components (icon-based, clumped by likeness, click to logs + efficiency)." It serves the "godmode:gm-ecosystem" driver under an "Ultimate agentic god mode" North Star tracked in the-sage/goals/goals.json. Predecessor/sibling clawdia-bar (mini side) is retired and superseded by claudecode-bar. As of a 2026-07-02 ledger snapshot it had 74 commits and was the most actively developed candidate in the whole sweep.

executive-agent-gui. A second, distinct, newer native macOS (SwiftUI) app, scaffolded and graduated out of the miscellaneous inbox on 2026-07-03. Built for a different audience: "a smart but non-technical executive... just needs key facts and decisions to navigate" (the operator's verbatim framing), not the operator-the-operator. Mechanism: passively tails a live Claude Code session's JSONL transcript (read-only), a triage gate classifies each turn as surface-or-suppress, a Sonnet-class "executive translator" (Haiku explicitly rejected) rewrites surfaced turns into plain executive language, and AskUserQuestion moments render as native, tappable decision cards whose answers relay back into the live session. As of the latest record found, it is scaffolded with INTENT.md, decision docs, and a vision-brief, but lessons.md/progress.md read "none yet"; no confirmed build activity after 2026-07-03 was found in this pass.

mima. Not a from-scratch app in this context, but a UI/interaction pattern: a persistent, unchanging conversation on the left and a modular, context-driven workspace on the right, with drafted cross-platform actions (via OAuth) going through Approve/Edit/Skip triage. Documented in a source doc dated February 2026 ("mima_story", the operator's own description: "the operator's Feb 2026 personal-AI concept"), stored on Google Drive under "_Unsorted Root/Docs" alongside mima_story_v2/v3.docx; a PDF (mima_story_v3.pdf) was generated from it on 2026-07-07 and used directly as the source reference for the Allan Marshall deck's product mockup slides.

The Allan Marshall deck's "front door" slide. The single most concrete, most recently built visual mockup of the GUI concept found anywhere in this sweep. Built inside a teaching deck for Allan Marshall (allan-ai-deck/deck.html, iterated v1 through v13 across 2026-07-06 and 2026-07-07, backed by a Google Slides deck). Version 10 (2026-07-07) introduced an "AI Front-Door" intake mockup combining mima with an intake/promotion model sourced from a document called gui_intake_concept_summary.pdf: an intent bar, a "Continue-a-thread" list, a "Start New" panel with a classification chip, and promotion buttons (Ask questions / Save as idea / Convert to task / Save as workflow / Build it now). Version 12 rebuilt the slide as a fuller two-pane app mockup citing the mima_story_v3 UI reference directly: chat thread on the left, a "Looks-like" classification bar plus a branch card plus a vendor-email Approve/Edit/Skip triage card on the right. Version 13 (latest known) retitled the whole deck to a teaching frame ("The state of AI") and rebuilt the three-modes slide as three miniature UI window demos. Three standalone working demo pages were also built and published: treasury.html, fleet.html, and triage.html (the last explicitly tagged "mima pattern"), live at a public but unlisted GitHub Pages URL.

Key features

Consolidated across all of the above (feature belongs to the artifact named in parentheses):

  • Two-pane "one front door" layout: persistent conversation on one side, modular workspace on the other (mima; echoed in executive-agent-gui's briefing view and the Allan deck mockups).
  • Conversational intent bar that classifies an incoming ask and offers promotion actions: Ask questions / Save as idea / Convert to task / Save as workflow / Build it now (Allan deck v10, sourced from gui_intake_concept_summary.pdf).
  • Approve/Edit/Skip (or "stage-only") triage gesture on agent-drafted actions, independently implemented at least four times: mima's cross-platform OAuth draft triage, claudecode-bar's Approvals panel (2-step confirm for irreversible items, built 2026-06-30), command-center's stage-only action buttons, and the Allan deck's vendor-email/pay-row demo cards.
  • Native decision cards for AskUserQuestion-style moments, answers relay back into the live agent session (executive-agent-gui's core mechanic, modeled on the existing twofa-relay publish-wait-result pattern).
  • Passive, read-only transcript tailing with an LLM "translator" layer that suppresses routine noise and surfaces only facts/questions/decisions, explicitly Sonnet-class or better, never Haiku (executive-agent-gui).
  • Always-on glanceable status HUD: curated tiles (about 11) plus a full expandable estate dashboard (about 103 components), including session need-you counts and chips, a validation/health tally with "scoped-red" failure signaling (one bad component never turns the whole tally red), one-click autonomous repair (Diagnose, Fix, Verify, Harden), a cost/savings odometer for the shared OAuth account pool, a goal-alignment "RUN" recap tile, a meeting-recording status tile, and a memory/knowledge-effectiveness "Brain" tile (claudecode-bar).
  • Click-to-debug: clicking a failing tile spawns a draggable, goal-briefed debug session instead of requiring a manual terminal dive (claudecode-bar).
  • Session recall and resume by topic or branch, with a three-rung resume ladder: active sessions focus instantly, dormant sessions checkout-relaunch then focus, gone sessions print a manual resume one-liner so nothing is silently unrecoverable (claudecode-bar's Recall panel).
  • Gated secret/2FA approval: tap a hardware key to release a secret without the HUD ever seeing its value (claudecode-bar).
  • Review-queue pipeline (pending, in_review, changes_requested, approved) with a roadmap step toward driving the live agent session directly from a reviewed item (anchorage-review-console, Anchorage-specific).
  • Live particle-flow visualization of memory-store reads and writes tied to the requesting agent (memory-viz, gbrain-specific precedent).
  • Concrete worked examples of the pattern applied to a specific business, built as real running demos rather than mockups: an agentic treasury desk (KPIs including SOL-per-share, four agent lanes, decision cards, a sparkline), a 20-vehicle multi-state fleet-registration agent with a dollar-amount approval card and a "your time YTD" counter, and an iMessage-style thread showing a full day of one-word approvals ending in a "14 actions, 3 approvals, 41 seconds of attention" receipt (Allan Marshall deck v6 through v9 demo pages, built against Allan's real Upexi pain points, published at the deck's GitHub Pages URL).

Must-haves

These recur, independently, across more than one of the built or planned efforts, which is why they read as product law rather than one team's preference:

  • Never auto-act on money, irreversible, or external/parent-facing actions without a human gate. Stated identically in claudecode-bar's INTENT.md, executive-agent-gui's INTENT.md, and command-center's CLAUDE.md.
  • Fail open to surfacing: anything touching decisions, money, deadlines, or irreversible actions must never be suppressed by a triage or translation layer (executive-agent-gui).
  • Never fabricate: a translation or summary layer must restate only what is grounded in the transcript and mark uncertainty rather than smooth it over (executive-agent-gui).
  • Quality floor for any LLM-facing translation or distillation step: Sonnet-class or better. Haiku was explicitly rejected for executive-agent-gui's translator ("this needs to be a quality output and feel like a conversation").
  • Calm by default: only the one tile or signal that truly needs the human pulses; false positives ("crying wolf") are treated as product failures to be actively fought, not tolerated (claudecode-bar).
  • Scope discipline: one failing component must never turn a whole tally or board red (claudecode-bar's "scope failure, never globalize" rule, recovered from a repeated real correction).
  • Whole-element click targets: a tile or card that only responds on a tiny sub-region reads as broken (claudecode-bar).
  • Native app delivery for both the operator-built GUIs to date: claudecode-bar is Swift/AppKit/WebKit, executive-agent-gui is native macOS/SwiftUI. Web, Electron, and an Omnara-layer approach were explicitly rejected for both, at least for V1.
  • Read-only access to live agent transcripts wherever a GUI observes a session; the only permitted write-back is an explicit, confirmed, exactly-once answer or action relay.
  • Verification as part of the product, not a side process: because a wrongly-suppressed message is silent and indistinguishable from nothing happening, both major GUIs carry an explicit requirement for liveness/health indicators and an auditable trail, and claudecode-bar's own build discipline requires screenshotting the real native panel before calling any UI feature done, not just checking served HTML.

Value propositions

  • "2-to-5x output for non-devs," stated as applying to any role in an organization (2026-06-19 vision meeting, source of the executive-agent-gui thesis).
  • A cited case study from today's (2026-07-08) gamification call: a 4-person team running at 16x output versus an 18-person control team at 2.5x (per the goal context's meeting notes, not independently re-derived in this pass).
  • "The future job of a CEO" is framed as operating the organization through the triage layer, positioning the GUI as necessary infrastructure for an agent-run organization, not a nice-to-have dashboard.
  • Suppression and triage as the core value: fewer, better messages instead of a firehose, letting one human operate many agents with "the least possible attention per agent" (claudecode-bar's own job statement), or letting an executive direct agent work "without ever seeing code, tool calls, or agent noise" (executive-agent-gui's job statement).
  • A client-experience layer for enterprise consulting engagements: the one stated constraint from an enterprise-pilot client CEO, as of 2026-07-03, was "client experience," and a calm executive briefing surface is positioned as exactly that layer, making the GUI a candidate component of an agentic-consulting pitch rather than purely internal tooling.
  • Cost and time reclaimed: claudecode-bar's odometer tile quantifies dollars saved versus API price for the shared account pool; the Allan deck's demo pages use a "your time YTD" counter as a felt-value metric; the deck's broader argument (loop compression citing a 40 percent BCG randomized-trial result, a 13-to-1 round reduction at Anthropic, and a 24-to-5 day cycle-time reduction at Rakuten) frames the underlying economic case for the whole category.
  • Adoption mechanism: gamification (leaderboards, progress, recognition), proposed in today's call per the goal context, as the mechanism to drive workforce-wide AI adoption against a small current base rate (verified figures used elsewhere in this sweep's Allan deck: McKinsey 62 percent of organizations experimenting with agents but only about 6 percent classed as high performers, Gartner 17 percent already deployed, MIT 5 percent realized value capture).

  • This consolidation swarm itself: the immediate short-term goal across the whole initiative is the comprehensive product concept overview this dossier is one input to (Track 2 in the running task list), followed by execution-path and competitive/risk research (Track 3) and a verified foundational data repository (Track 4).

Long-term:

  • "One window of consequence": a single calm, conversational surface through which a decision-maker, potentially any role in an organization per the "applies to any person" framing, directs and consumes agent work with no code or tool-call noise, with the triage layer eventually absorbing most routine choices so only high-leverage decisions ever reach a human. This end-state is explicitly in tension with executive-agent-gui's current never-auto-answer guardrail (see Grey Areas).
  • An org-wide, OKR-tied agent/organization operating system: today's (2026-07-08) framing per the goal context describes compressing idea-to-execution through one simple UI, tying organizational OKRs to weighted agent-run subtasks, sold as a consulting-plus-software hybrid for cash and equity, packaged good/better/best from a C-suite chief-of-staff tool up to a whole-organization operating system.
  • claudecode-bar's own stated umbrella goal: serve the "godmode:gm-ecosystem" driver under an "Ultimate agentic god mode" North Star, meaning this HUD is explicit infrastructure toward a larger self-improving-agent-ecosystem goal, not an end in itself.
  • command-center's shelved design already imagined a long-term graduated-autonomy path in which an advisor earns the right to auto-act on a class of decision only after proving at least 90 percent agreement with the operator's actual choices over at least 20 repetitions, the clearest candidate long-term trust/calibration model found in this sweep, currently unbuilt.
