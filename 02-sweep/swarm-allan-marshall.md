- Three immutable research packets were filed to source-check every stat used in the deck (adoption-stats, productivity-evidence, agentic-shift), each individual claim tagged MEASURED / SURVEY / VENDOR / [unverified] with primary source URLs (McKinsey, Ramp AI Index, Stanford AI Index, Gallup, BCG, NBER, METR, Anthropic Economic Index, MIT).

## Key features (of the State of AI deck / its embedded product mock)

- Three-mode ladder: "Ask" (question a chat model) -> "Build this for me" (delegate) -> "Build this with me" (agentic partnership, ~10x claimed). Presented in v13 as three literal UI window mockups rather than text.
- "Barrier collapse" thesis: the old idea-to-launch chain (idea -> advisors -> designers -> lawyers -> builders) collapses to idea -> AI -> launch; visualized as a fan-out/fan-in parallel-agent-lanes diagram in later versions.
- "This deck is the demonstration" meta-proof slide: the deck itself took roughly 3 days of thought plus about 6-8 hours of build and about 10 hours of AI-run research, contrasted against traditional production timelines.
- The closing "front door" product mock (final iteration, slide 15 of 16): a two-pane pattern taken directly from the mima UI story (persistent conversation on the left, modular Approve/Edit/Skip workspace on the right). Left pane shows a chat thread turning a plain-English instruction into a classified, branching task; right pane shows a classification bar, a branch-decision card, a vendor-email triage card, and a one-tap pay row. Footer line: "every approve, edit, and skip teaches it your voice," an explicit reference to a personalization/ingestion loop.
- Three (later four) fully working, clickable live demo pages standing in as feature proof rather than screenshots: an agentic treasury dashboard, a 20-vehicle multi-state registration agent, a mima-pattern inbox triage page, and a full simulated day run entirely by iMessage.
- An explicit "honest math" discipline running through the whole deck: no claimed stat is allowed past a MEASURED/SURVEY/VENDOR/[unverified] source tag; the headline 10x claim is reframed as loop deletion (e.g., "13 rounds to 1" or "24 days to 5"), not raw task-time compression, and an initial 88 percent adoption headline was deliberately killed as overstretched for Allan's real subject matter.

## Must-haves

- **Belief-indexed authoring before building.** The single hardest-learned rule from this project: v2 was rejected severity-high specifically because it was built without consulting Allan's belief profile or Jarred's own belief index, both of which existed on disk already. This produced a standing process rule (see Artifacts) requiring belief-profile lookup before authoring any named-recipient deliverable.
- **No hype, no forward-looking promises, no crypto slang, no "I built" framing, never position Allan as an "AI personality."** These are explicit, written red lines in `talk-track.md`, derived directly from Allan's profile (anti-hype, shareholder-first, compliance-conscious operator).
- **Chart-plus-minimal-narrative, no em dashes, infographic-dense (never plain stat tiles).** A stated visual/stylistic requirement tied to Allan's own preferences, enforced across every rebuild after v2.
- **Numbers must be sourced and honest, even if that means a smaller number.** Demonstrated concretely by the 88%-to-1% adoption-stat walkback and by the loop-deletion reframing of the "10x" claim.
- **No pitch, no naming of the business concept, no naming of Nick or Carter, in the teaching deck.** Jarred's explicit, repeated instruction: the deck stays education-only. The underlying incentivized-adoption-platform concept (an earlier framing of what has since become the Agent OS idea) was allowed to shape the deck's arc but was never to be named or pitched directly.
- **For the (not yet built) Agent OS pitch to Allan specifically:** treat it as a wholly separate deliverable from the State of AI deck, due around 2026-07-15, ahead of a meeting targeted for roughly 2026-07-17 to 18. Do not reuse the State of AI deck's "no pitch" framing for this one; this one is explicitly meant to solicit investment.
