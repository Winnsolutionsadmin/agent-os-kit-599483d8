Sweep — gamification mechanics

Research swarm: product-sweep-2026-07-08. Topic scope: XP, quests, scores, streaks, leaderboards, and productivity measurement of agents and humans, as it relates to the operator's consolidated product concept (agent-orchestration GUI, mima, Allan Marshall, Anchorage, gamification, Fieldy/Recall).

Headline

There is no single gamification product. There are three separate, non-integrated efforts that share one underlying thesis (measure and reward output, not hours or activity). Two of the three are real, running code that the operator built for himself. The third, the one that matters most for the consolidation goal, is an enterprise-facing pitch called "Agent Operating System," working name candidate "the prior engineering framework," developed with the game-design collaborator and the engineering collaborator. As of today (2026-07-08) it exists only as meeting notes and quotes: no repository, deck, or code artifact for it was found anywhere in the local Projects tree. The underlying thesis itself traces back at least to a 2026-06-19 conversation, seven days before the the game-design collaborator relationship even began.

The three efforts, in brief:

  1. A live internal achievement engine on mini (achievement-notify.py and friends) that has been scoring the operator's own agent/ops fleet with points, tiers, and a daily streak since 2026-04-19, currently on a 23-day streak with a running total score of 5027 as of today.
  2. A built personal productivity layer split across two repos, the-sage (prompt-coaching engine, houses the OKR/goals data model) and claudecode-bar (the menu-bar display), with three distinct gamified tiles: a quiz with points and streaks ("the sage," built 2026-06-21), an OKR goal-alignment score (0.0 to 1.0, built 2026-06-18 to 2026-06-19), and a task-suggestion "Quest" tile (built 2026-06-25/26) that is not itself scored.
  3. Agent OS / the prior engineering framework, the client-facing pitch: gamifying enterprise AI adoption via leagues, token-burn-to-output scoring, anti-abuse caps, and a rewards economy, sold in good/better/best tiers, with Anchorage floated as an equity pilot and Allan Marshall (Upexi) as the target first investor for a $10 to $50 million seed.

No source read anywhere states that these three are meant to converge into one technical system. The overlap in vocabulary and dates is this dossier's own observation, not a stated plan.

What exists / was built

1. mini's achievement engine (OCPlatform), live and running today

This is real, continuously operating code, not a concept. It gamifies the operator's own business and agent-ops event stream, not any client-facing product.

  • Built 2026-04-19. The launch event was itself logged as an achievement: {"title": "Achievement System Online", "tier": "legendary", "points": 25, "message": "Ready to gamify. Enjoy the ding."} (mini:~/.openclaw/workspace/data/achievements.jsonl, first line).
  • Core scripts: achievement-notify.py (fires macOS notifications with points/tier/streak), achievement-catalog.json (the scoring model), achievement-status.py (CLI dashboard), achievement-relay-server.py and achievement-relay-client.py (cross-device relay from mini to the MacBook over Tailscale, bearer-token authenticated).
  • Scoring model: points = rarity (1 to 5) times impact (1 to 5). Tiers are common, notable, rare, legendary. Roughly 25 catalog events span CRM actions, meetings processed, skills shipped, ops incidents resolved, viral content, revenue milestones, and dedicated streak tiers (7, 30, and a one-time-only 100-day "Century Streak" worth 50 points).
  • As of the 2026-07-08 snapshot (~/.openclaw/workspace/data/achievement-state.json): total_score 5027, daily_score 126, weekly_score 368, current_streak 23, longest_streak 23 (i.e. never broken), last_event_date 2026-07-08.
  • It gamifies agent/ops productivity specifically, not just business events: shipping an agent improvement (10 pts), a cron self-heal (9 pts), and resolving an incident (rare tier, 16 pts) all score.
  • Common-tier events are deliberately silent (log-only, no notification), so the human-facing layer only surfaces notable-or-better events. This is the same "don't surface routine noise" principle articulated in the 2026-06-19 source vision (see Timeline).
  • It fired live against today's actual the game-design collaborator and the engineering collaborator call, independent confirmation of the meeting's timing: achievements.jsonl logs "message": "the operator, the game-design collaborator & the engineering collaborator - A - 6 actions, 3 contacts" with total_after: 4955 at 18:25:33Z, followed by "event": "crm_contact_added", "message": "Added the engineering collaborator to CRM" at 18:29:24Z.
  • It is a single running tally for the whole operation, not a per-person or per-agent competitive leaderboard. The only literal "leaderboard" found anywhere in the mini corpus (viralwatch-authors.py, virality-scorer.py) ranks external social-media authors for content-virality monitoring, unrelated to internal productivity.

2. Personal productivity/prompting scorer: the-sage + claudecode-bar

This is also real, built, running code, evolving over about three weeks (2026-06-18 through 2026-06-30), covering the operator's own behavior rather than any client's.

The-sage (/Users/jarredwinn/Projects/the-sage) is fundamentally a "personal educator / prompt-coach": it parses the operator's own Claude Code session transcripts and teaches/tests him on lessons and unused skills (README.md: "Personal educator/prompt-coach: ingests the operator's session prompts, coaches better prompting + triggering, learns how he learns, asks calibration questions"). It also happens to house the OKR/goals data model that a separate scoring feature in claudecode-bar reads:

  • goals/goals.json (schema 2, updated 2026-06-22) defines three Objectives, each with Drivers mapped to specific repos and keywords: "Grow Growing Mindfully," "Build Winn.solutions advisory," and a third objective whose name is a direct the operator quote from 2026-06-18: "Innovate and develop the ultimate agentic god mode which solves for the gaps in human inefficiency from a personal and corporate perspective."
  • goals/goals.md documents the scoring philosophy explicitly and self-critically: "activity-alignment: the run-summary card shows the share of a run's commits that map to a stated objective... it goes up when you're busy, so it's the vanity axis... outcome-movement (the real one): did the KRs actually move toward a number. Blocked on (a) KRs defined [still TODO as of 2026-06-22] and (b) a data source for outcomes (ClickUp, not readable from this Mac yet)."

claudecode-bar (the menu-bar HUD app) is the display surface, with three distinct gamification-flavored tiles that are functionally separate from each other:

  • "The sage" quiz tile (hud.html lines ~3735-3998; built via commits 14dbc50 and b567c9a, both 2026-06-21, then 8fd73f8 "sage card v2: fix flicker/badge, reject-to-learn, scoring, varied quizzes, zen chime," also 2026-06-21). Three quiz formats rotate (recall, multiple-choice, define), testing the operator on his own logged AI-usage lessons. Points and streaks are tracked in localStorage: sc.answered++; if(ok){ sc.correct++; sc.streak++; sc.best=Math.max(sc.best,sc.streak); sc.points += 10 + Math.min(sc.streak-1,5)*2; } else { sc.streak=0; } (verified directly against the live file, line 3746). A WebAudio "zen chime" plays a different tone for a correct versus wrong answer, and a "reject" button lets the operator flag an invalid quiz item so the sage stops asking it.
  • "Goals" tile, trophy icon (built via commit 640e0e7, 2026-06-19, "trophy goal-tree editor (North Stars to Drivers)"). Shows the North Star to Driver OKR tree, editable in-app, plus a literal activity-alignment score (0.0 to 1.0, 0.7 considered good) computed by run-summary-gen.py's score_alignment() function (commits 847b3f8 and ff8510b, both 2026-06-19): the share of a day's git commits whose repo maps to a stated North Star or Driver, with an explicit "unaligned" orphan bucket as its own signal. The first run scored 99.3 percent activity-alignment (275 of 277 commits).
  • "Quest" tile (built via commits 5d5da3e, b4fb6f9, 28a4596, dated 2026-06-25 and 2026-06-26). quest-suggest.py writes hourly task suggestions, pulled from messages and email, to ~/.claude/quest-suggestions.json. Clicking "Start session" spawns or resumes an Omnara session pre-briefed on the clicked suggestion. This uses quest vocabulary as a prioritized task queue. It is not itself XP-scored: no points or streak attach to completing a quest.
  • A shared small design vocabulary ties the three together without unifying their mechanics: a trophy emoji for the goals tile, a fire emoji streak indicator on the sage score chip, and the WebAudio zen chime.

A separate, purely personal, non-gamified "quest tile" prototype is also referenced once in miscellaneous's own lesson log (memory-bank/lessons.md, entry dated 2026-06-28) and in memory-bank/activeContext.md's 2026-07-07 entry, describing a tile that at one point replaced a "brain tile" on the bar and was restored via a separate dream-cycle repo commit (e62c03c). This appears to be the same claudecode-bar Quest tile described above, referenced from a different angle (a housekeeping/lesson note about a stale background collector, brain-activity-collect.py, left running after the tile swap) rather than a second, different quest feature.

Key features

Tagged by status: BUILT (running code, verified), PITCHED (meeting-stage concept, no code found), or PRECEDENT (an existing, separately productized system the operator mentioned as prior art).

  • BUILT, internal ops: points/tier/streak achievement engine on mini scoring the agent and ops fleet itself. Rarity times impact scoring, roughly 25-event catalog, cross-device notification relay, common-tier events silenced.
  • BUILT, personal: quiz-based points and streak coaching (the sage tile in claudecode-bar). Three rotating quiz formats, WebAudio audio feedback, a reject-to-learn correction loop.
  • BUILT, personal: OKR goal-alignment scorer (0.0 to 1.0) mapping git commits to North Star and Driver objectives, with an explicit, documented self-critique distinguishing the "vanity" activity-alignment axis from the not-yet-built "real" outcome-movement axis.
  • BUILT, personal: quest-suggestion tile surfacing prioritized tasks from messages and email, one-click session spawn. Uses quest vocabulary as a queue label; carries no XP or scoring of its own.
  • PITCHED, enterprise: token-consumption-vs-output scoring, the core proposed mechanic for measuring AI adoption per employee or team.
  • PITCHED, enterprise: league and tier system gated by usage thresholds plus consistency, with anti-abuse daily caps and outlier detection.
  • PITCHED, enterprise: role-adjusted scoring, so meeting-heavy or non-technical roles are not structurally penalized.
  • PITCHED, enterprise: rewards economy (half-days off, +20 to 30 percent comp, a broader "points economy") plus a cosmetics monetization layer (buyable terminal skins).
  • PITCHED, enterprise: a case-study claim of 4-person-team-at-16x-baseline versus an 18-person control at 2.5x, credited to the engineering collaborator, cited as the pitch's headline productivity number.
  • PITCHED, enterprise: good/better/best tiered go-to-market: an individual chief-of-staff seat, then an engineering department, then a whole org.
  • PITCHED, enterprise: consulting-plus-software commercial model taking cash and equity rather than pure SaaS pricing.
  • PRECEDENT, unrelated to the prior engineering framework: "belief-indexing / operator-brand gamification," an older system already productized with Andrew King: nightly belief journaling, distilled into theses, scored, and rolled into future-vision and leader maps, for operator personal-brand output. Must-haves

Synthesized from what sources repeatedly treat as non-negotiable, not a single source's checklist:

  • Measure output and value, not hours or raw activity. A generalizable lesson logged elsewhere in this project (memory-bank/lessons.md, entry dated 2026-06-21, from an unrelated points-redemption bug) states this almost as a design law: "point cost and list order are not proxies for value," explicitly flagged as applying to "any rewards/loyalty redemption flow." Directly relevant if the prior engineering framework ever gates real rewards on a points balance.
  • Anti-gaming mechanics from day one: attainment caps, outlier detection, and role-adjustment. Named explicitly by the game-design collaborator on the origin call, informed by his own experience of a past engagement metric being gamed by a hedge fund via an exploitable leaderboard.
  • Domain-agnostic from day one. The founding 2026-06-19 vision is explicit that the interface "must apply towards any person in your organization," not be built for engineers and ported later.
  • The human sees only high-leverage decisions. The triage or agent layer must absorb the routine choices; the interface must not degrade into a notification feed or "17 terminal windows."
  • Preserve client experience. Named as Anchorage's CEO's sole stated constraint on any pilot in the 2026-06-30 digest.
  • A real outcome-data source is required before any score can be more than a vanity metric. This is true today in the operator's own dogfood system (blocked on ClickUp reachability and undefined KR targets) and would presumably be equally true for any enterprise version of the same idea.
