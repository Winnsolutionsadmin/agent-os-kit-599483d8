Prepared 2026-07-08 as part of the product-concept consolidation sweep. ## Headline

mima is Jarred's own Feb 2026 personal-AI concept: an always-on AI agent and liaison that reads a user's messages across every platform, drafts responses in the user's voice using relationship and calendar context, and asks for a single Approve/Edit/Deny (later Skip) decision before anything sends. The canonical spec document ("mima_story_v3") lays out a three-phase roadmap from a $49/month consumer product to a relationship-graph platform to an on-chain "Proof of Human" protocol, and the same file continues into a second, distinctly-branded pitch called "Project X" that generalizes mima's human-approval concept into cryptographic trust infrastructure for the wider AI agent economy. mima surfaced in scattered form through the spring (April 2026 conversations with a CAA agent and an Ethereum Foundation contact, a June business-taxonomy placement) and then, in July 2026, its core interaction pattern (two-pane UI, Approve/Edit/Skip triage cards) was pulled out and rebuilt as the closing product mock in a client-education deck for Allan Marshall, the CEO of Upexi. That last fact matters beyond this thread: Allan Marshall/Upexi is very likely the same "Alan (Upexi)" the broader swarm's goal context names as the target first pilot/investor for a separate, same-week gamification and agent-OS pitch, though no source in this topic's own corpus states that link directly.

## Key features

Everything below is drawn directly from mima_story_v3 unless noted otherwise.

mima is described as "your always-on AI agent that learns you, your voice, your style, your goals, your relationships, and has your back. It operates as your liaison between you and your digital world, combating the decision fatigue we encounter every single day." Its stated core mechanic is that it "compresses the timeline between trigger, thought, and response": mima reads an incoming message, checks relationship and calendar context, drafts a response in the user's voice, and surfaces one decision. The document's worked example is a mom-texts-about-dinner scenario in which mima notices the user has "pushed back on her last three dinner requests," checks the calendar, and proposes a message with buttons for Approve, Edit, or Deny; once approved, "mima sends the message," puts a temporary hold on the family calendar, and confirms once the reply comes back. The document's own framing is "you spent ten seconds and one tap."

The UI is specified as a two-pane layout: "The left side of the screen is your ongoing conversation with mima. It never changes. The right side is modular, evolving based on what you're doing." Onboarding is conversational rather than form-based: the user opens mima.ai in a browser and mima asks "Hi there, I'm mima. What's your name?", building "a working model of you" within five to ten minutes. Step two is a no-send demo mode across the user's actual connected-platform scenarios (an urgent investor email, a mom text, a reply to a contact's post on X), each carrying Approve/Edit/Skip buttons. Step three has the user pick their top three platforms from a grid that includes Gmail, Slack, iMessage, X, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, and Telegram. Step four connects each platform via standard OAuth, with the explicit user-facing claim "mima never stores your password."

Notably, the demo-mode screenshot in the spec document shows mima replying to a Balaji-Srinivasan-style X post about decentralized identity with: "This is exactly right, and it's what we're building. Cryptographic proof of human involvement in AI communications, not just identity verification. The approval flow creates the attestation layer." This line ties mima's own demo content directly to the vocabulary of the appended Project X pitch (see Grey areas, below).

Daily use is framed as proactive: a morning briefing, communication drafts across every connected platform (every approval, edit, or denial "feeds the learning loop"), task and follow-up management, and once a day a "sharpening question" such as "I noticed you responded to Michael more directly than usual yesterday. What was your reasoning?" The stated mechanism behind ongoing improvement is called the "ingestion loop": "Every message mima drafts, you review. Every review is signal... This is not a preferences page. This is not prompt engineering... The friction of reviewing isn't a bug, it's the entire mechanism." The dataset of "what AI proposed vs. what the human actually wanted" is named the "decision diff," described as "proprietary training signal that grows with every user, every day."

Three founding tenets are given equal billing: Privacy (zero-knowledge proofs and cryptographic hashing "obfuscate any connection between your identity and the data flowing through the model"; "your context lives in the cloud, your identity stays with you"), Humanity ("mima's blockchain layer records on-chain which behaviors were human-originated, human-approved, or agent-autonomous. Proof of Human will be a core component of the next technological revolution"), and Value ("AI companies should not be monetizing you. You should be monetizing AI," via passive income from leased skills/templates and zero-knowledge-verified expertise exchange).

The stated three-phase roadmap: Phase 1, Product ($49 per month, web and SMS, every major LLM used under the hood); Phase 2, Platform (a relationship knowledge graph, context-aware communication, "social intelligence," and relationship-health nudges such as "You haven't reached out to Marcus in 3 weeks"); Phase 3, Protocol (on-chain migration of the user's personality model and relationship graph, owned by the user's own cryptographic keys, an agent-to-agent marketplace, and skill-leasing passive income).

The appended Project X section (pages 16 to 18 of the same document) generalizes mima's human-origination tiering into standalone cryptographic infrastructure: "Proof of Endorsement Receipts" (a hash of the draft, the final output, and the policy used, plus a guardian signature, timestamp, and channel metadata, with the explicit design note that the chain "stores commitments and signatures, never raw content"); a tiered-authorization model reusing mima's own Human Originated / Human Authorized / Agent Autonomous labels; a sybil-resistant reputation model (wallet age, unique counterparties, value transacted, human-verification ratio, staking and slashing); and a zero-knowledge privacy layer for proving policy compliance without revealing policy contents. This section is framed against four named, real incidents (the 2024 Air Canada chatbot liability case, 2023 to 2024 lawyer AI-hallucinated court citations, AI-driven crypto-influencer impersonation, and a $25 million-plus deepfake-CEO wire fraud), each paired with what the proposed system "would have" prevented.

## Must-haves

The spec document itself repeatedly frames certain elements as non-negotiable rather than optional, and Jarred's own edits when the pattern was reused elsewhere confirm which parts he treats as load-bearing.

The three tenets are literally headlined "THREE THINGS THAT MUST BE PROTECTED": privacy via zero-knowledge proofs and hashing, humanity via the on-chain Proof of Human record, and value via user-side monetization of the AI rather than the reverse. The human-in-the-loop approval step is explicitly called the mechanism, not a limitation: "The friction of reviewing isn't a bug, it's the entire mechanism. People don't want to remove themselves from communication entirely. They want assistance, not replacement." Relationship and context awareness has to exist from the first session; the explicit foil is generic LLMs that are "the equivalent of hiring a genius assistant who has amnesia every morning," so the five-to-ten-minute onboarding is required to produce a genuine "working model," not a blank slate. Security is stated as a user-facing trust claim, not just an engineering detail: OAuth-only connections, end-to-end encrypted credentials, and the explicit promise that mima never stores a password.

A cross-project must-have surfaced in how Jarred handled the pattern's reuse: when it appeared in the Allan Marshall deck (v10, 2026-07-07), he cut the literal mima branding and slide as "ugly, not relevant" while explicitly directing that the underlying Approve/Edit/Skip pattern be kept and merged into the deck's front-door concept. That is direct evidence that, for Jarred, the interaction pattern itself is the must-have intellectual property; the mima brand name is disposable in external-facing artifacts, at least as of this instance.

## Value propositions

For the end user of the mima product itself (Phases 1 and 2): it replaces a $40,000-to-$80,000-per-year human assistant, available to "fewer than 1% of people," with a $49-per-month AI liaison, described in the document as "less than 0.1% the cost of a human assistant." It collapses what the document calls "the biggest productivity killer in the universe," lag-time of response, into "ten seconds and one tap." Its differentiation from generic LLM assistants is framed entirely around persistent relationship and context memory, with the pointed comparison that ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are each "the equivalent of hiring a genius assistant who has amnesia every morning." Five named personas anchor the value case: the drowning executive or founder, the small business owner with no support staff, the working parent in schedule chaos, the creator or freelancer maintaining an inconsistent cross-platform voice, and the retiree navigating healthcare systems while staying connected to family.

For mima as a platform (Phase 2 and into Phase 3): relationship health and social intelligence become a product surface in their own right, separate from simple task completion, and the monetization direction is explicitly inverted from the industry norm: "AI companies should not be monetizing you. You should be monetizing AI," with users eventually owning and able to lease their own personality model, relationship graph, and skills once these move on-chain.

For the Project X trust-layer extension: it targets a named market gap, stated plainly as "Everyone is building agents. No one is building the trust layer that makes agent interactions verifiable. This is like building the internet without HTTPS: it works until fraud scales, then the whole system loses credibility." That gap is anchored to four cited, real incidents rather than hypotheticals, and to a stated market size of $250 billion-plus for the AI agents market by 2030 and $50 billion-plus for the trust and settlement layer specifically, citing Bloomberg Intelligence, Gartner, and IDC AI spending forecasts as sources (as footnoted in the document itself; this dossier has not independently verified those figures against the cited firms' actual publications).

For Jarred's own consulting and business-development use of the pattern, which is the only place this swarm found the concept actually deployed rather than only pitched: the Approve/Edit/Skip triage-card pattern and the two-pane "front door" layout have already proven reusable as a concrete, buildable client-facing product mock inside the Allan Marshall deck, and as a published, working demo page (triage.html) rather than only a slide. mima is therefore not just a standalone personal pitch document; it is a working design pattern Jarred has already redeployed at least once, within about five months of writing the original spec, inside a paid relationship-building context.

## Goals

Short-term, as stated in the spec document itself (February 2026): ship Phase 1, the $49-per-month product, web and SMS from day one, every major LLM available underneath; land the onboarding flow exactly as scripted, five to ten minutes to a working model, no-send demo mode, top-three platform selection, secure OAuth connect; and begin accumulating the "decision diff" dataset from a user's first session, since the document frames this as the mechanism by which the product "gets better, not stale."

Short-term, based on what has actually happened since February, according to this corpus: the interaction pattern was pulled into the Allan Marshall deck and iterated through thirteen versions across 2026-07-06 and 2026-07-07, producing both a slide-level product mock and a separately published, working demo page. Separately, mima (specifically under a "Trust Origin" framing) was given a formal, if small and thesis-stage, place in Jarred's own business-pillar restructuring plan as of 2026-06-29.

Long-term, as stated in the spec document: Phase 2 builds the relationship knowledge graph, context-aware communication, and relationship-health intelligence layer; Phase 3 migrates the user's personality model and relationship graph on-chain, under the user's own keys, opening an agent-to-agent marketplace and skill-leasing passive income. The document frames this progression as close to inevitable rather than optional, arguing each phase's users will themselves demand the next ("Phase 1 gives users an AI that knows them deeply... they demand relationship context... Phase 2 gives users an AI that knows them and their world... they demand ownership... Phase 3 gives users cryptographic ownership... they demand expansion"). The Project X arm of the same document sets its own long-term goal as building the generalized cryptographic trust layer for the whole AI agent economy, against the stated $50 billion-plus TAM.

Long-term, as an inference from the broader swarm context rather than a sourced fact: if the Allan Marshall-equals-Alan-of-Upexi identity match noted above holds, the mima design pattern may be on a path toward informing, or riding alongside, a funded joint venture involving Upexi money, whether or not that venture is ever branded "mima." This is this dossier's own connective inference, offered explicitly as unconfirmed, and should be checked with Jarred before it is treated as a plan.
