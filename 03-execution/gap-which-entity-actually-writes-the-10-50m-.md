## Headline

The prior dossier (`fundraising-path.md`) correctly named this as the single biggest open question and recommended a personal-or-syndicate-led priced round over a corporate check. New numbers pulled today make that recommendation considerably stronger, and change the shape of the answer in one important way: Allan Marshall's own personal balance sheet, not just Upexi's, now looks too thin to personally fund $10-50M alone. Upexi's stock has fallen roughly 90 to 96 percent from its 2025 high, so Marshall's own 4.5 percent stake, worth about $13.2M in the figure the prior dossier cited, is worth closer to $2.7M to $3.5M today by direct recomputation against current price and share count (sources below). Upexi's own cash on hand was $3.48M as of March 31, 2026, against a market cap of about $61M and a stock price trading around 30 percent below the value of its own Solana treasury. None of the three named options, Marshall personally, a syndicate he leads, or Upexi's corporate balance sheet, can be ruled in or out from public information alone, but the hard numbers now argue for a specific most-likely shape: a Marshall-assembled syndicate (most plausibly anchored again by Hivemind Capital Partners, which has done three deals with Upexi in fourteen months) with Marshall himself contributing a comparatively small personal check inside it, exactly the structure he used for Upexi's own $100M Solana raise in April 2025. Upexi's corporate balance sheet writing this check directly is now the least financially plausible and least fiduciarily defensible of the three paths, not just the most legally encumbered one. This still has to be confirmed with Marshall directly; nothing here replaces that conversation.

## Findings

### 1. Upexi's own balance sheet cannot plausibly fund $10-50M today, on its own numbers

Pulled directly from Upexi's fiscal 2026 SEC filings and its own investor relations releases:

- **Cash and cash equivalents: $3,482,847** as of March 31, 2026 (fiscal Q3 2026 10-Q), up from $2,975,150 at the June 30, 2025 fiscal year end. Cash was as low as $1.6M at December 31, 2025 before a $36M convertible note plus a $7.4M registered direct offering pushed it briefly to about $9.7M in early February 2026 (source: 10-Q filings via SEC EDGAR, reported via MarketScreener/GlobeNewswire coverage).
- **Total current assets: about $121.8M**, almost entirely digital assets (SOL) at fair value of about $114.9M, not cash.
- **Fiscal Q3 2026 net loss: $109.3M**, driven by $92.3M of unrealized losses on digital assets (SEC 8-K/10-Q, covered by StockTitan).
- **Market capitalization: about $61M** as of the first week of July 2026 (Robinhood ~$61.15M, TradingView ~$61.83M, Google Finance ~$61.49M on ~70.26M shares outstanding), with the stock at $0.87 to $0.91. The 52-week range is $0.54 (February 24, 2026 low) to $10.62-$22.57 depending on source (high hit around July 2025), meaning the stock has fallen somewhere between 90 and 96 percent from its peak.
- **mNAV (market cap versus the value of its own Solana treasury): about 0.7x**, i.e. the stock trades roughly 30 percent below the liquidation value of its own SOL holdings (Enterprise Value / crypto NAV, standard digital-asset-treasury metric). For comparison, Upexi traded at 1.2x mNAV in July 2025 and 0.9x in August 2025; it has compressed steadily since. Upexi holds roughly 2.17M to 2.4M SOL depending on the exact date pulled, worth somewhere in the $163M to $206M range as SOL's price moves (CoinGecko treasury tracker, The Block treasury tracker, company press releases).
- Upexi's own capital-markets activity in 2026 to date has been entirely defensive: a $36M convertible note secured by locked Solana (January 2026, with Hivemind Capital Partners), a $7.4M registered direct offering (February 2026), and a roughly $19.5M debt-for-equity swap completed via a June 2026 securities purchase agreement that retired principal on an existing convertible note by issuing 12,242,300 new shares at about $1.60. All three of these were about shoring up Upexi's own balance sheet and reducing its own debt load after the SOL price decline, not about originating new outbound capital deployments.

**What this means for the gap:** even the low end of the ask, $10M, is roughly three times Upexi's entire cash position and about 16 percent of its market cap; the high end, $50M, is about 14 times cash on hand and roughly 82 percent of market cap. A corporate check at anything near the stated range would require either liquidating a material slice of the SOL treasury (directly contradicting the company's own stated long-term treasury thesis, the thing its stock price is entirely built around) or raising fresh, dedicated capital first, which reintroduces its own timeline and Nasdaq-approval risk (see Finding 6). This is a much harder, more concrete constraint than the general "equity-method accounting is a risk" framing in the prior dossier; it says Upexi functionally does not have $10-50M of deployable, uncommitted capital sitting around today, full stop, on its own most recent quarterly filing.

### 2. Marshall's personal liquid stake has cratered since the number in the prior dossier was pulled

The prior dossier cited Simply Wall St's figure that Marshall's 4.5 percent Upexi stake was worth roughly $13.21M, and flagged it as "likely priced off a different date... treat as approximate, not load-bearing." That caution turns out to be the important finding. Recomputing against today's numbers:

- GuruFocus (pulled February 2026, based on Form 4 filings): Marshall holds about 4,046,261 shares of Upexi, giving an "at least $2 million" net worth estimate at that date's price (near the 52-week low of $0.54, hit February 24, 2026).
- At today's price (~$0.87-0.88, per the market-cap search above), that same share count is worth approximately **$3.52M** (4,046,261 x $0.87).
- Cross-checked against the 4.5 percent-of-market-cap framing: 4.5 percent of a ~$61M market cap is about **$2.75M**.
- Both recomputations land in the same $2.7M-$3.5M band, a decline of roughly 73 to 79 percent from the $13.21M figure the prior dossier used, consistent with the stock's overall 90-96 percent collapse from its 2025 peak (the earlier figure was almost certainly pulled near a materially higher share price).
- One source (Benzinga) lists an "estimated net worth of $1.17 billion" for Marshall; this is very likely a data-aggregation artifact from historical share/price snapshots across Segmentz/XPO-era holdings decades ago and should not be treated as a real current figure. Flagging it here only so it does not get mistakenly cited later as if it were live data.
- Marshall's own recent trading is instructive: he bought 100,000 shares at $2.07 on December 15, 2025, 50,000 more at $2.07 on December 16, and 50,000 more at $1.80 on December 23, 2025 (all-buy, no sells, per his Form 4 history; 15 transactions over 5 years, all purchases). At today's ~$0.87-0.88 price, even those five-month-old buys are underwater by more than half.
- No public source shows any liquid asset for Marshall outside Upexi stock and one Florida holding company (Finding 3, below). Historical share ownership at his prior company (XPO, now XPO Inc.) is real but decades-stale and not a source of current liquidity; he exited that business relationship long before XPO grew into its current form (see Finding 5).

**What this means for the gap:** the reading that "Allan Marshall personally" is the simplest, most literal answer to the question "who is Alan/Allan Marshall for $10-50M" turns out, on the numbers, to be the least financially plausible of the three named options as a solo check, not the most obvious one. His own liquid, Upexi-linked net worth is meaningfully below even the low end of the stated range. If a personal check is part of the answer, it is very likely a modest signaling check inside a larger syndicate, not the bulk of the capital, mirroring his own April 2025 pattern exactly (see Finding 4).

### 3. MFA Holdings Corp: a real, existing, active personal vehicle, if this is meant to be personal

Marshall is listed as President and Director of **MFA Holdings Corp**, a Florida corporation (document number P14000010792, FEI/EIN 46-4751215, filed February 4, 2014, status ACTIVE, last event a reinstatement filed December 23, 2020, principal address 2510 Merchant Ave, Odessa, FL 33556, per Florida's Sunbiz corporate registry). This is not a hypothetical structure; it has already appeared as a real, named party in actual SEC-referenced deal documents involving Upexi. Specifically, MFA Holdings Corp appears as a 45 percent party (alongside Nutra Products LLC at 45 percent) in a stock purchase agreement with Upexi, and separately as a named party in an equity interest purchase agreement between Amplifyir Inc. and Upexi dated August 31, 2023 (the deal in which Upexi sold its Interactive Offers LLC subsidiary to Amplifyir for $1.25M plus a 2.5 percent revenue-share for two years, part of Upexi's 2022-2023 divestiture of its earlier CBD/consumer-brand-aggregation-era assets, back when the company traded under its prior "Grove, Inc." identity). This confirms two things directly relevant to the gap: (a) Marshall does have a working, real, currently-active personal legal entity he has used before to hold equity positions in Upexi-adjacent transactions, so if the answer is "personal," MFA Holdings Corp, not a brand-new ad hoc LLC, is the most likely existing vehicle; and (b) there is real, if dated, precedent for Marshall structuring deals where his personal entity and Upexi's corporate entity are both parties to the same transaction, side by side but legally distinct, which is close to the "NewCo plus personal investor, Upexi kept off the cap table" structure the prior dossier already recommends.

### 4. The syndicate bench exists and is deepening, but it is a crypto-thesis bench, not an enterprise-AI bench

Marshall's most-demonstrated capital-raising pattern (per the prior dossier and confirmed again here) is lead-plus-syndicate: the April 2025 $100M Solana PIPE was anchored by GSR, with Maelstrom Capital, Delta Blockchain Fund, Delphi Ventures, Hivemind Ventures/Hivemind Capital Partners, Borderless Capital, White Star Capital, Big Brain, Anagram, Morgan Creek Capital Management, and named crypto angels all participating alongside a personal Marshall check (size undisclosed in any source found). New this pass: that relationship with Hivemind Capital Partners specifically has kept deepening well past the original PIPE:

- **April 2025:** Hivemind participates in the original $100M PIPE.
- **January 2026:** Upexi signs a $36M securities purchase agreement with Hivemind Capital Partners for a convertible note secured by locked Solana, growing the treasury past 2.4M SOL.
- **June 30, 2026:** Upexi announces a partnership with Blueprint, described as "the institutional staking and digital-asset infrastructure affiliate of Hivemind Capital Partners," to stake a portion of the SOL treasury. Hivemind is described as managing roughly $1B in client assets "across six strategies spanning venture, private equity, liquid tokens, and public equity."

Three separate transactions in fourteen months make Hivemind Capital Partners the single most concretely-proven "Marshall could plausibly call this investor for a fast check" relationship in the public record, more so than GSR (which anchored the original PIPE but has no subsequent disclosed Upexi transaction found in this pass). Hivemind's stated multi-strategy mandate (venture and private equity alongside liquid tokens) means it is not purely a crypto-beta vehicle, which softens, but does not resolve, the concern that this bench was assembled and has stayed engaged specifically for Solana-treasury exposure, a different risk thesis than backing a concept-stage, non-crypto, consulting-plus-software venture with no revenue of its own. Two additional considerations cut against assuming this bench shows up eagerly and immediately for a second Marshall-adjacent raise: first, the original PIPE was priced at $2.28/share; at today's ~$0.87-0.88, that specific tranche is down roughly 60 percent, meaning Upexi's own existing syndicate is currently sitting on a real paper loss from backing Marshall once already this cycle; second, none of these investors have any documented history in enterprise software, agentic AI, or consulting-services businesses, so recruiting them (or anyone else) into a fundamentally different asset class is a real, unquantified execution risk layered on top of the accounting and governance risks the prior dossier already flagged, and one that plausibly does not close by 7/17-18 even if Marshall is personally willing.

### 5. XPO Logistics "founder" framing: a calibration note, not an identity problem

The estate's own records (confirmed identity, per `swarm-allan-marshall.md`: "CONFIRMED via prep doc + tracking thread") correctly establish that Alan/Allan Marshall is the Upexi CEO; that is not in question. But the specific "founder of XPO Logistics, grown to a $17B company" framing repeated across the estate (sourced from Upexi's own executive-bio language) is Marshall's own company bio's favorable simplification, worth calibrating before using it to infer his personal wealth capacity. The actual lineage, cross-checked against XPO's own investor-relations history and independent reporting: Express-1 Expedited Solutions was started in 1989 by Michael Welch and Keith Avery; Marshall's company, Segmentz, Inc. (founded November 2000), acquired Express-1 in August 2004 and eventually took its name and ticker; Marshall raised more than $25M and built the business through five acquisitions, then exited that chapter around 2011. Bradley Jacobs then invested $150M in June 2011 for roughly 71 percent ownership of what was, at that point, a roughly $72M market-value company, renamed it XPO Logistics, and built it through 17 further acquisitions (2012-2015) including the $3B Con-way deal, taking its market cap to a peak of about $16.8B and later spinning off GXO ($7B, 2021) and RXO ($5B, 2022). In plain terms: Marshall built and grew the predecessor entity to roughly $72M in value and then was bought out of control by Jacobs, who did essentially all of the value creation that produced today's $17B-revenue company. This does not change the confirmed identity of who Allan Marshall is, and it is consistent with him being a genuine, successful serial operator (he also built and sold U.S. Transportation Services, Inc. in 1995-2000), but it means the implicit inference "he founded a $17B company, so he is personally very wealthy from that outcome" is not well supported; whatever Marshall's proceeds were from the 2011 buyout, they are a 15-year-old, likely modest-by-comparison liquidity event, not a current balance-sheet fact, and no source in this pass or the prior dossier quantifies them. Treat Marshall's demonstrated capacity as "proven serial operator and capital-raiser," not "proven nine or ten-figure personal balance sheet."

### 6. The related-party trigger is not hypothetical or future-only, it is already latent today

The prior dossier (`fundraising-path.md`) correctly flagged Nasdaq Rule 5630 and Item 404 as risks if a future Upexi-as-customer relationship with the new venture ever emerges. New finding this pass: the conflict is not purely prospective. Upexi's own Code of Business Conduct and Ethics (filed as an exhibit to its 10-K, applicable to all directors, officers, and employees) requires that "conflicts of interest must be handled in an ethical manner," meaning "fully disclosed and considered prior to being resolved," with the CFO or legal counsel handling all conflicts questions "including coordinating with the Audit Committee as necessary," and conflicts "permitted only after full disclosure has been made, the Company (or Audit Committee) has given prior written approval." Winn.solutions already has an active, ongoing ghostwriting-and-advisory engagement with Upexi/Marshall (confirmed directly in `org/objective-ledger.yaml`, status active, confidence high, per the local estate). That means the moment Marshall personally invests in Jarred's NewCo, or leads a syndicate into it, Upexi's own Code arguably already requires him to proactively disclose that relationship internally (to the CFO/legal counsel, coordinating with the Audit Committee as needed), independent of whether Upexi's corporate balance sheet is involved at all, because the counterparty (Jarred, via Winn.solutions) is simultaneously a paid Upexi vendor and the target of Marshall's personal investment. Item 404 of Regulation S-K and Nasdaq Rule 5630 (audit committee review of transactions requiring Item 404 disclosure) most clearly bite if Upexi itself remains a party to any transaction with Winn.solutions/NewCo going forward (which it already is, via the active advisory engagement) while a related person (Marshall) also holds a financial interest in the counterparty. The practically safer reading, pending actual counsel confirmation, is to treat this whole web (Marshall as Upexi CEO/director, Winn.solutions as an active paid Upexi vendor, Jarred Winn as NewCo founder, Marshall as a prospective NewCo investor) as related-party territory requiring proactive disclosure regardless of which of the three entities technically signs the NewCo check. This sharpens, rather than replaces, `venture-structure.md`'s existing recommendation (Finding 6 there) to disclose the dual role to Allan in writing before 7/17-18; it adds that Marshall likely has his own, separate, pre-existing obligation under Upexi's own Code to disclose the same facts upward inside Upexi, and that obligation exists today, not only if and when a future commercial relationship is signed.

### 7. If Upexi's corporate balance sheet is chosen anyway: the specific governance gauntlet, sized to today's numbers

Restating and sizing the prior dossier's structural risks against the hard numbers found this pass, for whoever ends up scoping the legal work:

- **Board composition:** five directors. Two are executive (Marshall as CEO/Chairman, Andrew J. Norstrud as CFO); three (Gene Salkind, Thomas Williams, Lawrence Dugan, the last elected at the June 15, 2026 annual meeting) are the likely independent bench. A transaction this size relative to Upexi's own balance sheet (Finding 1) would need real, substantive review by the three non-executive directors, not a rubber stamp, especially given Marshall's own personal interest in the outcome.
- **Audit Committee pre-clearance:** per Upexi's own Code (Finding 6) and its related-person-transaction policy (which ties disclosure to Item 404's threshold, the lesser of $120,000 or 1 percent of average total assets for a smaller reporting company; Upexi is both a "smaller reporting company" and an "emerging growth company," per its own SEC filings, and 1 percent of roughly $121.8M in total current assets is about $1.2M, so the operative floor is the flat $120,000 figure), any transaction between Upexi and a related person's counterparty at this size clears the disclosure threshold many times over.
- **Equity-method consolidation risk:** unchanged in kind from the prior dossier (ASC 323, roughly 20 percent ownership or board/information rights triggers a presumption of "significant influence"), but now landing on a company that just posted a $109.3M quarterly net loss; picking up a concept-stage venture's own likely losses on top of that is a materially worse optics and disclosure problem today than it would have been a year ago.
- **Nasdaq Rule 5635(d), the "20% Rule":** relevant only if Upexi needs to raise new, dedicated capital to fund an outbound check (a real possibility given Finding 1's cash numbers) via a private placement priced below the "Minimum Price" (the lower of the last closing price or the five-day average close) that, alone or combined with sales by officers/directors/substantial shareholders, equals 20 percent or more of shares or voting power outstanding. Given roughly 70M shares outstanding at ~$0.87-0.88, a raise anywhere near the top of the stated range, at anything like current prices, would very plausibly cross the 20 percent threshold and require a shareholder vote, which does not fit inside the 7/15-7/31 internal timeline referenced in the product overview. This is a new, concrete addition to the prior dossier's more general accounting/related-party risk list: even setting aside whether Upexi should invest at all, actually funding a corporate check at scale could independently require a shareholder meeting.
- **Strategic/optics problem, not just a legal one:** Upexi trades at roughly a 30 percent discount to the value of its own SOL treasury (Finding 1). The market is already discounting management's capital allocation. Announcing an unrelated $10-50M outbound bet on a concept-stage AI venture, backed by the CEO's own outside consultant, while the core Solana-treasury thesis trades below its own liquidation value, is the kind of move that plausibly widens the mNAV discount further rather than closing it, independent of any legal analysis. This is the strongest new argument in this pass for why the corporate-balance-sheet path is not just complicated but actively unlikely to be chosen by a board acting in the company's own interest right now.

## Options considered (revisited with a probability read)

Same three structural options the gap names, re-scored against this pass's hard numbers. These probabilities are a reasoned judgment call from the evidence above, not a measured fact; they exist to help sequence the legal work, not to replace asking Marshall directly.

1. **Allan Marshall personally, solo.** Financially the least plausible of the three at anything near the stated range: his own recomputed liquid Upexi-linked stake is about $2.7M-$3.5M today (Finding 2), well under even the $10M floor, with no other disclosed liquid asset besides MFA Holdings Corp (an active vehicle, but with no public evidence of its own independent balance sheet size). Rate this low probability as the entire answer; more plausible as a partial, signaling-sized personal check (in the low hundreds of thousands to low millions, based on his own demonstrated pattern) inside a larger structure.
2. **A syndicate Marshall leads or assembles.** The best-evidenced match to his own demonstrated behavior (the April 2025 PIPE) and to an active, deepening relationship (Hivemind Capital Partners, three transactions in fourteen months, explicit venture/private-equity mandate alongside its crypto strategies). The most likely single real-world shape of this deal if it closes anywhere near the stated range, with the important caveat that recruiting any of these investors into a non-crypto, concept-stage enterprise-software bet is unproven and could plausibly slip past the 7/17-31 internal timeline even if Marshall is fully willing.
3. **Upexi's corporate balance sheet directly.** Now the least likely and least defensible path on the hard numbers: $3.48M cash on hand against a $10-50M ask, a $61M market cap that a $50M check would consume 82 percent of, an existing 30 percent discount to the company's own treasury NAV that this move plausibly worsens, a board (three likely-independent directors) that would need to approve a related-party-adjacent transaction of this size against a company that just posted a $109.3M quarterly loss, and a real chance that funding it at all requires a shareholder vote under Nasdaq 5635(d). Keep this as a documented fallback only, exactly as the prior dossier already recommends, capped well under 20 percent ownership and without a board seat if Marshall insists on it, not as a primary planning assumption.

## Recommendation with reasoning

**Primary recommendation, updated from `fundraising-path.md` with today's numbers: plan the legal and diligence work around a Marshall-led syndicate (most plausibly anchored again by Hivemind Capital Partners or a similar existing Upexi-relationship investor) with Marshall contributing a modest personal check inside it, likely through MFA Holdings Corp or as an individual alongside the syndicate, and treat a direct Upexi corporate investment as a documented but unlikely fallback. Get this confirmed with Marshall directly, in writing, before 7/17-18, not during the pitch.**

Reasoning, in order of weight:

1. **The dollar evidence, not just the structural precedent, now points the same direction.** The prior dossier's recommendation rested mainly on Marshall's demonstrated preference for syndicate-led, priced, staged deals. This pass adds the harder constraint: neither Marshall personally (recomputed $2.7M-$3.5M liquid stake) nor Upexi corporately ($3.48M cash, $61M market cap, 0.7x mNAV) can comfortably fund $10-50M alone on today's numbers. A syndicate is not just the pattern-matched preference anymore; it looks like the only path with enough actual capital behind it.
2. **A real, active, three-transaction-deep relationship (Hivemind Capital Partners) already exists and is the most concrete lead for who else might be in the room**, more so than the broader original PIPE syndicate, most of whose members have no subsequent disclosed Upexi relationship found in this pass.
3. **The corporate path has gotten measurably worse, not better, since the prior dossier's more general accounting-risk framing.** A specific $109.3M quarterly net loss, a specific 30 percent NAV discount, and a specific $3.48M cash position are much harder for a board to explain away than an abstract "equity method accounting is a risk" caution. Treat the corporate path as something the team should be ready to decline gracefully if Marshall proposes it, not something to build legal infrastructure around as the base case.
4. **The related-party disclosure obligation is not just a future risk to manage, it is arguably a present one Marshall is already carrying** under Upexi's own Code of Conduct, given the active Winn.solutions-Upexi engagement (Finding 6). Surfacing this proactively, on Jarred's side, before Marshall's own compliance process might otherwise surface it, is both the more defensible posture and consistent with `venture-structure.md`'s existing recommendation to disclose the dual role in writing before the pitch.
5. **Close the gap with a direct, low-pressure question, not an assumption walked into the room.** Given the existing "32 pending Allan follow-ups" and unresolved Anchorage pilot-thread status already flagged in the product overview, the cleanest path is to fold this question into whatever written communication is already sweeping those open loops, rather than raising it cold in the 7/17-18 meeting itself. A workable framing, subject to Jarred's own voice and relationship judgment: ask whether Marshall is thinking about this personally, whether he would want to bring in some of the same people from the Solana raise, or whether this is something he would want Upexi's board to formally evaluate as a strategic investment. Frame it as wanting to build the right legal container before the deck lands, which is true, rather than as presuming an answer.
6. **Prepare the concept doc and deck to be structure-agnostic where possible**, and explicit about the tranching idea already recommended in `fundraising-path.md` (release capital against named milestones rather than a lump sum) regardless of which entity ends up as the counterparty, since tranching is valuable under all three structures and does not require the entity question to be resolved first.

## Risks to this recommendation

- **This is still inference from public financial data, not a confirmed fact.** Nothing here, or in the prior dossier, is a substitute for Marshall answering the question directly. Treat every dollar figure and every probability estimate above as context for that conversation, not as a replacement for it.
- **Marshall may have undisclosed personal liquidity outside Upexi and MFA Holdings Corp.** No source in this pass found evidence of other major assets, but absence of evidence is not evidence of absence for a private individual; a serial operator with his history plausibly has other holdings that simply do not show up in SEC filings or public corporate registries.
- **Upexi's numbers move fast and are volatile by design.** Cash, SOL treasury value, share price, and market cap all shift meaningfully week to week for this company; treat the specific figures above as dated to the sources cited (mostly late June and early July 2026, with the underlying 10-Q dated March 31, 2026) and re-verify anything load-bearing closer to 7/17-18.
- **The Hivemind-as-likely-syndicate-anchor read is a reasoned inference from a pattern of three transactions, not a confirmed fact.** There is no public evidence Hivemind or any other Upexi-linked investor has any interest in, or has even been approached about, the Agent OS concept. Treat this as "most plausible existing relationship to draw on if a syndicate is assembled," not as a lead already in motion.
- **A hybrid outcome is likely undercounted above.** The three options are presented as distinct for analytical clarity, but the most probable real-world outcome may blend all three: a small Upexi corporate stake (kept deliberately under 20 percent and without a board seat, as the prior dossier's fallback already suggests) alongside a Marshall personal check and a syndicate he assembles, rather than any one of the three being the whole answer.
- **The related-party disclosure analysis in Finding 6 is this dossier's own legal reasoning from Upexi's public Code of Conduct language, not a conclusion any lawyer has reviewed.** Route it through the planned `/counsel` verification step (Track 4 in the product overview) before treating it as settled, exactly as `venture-structure.md` already flags for its own recommendations.
