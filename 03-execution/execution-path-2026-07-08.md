## 2. Alternatives considered and why not

`sequencing-roadmap.md` is the dossier that frames this choice directly, across a twelve-month horizon from 2026-07-08 to roughly 2027-07.

Kept as a parallel branch, not the primary bet, because the paperwork and structure work (entity formation, IP assignment, vesting, the syndicate conversation) has real value even if it closes later or smaller than hoped, and because Marshall may simply move faster than the twelve-month spine assumes.

**Option B, pilot-first.** Use Anchorage and Upexi as design partners, raise no fresh capital, and let the pilot prove the product. Burn under this option runs $20,000 to $40,000 per month through month six, and it does not stand alone in the recommended path, it is absorbed into Option C as literally the first ninety days of that longer plan. It was not rejected; it was subsumed.

**Option C, consulting-revenue-first.** Treat the existing consulting motion (the reference implementation Jarred already operates, plus Anchorage's roughly $25,000 per month) as the funded engine. Each new client engagement pays for closing one scorecard gap. By month twelve, three to five concurrent engagements should be running, at which point a fundraise, if still wanted, is evidence-backed rather than deck-alone. This is the recommended spine. The strongest external validation cited is the Forward-Deployed Engineer model, originated at Palantir and now copied by Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Databricks, and Cohere, which is structurally the same bet: embed engineers with paying clients, let the product harden against real workflows, let revenue fund the build. `sequencing-roadmap.md` is explicit that this choice does not require moving the 7/11, 7/15, or 7/17-18 dates; it only requires reframing what is being asked for at the 7/17-18 meeting.

A fourth framing worth naming even though no dossier presents it as a fourth numbered option: keeping Winn.solutions and the new venture running fully separate and parallel indefinitely. `venture-structure.md` cites the 37signals and Basecamp precedent and the Tiny Speck to Slack precedent, both cases where an internal tool that became the company wound down the original business within roughly a year rather than running two businesses side by side indefinitely. This is not an action for the next 90 days, but it is a signal to revisit the "keep Winn.solutions separate" assumption around the month six to twelve mark rather than treating permanent separation as the default.

The detailed fundraise-mechanics work in `fundraising-path.md`, `venture-structure.md`, and both gap-closers is not in tension with Option C's slower posture. Prepare the legal structure, the entity, the IP assignment, and the syndicate conversation now, in parallel, so the mechanism is ready whenever it is needed, whether that is 7/17-18, month six, or month twelve.
