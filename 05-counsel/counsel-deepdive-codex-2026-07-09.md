Credible: persistent READ ONLY label, provenance and freshness shown, action controls removed, narrate only the data path actually observed; a timestamped recording beats pretending a failed live path worked.
2. Reference implementation as enterprise product. Risk: the fleet, 296 skills, corpus size, note count, and streak prove sustained founder use, not multi-tenancy, enterprise deployment, organizational adoption, marketplace quality, or customer outcomes. Credible: a portability table (reusable now, requires productization, pilot requirement, future); call the estate N=1 operating evidence.
3. Measurement as financial return. Risk: precise charts convert scenarios, activity counts, alignment scores, or theoretical time savings into implied productivity, EBITDA, headcount savings, valuation. Credible: separate baseline, output quality, rework, cost to serve, adoption, actual redeployment; remove the 3x to 10x band and the unsupported four-versus-eighteen fallback.
4. Governance diagrams as implemented controls. Risk: human-gate graphics, audit trails, tenant diagrams, SOC-aligned language imply tested isolation, access control, retention, incident response, certification. Credible: mark each control as built and tested, internal only, pilot prerequisite, or roadmap; trusted companion is a behavior contract, not a compliance badge.
5. Team, traction, and moat. Risk: professional team slides imply full-time founders, signed contributor grants, assigned IP, product customers, design partners, durable client-data effects; a research corpus and warm investor relationship are not market validation. Credible: Jarred as founder; Nick and Carter as originating contributors with dated deliverables and actual agreement status; Recurro separate; consulting clients as consulting clients; the signed pilot as the first product-market achievement.

## 7. STRONGEST CONVICTION

This launch becomes exceptional if trust is made visible as product behavior, not claimed as brand language. A live system that cites its sources, distinguishes observation from inference, exposes uncertainty, shows the limits of its authority, and refuses to act outside them will create more conviction than a more theatrical product that appears finished. The company is not exceptional because it has more agent features. It is exceptional if it can operationalize restraint, prove that leaders value that restraint, and turn one signed pilot into measured evidence that the transition can be both faster and more human.
