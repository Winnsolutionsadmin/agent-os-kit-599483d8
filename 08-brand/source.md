## 1. Positioning statement and narrative territory

### 1.1 The one-line positioning (the spine everything hangs on)

> The trusted companion that brings companies through the AI transition and gives them the head start. `CORPUS`

This line is already load-bearing across the corpus (the roadmap, decision 0003, and the CLAUDE.md house version all state it). `CORPUS` Treat it as fixed raw material, not something to reinvent.

### 1.2 Positioning statement (long form, editable)

For a C-level leader whose company knows it has to cross into AI but does not trust itself to cross alone, **[NAME]** is the calm operating surface and the consultative companion that carries the organization through the transition and gives it a real head start. Unlike a frontier lab, which sells raw capability, and unlike the automation vendors, who sell headcount you can cut, **[NAME]** earns adoption instead of imposing it: humans hold the final gate on every consequential action, work is traceable to its origin, and the people who adopt the system are rewarded rather than replaced. The proof is not a promise. The founder already runs the reference implementation as a fleet of one, every day. `ASSUMPTION` (assembled from `CORPUS` positioning language)

### 1.3 The category we occupy

We are not the intelligence. We are the **trusted companion** that brings a company across the transition and hands it a head start. `CORPUS` The frontier labs supply the horsepower; we supply the safe passage, the organizational psychology, and the human relationship that turns capability into adoption. The category sentence a reader can repeat verbatim: **"the trusted companion that brings companies through the AI transition."** `CORPUS`

### 1.4 Narrative territory (the story the brand tells)

Five beats, in order. Every downstream artifact can draw from these without contradicting the positioning.

1. **The transition is real and frightening.** Every company knows it must adopt AI. Most do not trust themselves to do it without breaking something or breaking faith with their people. The philosophical enemy is not a competitor; it is **chaotic AI adoption**. `CORPUS`
2. **Capability is not the question; adoption is.** Concede plainly that Anthropic and its peers can build the underlying capability. That concession is the market premise. What they do not sell is the organizational psychology of getting a whole company to actually adopt it, safely, without fear. `CORPUS`
3. **The moat is trust, not the model.** Sales-cycle compression, client-data compounding, and the founder's working estate as a proof asset are the durable advantages. The safest bridge across the transition wins the relationship, and the relationship compounds. `CORPUS`
4. **Reward, not replace.** Every competitor frames agents as work a human used to do. We frame agent output as something that earns the employee recognition, time back, or comp. This is a genuine language gap in the market, not table stakes. `CORPUS`
5. **Calm by default, and the founder is the proof.** Only the one thing that needs a human ever pulses; false alarms are treated as product failures. And the whole thesis is already running: the founder operates the reference implementation at fleet-of-one scale. This operation is the demonstration. `CORPUS`

### 1.5 The domino it moves through (narrative GTM, not an ICP)

C-levels first (the chief-of-staff or organizer seat), engineers next (the revamp and overhaul), then everything else as demand pulls it. `CORPUS` The brand speaks to the C-suite first: calm, trustworthy, structurally sound. It earns the engineer's respect second: precise, honest about what is built versus designed, never a brochure.

### 1.6 Anti-positioning (what the brand must never say it is)

The brand is disqualified the moment it implies any of these:

- that it "runs your company" (it orchestrates; a human holds the last gate). `CORPUS`
- that it replaces employees (it rewards the ones who adopt). `CORPUS`
- that it is immune to frontier labs (it concedes their capability and competes on trust). `CORPUS`
- that it is perfect or hallucination-free (it promises provenance and traceability, not perfection). `CORPUS`
- that integration is effortless (legacy integration is real friction; the companion is the bridge). `CORPUS`

## 2. Voice guide

### 2.1 What we sound like

- **Calm.** The register of someone who is not panicking because they have done this before. Short, settled sentences. No exclamation energy.
- **Structurally sound.** Claims rest on evidence. If a beam is load-bearing, it can hold weight. Nothing decorative pretends to be structural.
- **Operator-direct.** A builder talking to a builder. No hype, no forward-looking promises dressed as facts, no "I built" chest-thumping. `CORPUS`
- **Honest to a fault about math.** Smaller true number over bigger impressive one, every time. A sourced stat or nothing. `CORPUS`
- **Human-first.** The person keeps agency, the last gate, and the credit. Warmth without softness.
- **Quietly confident.** The proof is that it already runs. We can afford to be calm because we are not bluffing.

### 2.2 The register dial: concede, then pivot

When frontier labs come up, the move is always the same: **concede capability, pivot to adoption.** Never existential-fear framing about platform capture; that leaks anxiety and undercuts the calm. `CORPUS` "Yes, they can build the engine. The engine was never the hard part. Getting your whole company to trust it and use it is." `ASSUMPTION` (register sample)

### 2.3 Banned words and tics

**Hype register (cut on sight):** revolutionary, seamless, effortless, magic, magical, unleash, supercharge, turbocharge, game-changing, cutting-edge, next-gen, disrupt, 10x (as a headline), blazing, frictionless, world-class, best-in-class. `ASSUMPTION` (house style, consistent with `CORPUS` no-hype red lines)

**Overclaim register (cut, and treat as a defect):** autonomous (unqualified), fully autonomous, runs your company, zero hallucination, zero errors, perfect accuracy, 100 percent, guaranteed, never fails, replaces your team, set-and-forget, hands-free. `CORPUS`

**Automation-aggression and omniscience registers (cut; they spook a control-anxious buyer):** Blitz, Force, Auto (as a prefix flex), Omni, God-mode, Overlord, Autopilot, dominate, crush, kill. `CORPUS`

**Punctuation:** no em dashes, ever; no double-hyphen dashes. Use commas, colons, parentheses, or a full stop. `CORPUS`

### 2.4 The claims guardrails (voice law, not suggestions)

Seven gates. A tagline, headline, or sentence that trips any of them is a defect, not a style preference.

1. **The 16x throughput number is an internal benchmark on one measured activity**, achieved with Carter's Recurro framework, methodology memo pending (task universe, baseline, comparison design, quality measure, confidence). Never a customer promise, never generalized. Until the memo lands, no throughput or headcount comparison of any kind runs in external material; the only safe framing is the scoped internal-benchmark mention itself. `CORPUS`
2. **Autonomy is orchestration with a human gate.** The system proposes; a person chooses Approve, Edit, or Skip; autonomy is earned by graduation (90 percent agreement over 20-plus reps per decision class). Never "runs your company." `CORPUS`
3. **Traceability over perfection.** Promise provenance and auditability. Never zero-hallucination or perfect accuracy. `CORPUS`
4. **Integration honesty.** Legacy integration is real friction; the consultative companion is the bridge, not a magic wand. `CORPUS`
5. **No frontier-lab immunity.** Concede the capability baseline; compete on adoption psychology and trust. `CORPUS`
6. **Built speaks in present tense; designed does not.** Anything designed-but-unbuilt is named as designed. A roadmap item written in the present tense is a defect. `CORPUS`
7. **Reference implementation, not multi-tenant product.** Every demo and proof is labeled fleet-of-one reference implementation. Never imply live multi-tenant paying usage that does not exist yet. `CORPUS`

### 2.6 Fixed terms of art (do not paraphrase)

### 3.1 How to read the screen, honestly

Each candidate carries a rationale (why it fits the positioning), a connotation risk (how it could misread), and a web screen (collisions and a domain inference). The screen was run today via WebSearch. It is a knockout pre-filter, not a legal clearance. `CORPUS`

### 3.5 The coined / abstract lane (why it exists, and three cautionary checks)

Because the real-word space is saturated (3.6), the names most likely to actually clear are coined, invented, or modified marks. That lane is where the founder should probably expect to land. Three candidates that felt promising were screened today, and even these collided, which is the point:

**Wend** `[web-checked 2026-07-09]`: a lovely verb (to wend one's way, to proceed on a journey) that also satisfies the "works as a verb" test. But wendai.ai already ships "AI worker agents" with a human-in-the-loop gate ("when in doubt, it checks with you"), almost exactly our product. Occupied.

**Tessera** `[web-checked 2026-07-09]`: a tessera is a single mosaic tile (the whole assembled from trusted pieces) and, in Latin, a token or tally (provenance), a near-perfect metaphor. But Tessera Labs raised a $60M a16z-led Series A in 2026 for multi-agent enterprise modernization and already uses that exact etymology. Occupied.

**Cadre** `[web-checked 2026-07-09]`: a cadre is a trusted core group, and "cadre" also means framework; on-thesis. But Cadre Technologies (a Constellation Software subsidiary) holds it in logistics software, so class 9/42 is contested even though the domain register differs. Contested.

**The shape of a clearable name (illustrative only, `[unchecked]`, NOT part of the candidate set):** a coined two-syllable word with no dictionary entry, a real root with a distinctive coined suffix, or a two-word compound that reads as one idea. These are directions for a generation-and-screen pass, not names to adopt. Do not treat them as vetted.

### 3.6 What the screen tells us (read this before judging any single name)

The honest synthesis: **twenty names screened, twenty live collisions, the majority in-category, several holding the exact premium domain, and one (Verity) landing on our exact "trusted AI for the C-suite" positioning.** `[web-checked 2026-07-09]` This is the market the corpus already described. `CORPUS` The implication is not "these candidates are bad." It is that the founder should choose a *path*, and the taste-level pick of a word comes after:

1. **Coined or invented mark.** Highest clearance odds. Requires a generation pass (aim for 20 to 40, then cut hard by feeling and enterprise register) plus an attorney screen. `CORPUS`
2. **Real word plus a distinctive modifier, or an alternate premium domain.** Accept a two-word .com, or a .ai / .co, rather than fighting for a held one-word .com.
3. **A compound or two-word mark that reads as a single idea.**
4. **Commit to a full attorney knockout on a small finalist set, expecting most to be dead,** and file an intent-to-use on a survivor the same week the founder decides. A trust-positioned company cannot afford to rename after launch. `CORPUS`

Whichever path, the sequence holds: a knockout search now, a full attorney availability search before any spend, an intent-to-use filing on the top one or two before public launch, and a linguistic and cultural check for unfortunate meanings in major languages. `CORPUS`
